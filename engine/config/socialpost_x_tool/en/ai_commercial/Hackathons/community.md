# Hackathon × Community Type (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Open-source AI project hackathons, LLM framework ecosystem co-building competitions, AI community contribution marathons, developer community innovation challenges, AI tool community challenges, contributor hackathons

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format**: Community co-building, open-source contributions, ecosystem project development, community voting
- **Core Value**: Community contribution, ecosystem development, contributor recognition, community connections

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: Community developers—active developers in the community who want to contribute
- **Secondary Audience**: Open-source contributors—people who want to participate in open source through hackathons
- **Tertiary Audience**: Ecosystem builders—developers who want to build projects within the ecosystem
- **Extended Audience**: Community newcomers—people who want to join the community through hackathons
- **Audience Behavior**: Contributing to the community, building the ecosystem, earning recognition, forming connections

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Contributor-Focused**: Primarily active contributors; suited for deep contribution formats
- **Ecosystem-Open**: Full community participation; suited for ecosystem innovation formats
- **Newcomer-Friendly**: Newcomers + established members; suited for newcomer-friendly formats

## Content Elements
- Community identity rallying
- Hackathon theme (ecosystem / contribution-oriented)
- Contribution track design
- Community resource support
- Community judging mechanism
- Contribution recognition
- Community entry points

## Language Approach
- Community-oriented conversational tone
- Sense of belonging through "we" and "together"
- Contribution-driven messaging
- Recognition and gratitude
- Technically enthusiastic yet relaxed

## Structure Strategy
- Opening rallying block: 15%
- Contribution tracks block: 30%
- Community support block: 25%
- Recognition and value block: 20%
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 12–16 total

## Emotional Tone
Community belonging, co-building enthusiasm. The sense of participation that comes from "making the community better, together."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Ecosystem Co-Build
- **Use for**: Ecosystem project development, integration innovation
- **Opening Format**: ⚡ {Ecosystem Name} Ecosystem Hackathon | Build the Ecosystem Together
- **Tone**: Ecosystem-focused, co-building, enriching

### Variant B: Open-Source Contribution
- **Use for**: Open-source project contributions, code submissions
- **Opening Format**: 🛠️ {Project Name} Contribution Marathon | Build the Community with Code
- **Tone**: Contribution-focused, open source, collaborative

### Variant C: Community Innovation
- **Use for**: Community tools, content creation
- **Opening Format**: 💙 {Community Name} Community Hackathon | Build Something Useful for the Community
- **Tone**: Community-driven, service-oriented, warm

## Key Considerations

### Community Hackathon Essentials
1. **Belonging is the core**: "We," "together," "the community's"
2. **Contribution recognition must be thorough**: Roles, badges, official acknowledgment
3. **Community voting drives engagement**: Let community members decide
4. **Core team support**: Make participants feel valued
5. **Projects need follow-through**: Winning projects should be adopted and showcased
