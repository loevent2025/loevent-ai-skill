# Hackathon × Investment (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
AI Startup Hackathon, AI Productization Challenge, AI Venture Networking Hackathon, Accelerator Selection Hackathon, AI Business Innovation Marathon, Startup Weekend AI Edition

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Product development, business planning, demo showcase, investor review, networking sessions
- **Core Value**: Startup validation, investor networking, team formation, incubation resources

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: AI founders — Founders with startup ideas looking for rapid validation
- **Secondary Audience**: Technical founders — Developers with technical backgrounds looking to build products
- **Tertiary Audience**: AI product builders — Product people looking for co-founders or validating a direction
- **Extended Audience**: Direction seekers — Developers exploring startup ideas
- **Audience Behavior**: Validating startup ideas, assembling founding teams, gaining investor attention, accessing incubation resources

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Startup Validation**: Founders + Technical founders — ideal for productization hackathons
- **Team Formation**: Founders + Developers + Product people — ideal for startup weekends
- **Investor Networking**: Founders + Investors — ideal for fundraising-oriented hackathons

## Content Elements
- Hackathon theme (startup/business oriented)
- Track setup (product directions)
- Evaluation criteria (business + technical)
- Investor judge lineup
- Incubation / acceleration resources
- Networking mechanisms
- Compliance disclaimer

## Language Treatment
- Founder perspective framing
- Emphasize business viability
- Investor participation is a key draw
- Incubation resources as an attractor
- Must include networking ≠ commitment disclaimer

## Structure Strategy
- Opening information block: 15%
- Theme & tracks block: 25%
- Investment & incubation block: 30%
- Participation value block: 15%
- Compliance & action block: 15%
- Emoji density control: 10–12 total

## Emotional Tone
Startup energy, opportunity-driven. A hands-on sense of "turning an idea into a fundable product."

## Key Considerations

### Investment Hackathon Essentials
1. **Investor Participation Is the Core Draw**: Be explicit about how they're involved
2. **NFA Disclaimer Is Required**: Networking ≠ Funding commitment
3. **Be Specific About Incubation Resources**: What exactly is provided
4. **Business-Oriented Evaluation Criteria**: Not just technical excellence
5. **Post-Event Networking Mechanisms**: How follow-up happens after the event
