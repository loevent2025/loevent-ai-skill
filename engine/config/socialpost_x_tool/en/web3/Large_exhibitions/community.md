# LARGE EXHIBITIONS × COMMUNITY

## Applicable Scenarios
Community pavilions, NFT collector zones, DAO gatherings, fan meetups, project community booths, social events at expos, side parties, networking lounges, ecosystem community days, holder exclusive areas

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale:** 500–10,000+ attendees (community zones typically see 500–2,000 visitors/day)
- **Duration:** 1–5 days
- **Format:** Community booths, meetups, parties, offline gatherings, giveaways, social activities
- **Core Value:** Community belonging, offline connection, perks and rewards, brand identity

## Audience Profiles
- **Primary Audience:** Core Community Members — active participants, early supporters, holders
- **Secondary Audience:** Project Fans — followers with moderate engagement
- **Tertiary Audience:** Potential Members — interested in the community and exploring whether to join
- **Extended Audience:** Cross-community Explorers — visiting from other communities, discovering new spaces
- **Audience Behavior:** Meeting online friends IRL, collecting perks, participating in activities, making connections, finding belonging, taking photos and checking in

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Core Gathering:** Core members + project fans — ideal for internal community events
- **Open House:** Full audience coverage — ideal for community open days and recruitment
- **VIP Only:** Core members (verified holders) — ideal for holder-exclusive events

## Content Elements
- Community identity call-out ("friends of XX community," "holders only")
- Booth / gathering location details
- Community-exclusive activities (meetups, AMAs, parties)
- Perks and airdrop information (only when confirmed and real)
- Social atmosphere description (relaxed, fun, sense of belonging)
- Core team / KOL attendance details
- Check-in and commemorative activities (POAP, merchandise, group photos)
- Side event information

## Language Guidelines
- Prioritize conversational, casual expression ("come hang," "let's meet IRL," "something's brewing")
- Use "you" to close the distance — keep it warm and direct
- Community slang and in-jokes are welcome (ensure target audience will understand)
- Describe attendees by vibe, not title ("a group of interesting people")
- Lower the participation barrier ("just vibing is totally fine")
- Build curiosity and anticipation ("you'll meet some really interesting people")
- Emphasize belonging ("us," "together," "our community")
- Avoid overly formal or corporate language

## Structure Strategy
- Opening call-out block: 15% — community identity + event theme + excitement
- Event info block: 25% — time, venue, activities, guests / team
- Perks and vibe block: 25% — perks, atmosphere, things to look forward to
- Participation guide block: 20% — who should come, how to join, logistics
- Call-to-action block: 15% — registration, community channels
- Emoji density: 14–18 total, lively and energetic

## Emotional Tone
Warm and inclusive, celebratory and connected. The overall energy is community-first and belonging-oriented — capturing the joy of meeting like-minded people and being part of something bigger. Anti-formal, anti-transactional, pro-vibe, pro-fun.

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Community Celebration
- **Best for:** Large community gatherings, anniversaries, milestone celebrations
- **Opening format:** 🎉 {Community Name} fam — we're finally meeting IRL!
- **Tone:** Enthusiastic, infectious, high-energy

### Variant B: Holder Exclusive
- **Best for:** Holder meetups, VIP events, airdrop activations
- **Opening format:** 🔐 {Project Name} Holder Exclusive | {Event Name}
- **Tone:** Exclusive, intimate, member-first

### Variant C: Open Welcome
- **Best for:** Community open days, recruitment events, booth welcoming
- **Opening format:** Want to meet a group of {description} people? Come find us at {location}!
- **Tone:** Friendly, inviting, zero pressure

## Contrast Phrase Bank
- No business cards needed — just bring your curiosity
- Not a stiff conference panel — more like a reunion with old friends
- Meeting online friends IRL is always better than you expect
- You arrive as an ID. You leave as a friend.
- We're not here to "network." We're here to have fun.
- We've been chatting online long enough. Time to meet IRL.

## Notes

### Special Considerations for Community Exhibition Copy
1. **Vibe matters more than information:** Community events sell belonging and atmosphere above all else
2. **Keep the language community-native:** In-jokes, slang, and community shorthand are welcome
3. **Only include real perks:** Airdrops, merch, and rewards should only be listed if confirmed
4. **Be clear about holder verification:** Explain how it works and when it happens

### Authenticity Principles
- Core team attendance should only be listed after confirmation
- Perks and merchandise must be prepared before being announced
- Venue capacity figures must be accurate
