# WEB3 BUSINESS CONFERENCES × COMMUNITY

## Use Cases
Web3 developer community conferences, L1/protocol community meetups, DAO governance conferences, NFT holder gatherings, builder community offline events, Web3 founder community gatherings

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Community sharing + member exchange + project demos + social gathering
- **Core Value:** Community belonging, builder connections, on-chain collaboration, growing together

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Community Members / Token Holders — staying up to date on project progress, building connections, sense of belonging
- **Secondary Audience:** Core Contributors / DAO Members — showcasing achievements, earning recognition, participating in governance
- **Tertiary Audience:** Prospective Members / New Builders — learning about the community, considering joining, finding direction
- **Extended Audience:** Partners / Ecosystem Collaborators — understanding the community ecosystem, exploring collaboration opportunities
- **Audience Behavior:** Gaining information, social connection, sharing experience, participating in governance, building belonging

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Internal-oriented:** Community members + core contributors — best for internal cohesion events
- **Open-oriented:** Fully open — best for community growth events

## Content Elements
- Community identity callout (GM / WAGMI)
- Project / protocol progress updates (Roadmap Update)
- Member Builder Story sharing
- Contributor recognition segment (Contributor Spotlight)
- Open networking time
- Governance proposal discussion / community future planning

## Language Tone
- Web3-native language; emphasize "we," "community," "WAGMI"
- Warmth, recognition, and builder culture
- Warm, genuine, relaxed, decentralized atmosphere
- Emphasize co-building and contribution
- Genuine community tone + Web3 meme culture

## Content Structure
- Opening callout block: 15% — community identity affirmation (GM fam!)
- Content sharing block: 30% — project updates + ecosystem insights
- Member sharing block: 25% — real builder experiences
- Social interaction block: 20% — exchange + recognition + governance
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 14–18 total, warm and energetic

## Emotional Tone
Warm, belonging, building together. The feeling of "WAGMI with fellow Web3 travelers."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Builder Community
- **Best for:** Web3 developer communities, L1 tech communities
- **Opener format:** 👨‍💻 {Community Name} Builder Meetup | Catching up with fellow on-chain builders
- **Tone:** Technical exchange, relaxed, builder culture

### Variant B: DAO / Governance Community
- **Best for:** DAO community conferences, governance discussions
- **Opener format:** 🏛️ {DAO Name} Community Conference | Let's decide what's next — together
- **Tone:** Decentralized spirit, co-governance, transparent

### Variant C: Founder Community
- **Best for:** Web3 founder community gatherings
- **Opener format:** 🚀 Web3 Founder Meetup | Coffee with fellow builders
- **Tone:** Empathetic, supportive, solidarity, building through the bear

## Attendance Tips

### Before the Event
- Think about what you want to talk about and who you want to meet
- Prepare a simple, natural introduction (project / role / interests)
- Update your ENS / Lens / Farcaster profile
- Come to community events with curiosity and an open mind

### On-Site Tips
- Say GM first — everyone here is part of the community
- Find the people you've been talking with on Discord / TG
- Don't just stick with people you already know — meet new builders
- Exchange contact info (TG / Twitter / Farcaster)
- Take photos and post, tagging the community's official accounts

### Post-Event Follow-Up
- Share your experience in the community (Twitter thread / social post)
- Stay in touch with the new builders you met
- Stay active in the community and participate in governance
- If you have an idea, put up a Proposal

## Notes

### Key Principles for Community Web3 Business Conferences
1. **Belonging is the core:** Make members feel "this is our community"
2. **IRL is more valuable than online:** Web3 communities rarely meet in person — make every IRL count
3. **Recognize contributors:** Make builders and contributors feel seen and respected
4. **Keep the atmosphere relaxed:** This isn't a formal conference — it's a fam gathering
5. **Keep it to the right size:** Too large and the community feeling disappears — intimacy matters
6. **Decentralized spirit:** Avoid overly centralized formats; encourage organic, self-initiated sharing
