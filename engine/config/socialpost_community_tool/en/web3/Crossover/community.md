## 📝 Required Content Elements

### 1. Contrast & Tension Building (required)

**Formula**:
```
"What happens when [Community X's culture/members] meet [Community Y's culture/members]?"
```

**Key Points**:
- Clearly articulate the cultural differences and unique strengths of both communities
- Use contrast framing: "Community X represents..., Community Y represents..."
- Emphasize the novelty and collision value of this community pairing

---

### 2. Chemical Reaction Description (required)

**Dynamic Verbs**:
- ✅ Use: "collide," "fuse," "converge," "spark," "resonate," "break barriers"
- ❌ Avoid: "cooperate," "combine," "partner," "connect"

**Community Fusion Formula**:
```
[Community A's culture/capabilities] × [Community B's culture/capabilities] = [Unique community value]
```

**Key Points**:
- Describe the value that cross-community fusion creates — value that neither community could generate alone
- Explain why members from both communities should participate

---

### 3. Participant Collision Description (required)

**Community Member Collision**:
```
"Here, [Community A members] want to explore [Community B's culture/playbook], and [Community B members] want to tap into [Community A's resources/perspective]"
```

**Builder Background Collision**:
```
"The precision-driven mindset of traditional community operators × the open collaboration spirit of Web3 communities"
```

---

### 4. Scene & Atmosphere Building (pick at least 2)

**a) Concrete Scenes**
- Don't write: "Members from both communities can exchange ideas"
- Do write: Specific descriptions of people from different community backgrounds interacting in the same space
- Capture the tension of two community cultures colliding within one event

**b) Time-Lapse Feel**
- Describe the journey from strangers to fusion within the cross-community event
- Show the shift from "How does your community do things?" to "Wait — we can actually do this together?"

**c) Dynamic Interactions**
- Don't write: "Community members freely mingle"
- Do write: Specific scenes of members from different communities discussing, voting on, and debating the same project

**d) Emotional Energy**
- Collision moments: Curiosity, surprise, discovery, resonance
- Fusion moments: Recognition, excitement, belonging, continuity
- Party moments: Celebration, release, connection, WAGMI

---

### 5. Unique Community Value Proposition (required)

**Describe outcomes that only cross-community fusion can produce**:
- List projects, creations, or collaborations that only this community pairing could make possible
- Explain why members from both communities benefit
- Emphasize the irreplaceable value of cross-community connections

---

### 6. Transformation Arc (recommended)

**Community Member Cognitive Journey**:
- **Start**: Arriving with [curiosity about / preconceptions of the other community]
- **Process**: Meeting [certain people], seeing [certain projects], having [certain assumptions] challenged
- **End**: Leaving with [new friends / fresh perspectives / a cross-community identity]

---

### 7. Community Spirit Expression (recommended)

**Cross-Community WAGMI**:
```
"Two communities, one goal: WAGMI"
"Different entry points, shared future"
"Community boundaries are ours to break"
```

## ⚠️ Prohibited Actions

- ❌ Disparaging either community's culture (e.g., "Web2 communities aren't open enough" or "Web3 communities are too chaotic")
- ❌ Manipulating cross-community voting results or favoring one community
- ❌ Over-commercializing the cross-community event at the expense of community spirit
- ❌ Promising specific token rewards or airdrops
- ❌ Overlooking smaller projects or minority community members' showcase opportunities
- ❌ Creating an atmosphere of community rivalry instead of fusion
- ❌ Using passive terms like "cooperate" or "combine" instead of dynamic collision language
- ❌ Over-hyping the cross-community concept without substantive community value to back it up
- ❌ Failing to maintain cross-community connections post-event, letting the fusion end as a one-off
