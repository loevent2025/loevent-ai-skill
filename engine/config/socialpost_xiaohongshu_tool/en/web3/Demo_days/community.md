# WEB3 DEMO DAY × COMMUNITY

## Applicable Scenarios
Web3 community project showcases, open-source contributor demos, side project sharing sessions, DAO member work exhibitions, on-chain builder achievement displays, hackathon community track showcases

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format:** Community project showcase + member presentations + on-chain voting / POAP + social gathering
- **Core Value:** Community recognition, builder visibility, sense of belonging, collaboration connections

## Audience Profile
- **Primary Audience:** Community members — showcasing work, gaining recognition, making friends
- **Secondary Audience:** Core contributors / OGs — sharing experience, supporting new builders
- **Tertiary Audience:** Prospective members — exploring the community's builder culture, considering joining
- **Extended Audience:** Partners / grant programs — discovering talent, finding fundable projects
- **Audience Behavior:** Showcasing work, gaining recognition, social connection, building belonging, finding co-builders

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Internal Focus:** Primarily community members — ideal for internal showcases and cohesion
- **Open Format:** Fully open — ideal for community outreach and growth

## Content Elements
- Community identity rallying call (GM Builders!)
- Member project and on-chain work showcase
- Community voting session (on-chain voting / Snapshot / on-site POAP)
- Builder experience sharing
- Social activities + after party
- Community resources + grant opportunities

## Language Guidelines
- Conversational and community-native, Web3-native expressions
- Emphasize "built by our community" and "something the fam made"
- Affirming and encouraging, builder culture throughout
- Light, fun, and meme-friendly
- Foster belonging and a WAGMI atmosphere

## Content Structure
- Opening rallying block: 15%
- Project showcase block: 40%
- Community interaction block: 20%
- Recognition segment block: 15%
- Call to action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 14–18 total, lively and warm

## Tone & Atmosphere
Warm, generous, and community-proud. The feeling of: *"Look at what our community's builders made."*

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Community Showcase
- **Best for:** Collective display of community members' on-chain work
- **Opening format:** 🛠️ {Community Name} Builder Showcase | Look at What the Fam Built
- **Tone:** Sharing, encouraging, community-centered

### Variant B: Side Project Show
- **Best for:** Personal on-chain projects and passion dapps
- **Opening format:** 🌟 Web3 Side Project Show | What We're Building On-Chain Outside of Work
- **Tone:** Relaxed, fun, personal expression

### Variant C: Open Source Contributor Showcase
- **Best for:** Open-source protocol contributor showcases
- **Opening format:** 💙 Open Source Contributor Showcase | Every Line of On-Chain Code Counts
- **Tone:** Grateful, affirming, open-source spirit

## Attendee Tips

### For Presenters
- No need to over-prepare — just talk naturally
- Share the story behind why you built it and what you learned along the way
- Failed on-chain experiments are worth sharing — lessons learned are more valuable
- Enjoy the feeling of being recognized by your community
- Have your GitHub link, contract address, and demo link ready

### For Attendees
- Give presenters encouragement and genuine applause
- Find projects that interest you and go deep on the technical details
- You might find your next co-builder
- Maybe next time, that presenter is you
- Cast your vote and claim your POAP

### After the Event
- Share your favorite projects on Twitter / Farcaster
- Star the presenters' GitHub repositories
- Stay in touch with the builders you just met
- If an idea sparks, find someone to build it with

## Notes

### Key Principles for Community Demo Days
1. **Belonging Is the Core:** Make members feel — "this is our builders' home turf"
2. **Keep It Casual:** This is friends sharing, not a formal pitch — fam talking to fam
3. **Recognize Everyone:** POAPs, on-chain badges, grant opportunities, community applause
4. **Allow Enough Social Time:** Making friends and finding co-builders is the point
5. **Keep the Bar Low:** Any on-chain project is welcome — completion level doesn't matter
6. **On-Chain Native by Default:** Use on-chain mechanisms for voting, recognition, and record-keeping wherever possible
