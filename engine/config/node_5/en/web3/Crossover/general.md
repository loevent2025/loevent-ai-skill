### Speaker Matching Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tier parity | Speakers from both sides should be comparable in seniority and influence |
| Topic complementarity | One side presents the challenge; the other offers a solution perspective |
| Open mindset | Select speakers who are willing to engage in dialogue, not those with a closed stance |

## Execution Guidelines

### Priority Sequence
1. **Cross-industry theme finalization** — 2–3 months before event
2. **Bilateral co-hosts** — Immediately after theme confirmation (must be pursued in parallel)
3. **Bilateral keynote speakers** — 1–2 months before event
4. **Communications network setup** — 3–4 weeks before event
5. **Matchmaking mechanism design** — 2 weeks before event

### Special Considerations for Cross-Industry Events

| Key Point | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Bilateral balance | Partner count, speaker quality, and speaking time must be equitable across both sides |
| Terminology bridge | Prepare a bilateral glossary; assign moderators fluent in both domains |
| Expectation management | Cross-industry partnerships take time to mature — avoid promising immediate outcomes |
| Post-event channels | Establish cross-industry communities and matchmaking mechanisms to sustain momentum |
| Content review | Compliance requirements may differ across sides — all content requires bilateral sign-off |

### Common Issues & Contingency Plans

| Issue | Contingency Plan |
|-------|-----------------|
| Imbalanced participation ratio | Dynamically adjust invitations; enforce mixed grouping during sessions |
| Dialogue fails to go deep | Moderator facilitation; prepare structured discussion questions in advance |
| Speaker cancellation from one side | Maintain backup speakers on both sides; preserve tier parity |
| Terminology communication barriers | Assign a "translator" role; distribute background materials in advance |
