# Technical Workshop × General Purpose (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
LLM Application Development Workshop, AI Agent Hands-on Workshop, RAG System Build Workshop, Prompt Engineering Workshop, AI Tool Training, Model Fine-tuning Hands-on Workshop

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Instruction + hands-on practice, live coding, mentor guidance, real-time Q&A
- **Core Value**: Skill acquisition, hands-on practice, problem-solving, immediate feedback

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: AI Developers—engineers looking to learn specific skills
- **Secondary Audience**: Technical Product Managers—product professionals who need to understand technical implementation
- **Tertiary Audience**: AI Learners—beginners who learn through hands-on practice
- **Extended Audience**: Technical Leaders—managers who need technical details to inform decisions
- **Audience Behavior**: Skill acquisition, hands-on practice, problem-solving, experience building

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Developer-Centric**: Primarily engineers; suited for technically intensive workshops
- **Mixed Backgrounds**: Developers + product + managers; suited for concept + hands-on blended sessions
- **Learning-Focused**: Learners + entry-level developers; suited for introductory workshops

## Content Elements
- Workshop topic positioning
- Learning objectives (specific skill targets)
- Prerequisites
- Hands-on content schedule
- Mentor/teaching assistant configuration
- Environment setup instructions
- Deliverables description

## Language Guidelines
- Skill-oriented phrasing
- Emphasize "hands-on" over "lecture-based"
- Learning objectives should be specific
- Maintain technical terms in English
- Prerequisites should be clearly stated

## Structure Strategy
- Opening block: 15%
- Learning objectives block: 25%
- Hands-on content block: 35%
- Preparation block: 15%
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 10–12 total

## Emotional Tone
Skill-building, hands-on practice. A "30% learning, 70% doing" hands-on feel.

## Key Considerations

### Technical Workshop Essentials
1. **Hands-on is the core**: Practice immediately after instruction—never lecture without practice
2. **State prerequisites clearly**: Let participants know if the workshop is right for them
3. **Ensure adequate mentor-to-participant ratio**: Make sure everyone can get help
4. **Prepare environments in advance**: Avoid spending time on setup during the session
5. **Define concrete deliverables**: Clearly communicate what participants will walk away with
