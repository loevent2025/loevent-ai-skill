# AI Large-Scale Conference Knowledge Base — Investment Scene

## Scene Overview
Investment-focused AI conferences center on fundraising, startup pitching, and capital matchmaking within the AI space, bringing together AI founders, venture capital and private equity firms, corporate venture capital, and financial advisors. These events are core venues for discovering high-quality AI projects, understanding investment trends, and building capital relationships. They also serve as important stages for founders to secure funding and raise their company's profile.

---

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale**: Hundreds to thousands of participants
- **Duration**: Typically 1–2 days
- **Format**: Project pitches + investment forums + 1-on-1 meetings + closed-door sessions + awards ceremonies
- **Participants**: AI founders, VC/PE investors, corporate venture capital, financial advisors, incubators, government-backed funds
- **Defining Traits**: High concentration of projects, capital-dense environment, efficient deal-making

## Attendee Tips

### For Investors
- Review the project list and pitch decks in advance
- Define your priority verticals for this event
- Prepare a standard set of due diligence questions
- Pre-schedule 1-on-1 time with projects of interest
- Bring business cards and a concise fund overview

### For Founders
- Prepare a tight, compelling 8-minute pitch
- Anticipate likely investor questions and prepare thoughtful answers
- Have pitch decks ready at varying levels of detail
- Research each attending investor's portfolio and focus areas
- Dress professionally — first impressions matter

### Engagement Tips
- Lead with your core differentiator — don't bury the headline
- Let data tell the story — avoid vague, unsupported claims
- Be direct and transparent — don't sidestep difficult questions
- Proactively follow up and define the next step before parting
- Take thorough notes and organize them promptly
