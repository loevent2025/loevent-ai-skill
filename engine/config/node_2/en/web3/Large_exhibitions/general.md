# Web3 Large-Scale Exhibition & Expo Event Planning Knowledge Base

## Scenario Overview

Large-scale exhibitions and expos are the most prominent event format in the Web3 industry, typically drawing thousands of attendees including project teams, investors, developers, media, and community members. These events span 2–5 days and encompass exhibition floors, main-stage forums, breakout tracks, and Side Events.

### Event Characteristics

- **Scale:** 500–10,000+ attendees
- **Duration:** 2–5 days
- **Format:** Exhibition Floor + Main Forum + Breakout Tracks + Side Events
- **Core Value:** Brand visibility, industry exchange, business networking, ecosystem building

## Exhibition Floor Design

### Zone Layout Logic

| Zone | Content | Placement Recommendation |
|------|---------|--------------------------|
| Main Stage Area | Main forum, major announcements | Venue center or visible from entrance |
| Premier Booth Zone | Flagship project and sponsor booths | Along primary traffic aisles |
| Emerging Projects Zone | Up-and-coming projects, early-stage teams | Dedicated standalone area |
| Interactive Experience Zone | Live demos, NFT minting | High-footfall areas |
| Networking Lounge | Rest areas, conversation spaces, light refreshments | Between zones |
| Media Zone | Press interviews, live streaming | Dedicated enclosed space |

### Booth Configuration Guidelines

- **Premier Booth:** 30–50 m², includes a private meeting area, demo display, and branded backdrop wall
- **Standard Booth:** 9–15 m², core branding and interactive display
- **Startup Booth:** 6–9 m², lightweight display setup

## Speaker and Guest Configuration

### Opening Ceremony Speakers

| Role | Profile | Recommended Count |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| Opening Remarks | Organizer executive or industry association representative | 1–2 |
| Keynote Speaker | Founder of a top exchange, leading blockchain, or prominent VC | 1–2 |

### Main Forum Speakers

| Session Type | Speaker Profile | Recommended Count |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Keynote | Industry leaders, technical pioneers, flagship project founders | 1 per session, 6–10 total |
| Panel Discussion | Diverse backgrounds: L1/L2, DeFi, NFT, Infrastructure | 4–5 per panel |
| Fireside Chat | Prominent KOL or investor paired with a project founder | 2 per session |

### Breakout Track Speakers

Allocate by theme; recommended configuration per breakout track:
- 1 moderator
- 3–5 speakers
- 4–5 panel guests

## Web3 Signature Experiences

### On-Chain Engagement Design

| Experience | Format | Value |
|------------|--------|-------|
| POAP / Commemorative NFT | Claim at check-in or upon completing activities | Proof of attendance, social currency |
| On-Chain Check-in | Wallet-connect-based registration | Native Web3 experience |
| NFT Gallery | Artist works on display | Cultural dimension, shareable content |
| On-Chain Voting | Community vote for best project | Community participation and engagement |

### Side Events Coordination

Large expos typically generate a high volume of associated Side Events:

- **Official Side Events:** Organized directly by the event host
- **Partner Side Events:** Hosted by sponsors or ecosystem partners
- **Community Side Events:** Independently organized by the community

**Coordination Recommendations:**
- Publish an integrated Side Events calendar
- Provide a centralized information board or mini-program hub
- Avoid scheduling conflicts with key main-program sessions

## Marketing and Communications Strategy

### Campaign Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Key Activities |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Build-Up | 4–8 weeks before | Speaker announcements, agenda preview, early-bird registration |
| Countdown | 1–2 weeks before | Full agenda release, Side Events roundup, attendee guides |
| Live Coverage | During the event | Real-time updates, quote graphics, live streaming |
| Post-Event | 1–2 weeks after | Highlight recap, session recordings, event data report |

### Primary Channels

- **Twitter / X:** Core communications platform for real-time updates
- **Telegram / Discord:** Attendee community management
- **YouTube:** Session recordings and highlight clips
- **Media Partnerships:** In-depth coverage by industry publications

## Risk Management

| Risk Type | Description | Mitigation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Speaker Risk | Key speaker cancels last minute | Maintain backup speaker list; pre-record session as fallback |
| Technical Risk | Live stream or AV equipment failure | Backup equipment on standby; dedicated technical crew on-site |
| Crowd Risk | Overcrowding in specific zones | Dynamic crowd management, appointment-based zone access |
| Compliance Risk | Sensitive content in sessions | Pre-event content review, advance speaker alignment |
| Safety Risk | Personal or property security incidents | Security deployment, event insurance coverage |

## Execution Checklist

### Venue Requirements

- Main venue capacity must accommodate peak attendance
- Number of breakout venues aligned with planned track count
- Exhibition floor aisles must meet minimum clearance requirements
- High-bandwidth network to support live streaming and live demos
- Sufficient power supply for all booth installations

### Materials Checklist

- Master visual key artwork and wayfinding signage
- Speaker, media, and VIP credentials / badges
- Exhibition floor maps and printed agenda booklets
- Live streaming and audio recording equipment
- POAP / NFT claiming guides and instructions

### Team Structure

- Main Forum Operations Team
- Exhibition Floor Operations Team
- Guest Relations and VIP Hospitality Team
- Media and Public Relations Team
- Technical Support Team
- Security and Safety Team
