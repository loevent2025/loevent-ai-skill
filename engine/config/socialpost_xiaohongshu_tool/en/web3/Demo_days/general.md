# WEB3 DEMO DAY × GENERAL

## Applicable Scenarios
Web3 accelerator graduation showcases, crypto project pitch days, comprehensive Web3 demo days, ecosystem project exhibitions, vertical track pitch sessions

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format:** Project pitches + live on-chain product demos + judge commentary + networking + after party
- **Core Value:** Project visibility, product showcase, feedback gathering, investor connections, ecosystem networking

## Audience Profile
- **Primary Audience:** Web3 startup teams — showcasing projects, gathering feedback, seeking investment and partnerships
- **Secondary Audience:** Crypto investors — discovering projects, evaluating opportunities, expanding deal flow
- **Tertiary Audience:** Web3 practitioners — tracking startup trends, finding collaboration opportunities, gathering inspiration
- **Extended Audience:** Corporate venture teams, exchange BD, and media — identifying targets and covering industry trends
- **Audience Behavior:** Showcasing products, gathering feedback, making connections, discovering opportunities

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Investment Focus:** Startup teams + investors — ideal for fundraising-oriented demo days
- **Ecosystem Focus:** Startup teams + practitioners + partners — ideal for ecosystem showcase events

## Content Elements
- Demo day theme and positioning
- Presenting project overview (count, tracks, funding stage distribution)
- Judge and guest lineup (investors + industry OGs + technical experts)
- Pitch + live demo format details
- Networking session + after party
- Post-event follow-up mechanism

## Language Guidelines
- Project-driven; lead with the showcase opportunity
- Emphasize the value of "live on-chain demo" over narrative pitches
- Judge and reviewer lineup is a key draw
- Professional but engaging tone
- Investment-related content must include NFA / DYOR disclaimer

## Content Structure
- Opening info block: 15% — name, time, location, scale
- Project overview block: 30% — presenting projects + track distribution
- Judge lineup block: 25% — reviewer backgrounds + commentary format
- Value proposition block: 20% — who it's for + what they'll gain
- Call to action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 10–14 total

## Tone & Atmosphere
Opportunity-driven, product-focused. The anticipation of: *"The next Web3 unicorn might be in this room."*

## Style Variants

### Variant A: General Showcase
- **Best for:** Multi-track comprehensive Web3 demo days
- **Opening format:** 🔥 {Demo Day Name} | {N} Web3 Projects Taking the Stage
- **Tone:** Comprehensive, opportunity-driven

### Variant B: Accelerator Graduation
- **Best for:** Accelerator and incubator graduation showcases
- **Opening format:** 🎓 {Accelerator Name} Demo Day | Cohort {X} Final Presentations
- **Tone:** Achievement showcase, milestone validation

### Variant C: Track Focus
- **Best for:** Vertical track showcases (DeFi, Infra, DePIN, AI × Crypto, etc.)
- **Opening format:** 🤖 {Track} Demo Day | The Latest Projects in the Space, All at Once
- **Tone:** Vertical-specific, professional, in-depth

## Attendee Tips

### For General Attendees
- Review the presenting projects and track distribution in advance — flag what interests you
- Come with questions ready for the Q&A sessions after each demo
- Use the networking and after party to find the teams you want to talk to
- Share your favorite projects and takeaways on Twitter

### For Investors
- Review project materials and on-chain data ahead of time; come with DD questions
- Focus on demo completeness, on-chain traction, and how teams handle unexpected questions
- Follow up promptly with teams you're interested in; schedule an office hour
- Compare projects across the same track to sharpen your investment thesis

### For Founders
- Prioritize your live demo — it's the most critical part of your presentation
- Be ready to answer technical, tokenomics, and business model questions
- Make time to connect with other founders — peer learning matters as much as investor meetings
- Don't focus only on investors — ecosystem partners are equally valuable
- Prepare deck + one-pager + contract address + on-chain data dashboard

## Pitch Guide (Web3-Specific)

### For Presenters
- **Opening:** Hook the audience in 30 seconds — be clear about what on-chain problem you're solving
- **Problem:** Back the problem with on-chain data or a concrete case study
- **Solution:** Explain your on-chain approach simply and clearly
- **Market:** Show track size (TAM/SAM) and growth potential
- **Product:** Use a live demo to show your working on-chain product
- **Token:** Briefly explain your tokenomics and value capture logic
- **Team:** Highlight relevant Web3 experience and execution track record
- **Traction:** Show on-chain metrics (TVL, user count, transaction volume, growth rate)
- **Ask:** State your funding round, amount, valuation, and use of proceeds clearly

### Common Questions to Prepare For
- What's your core differentiation from competitors in the same track?
- How does your tokenomics model ensure long-term sustainability?
- What's your current on-chain traction and growth trend?
- Has your contract been audited? What's your security approach?
- What are your biggest technical risks and challenges?
- What are your 12-month milestones post-funding?

### Presentation Tips
- Stay within your time limit — shorter is better than going over
- Use on-chain data and charts; minimize dense text blocks
- Test your live demo thoroughly in advance, including network and gas conditions
- Have a backup plan if the demo fails (screen recording / screenshots)
- Let your passion for the project show — but back it up with solid data
- Be honest about risks and security concerns; don't deflect

## Notes

### Key Principles for General Demo Days
1. **Demo Is Non-Negotiable:** Web3 projects must have a live on-chain demonstration
2. **Be Clear About Project Quality:** How many projects, which tracks, what stage, what on-chain data
3. **Reviewer Lineup Matters:** Investors + technical experts + industry OGs determine feedback quality
4. **Value Both Sides:** Compelling for both founders and attendees / investors
5. **Disclaimers Are Required:** NFA / DYOR / matchmaking ≠ investment commitment
6. **After Party Matters:** In Web3, many deals get started at the party — don't skip it
