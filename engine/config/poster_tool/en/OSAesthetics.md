# RETRO PIXEL Style Guide

## Use Cases
Holiday event promotions, cultural celebrations, nostalgia-themed parties, digital art exhibitions, indie game launches, Y2K retro events, community offline meetups, retro tech expos

## Color Palette
- **Primary tones:** Warm beige, cream white, soft pinkish-brown
- **Backgrounds:** Off-white, light gray, warm apricot
- **Saturation:** Low saturation, soft retro tones
- **Color rendering:** Limited palette, pixelated gradients, dithering effects

### Recommended Color Combinations
- **Classic Nostalgia:** Off-white (#F5F0E6) + Warm brown (#8B6F5C) + Gray (#7A7A7A) + Dark brown (#4A3728)
- **Sakura Warmth:** Cream white (#FDF8F3) + Blush pink (#D4A5A5) + Grayish brown (#9C8578) + Charcoal black (#2D2D2D)
- **Retro Computer:** Light gray (#E8E4DC) + Window border brown (#B8906F) + Pixel black (#1A1A1A) + Soft pink (#E8C4B8)
- **Warm Neutrals:** Apricot (#F2E6D9) + Rose gray (#C4A698) + Graphite gray (#5C5C5C) + Deep espresso (#3D2B1F)

## Visual Elements
- Retro OS window interfaces (Windows 95/98 style)
- 8-bit/16-bit pixel art graphics
- Retro mouse cursor icons
- Skeuomorphic icons: folders, floppy disks, etc.
- Progress bars and scrollbar elements
- Grid dot-matrix background textures
- Window title bars and close buttons
- Pixelated halftone patterns

## Visual Effects
- Limited color palette (8–16 colors)
- Pixel dithering to simulate gradients
- Hard-edge pixelation
- 1px or 2px black outlines
- Sharp edges with no anti-aliasing
- Grid-aligned pixel precision
- Optional retro CRT scanline effect
- Subtle paper/noise texture overlay
- Window drop shadows
- Isometric or orthographic perspective

## Composition Strategy
- Multiple overlapping windows to create a desktop feel
- Primary information window centered and enlarged for emphasis
- Decorative smaller windows scattered around the periphery
- Bottom taskbar/status bar as an information zone
- Top navigation bar displaying brand name, date, and location
- Strict grid-based alignment
- Rhythmic contrast between whitespace and dense areas
- Icon elements accentuating blank areas

## Typography Settings
- **Font style:** Pixel fonts, dot-matrix fonts, monospace fonts
  - Recommended styles: Press Start 2P, VCR OSD Mono, pixel-style serif, dot-matrix gothic
- **Color:** High-contrast solid colors
  - On light backgrounds: Pure black (#000000), dark brown (#3D2B1F), charcoal gray (#2D2D2D)
  - On dark backgrounds: Pure white (#FFFFFF), off-white (#F5F0E6)
- **Fill method:** Solid color fills only — single color per element
- **Prohibited effects:**
  - No gradient fills
  - No glow effects
  - No drop shadows
  - No 3D text
  - No blur
  - No metallic textures
- **General principle:** Text must maintain pixelated sharp edges. Use heavier font weights to ensure readability, consistent with the retro interface aesthetic.

## Mood & Atmosphere
Warm nostalgia, digital retro, approachable, culturally resonant. The overall atmosphere blends comfortable Y2K memories with contemporary design sensibility — conveying sophistication through lo-fi aesthetics, evoking the charm of the early internet era through pixel art's unpretentious appeal, while maintaining the refinement and restraint of modern design.

## Sample Prompts

### Holiday Event Poster
Render as a flat image, surface graphic design, pattern design. The composition fills edge to edge — no outer borders, no canvas/paper texture, no framing. Retro pixel art style simulating a 90s operating system desktop interface. Background is warm off-white (#F5F0E6) with a subtle grid dot-matrix texture. Centered in the composition is an enlarged primary window in classic window style — title bar in warm brown (#B8906F), window border in dark brown (#8B6F5C), content area in pure white (#FFFFFF). **Main title text in solid black (#000000) pixel font, bold weight, no effects — sharp pixel edges and high legibility.** Multiple smaller decorative windows scattered around the primary window. A retro mouse cursor icon is positioned on the left side of the composition. The bottom is designed as a taskbar with venue information and small icons. The top navigation bar displays the brand name, date/time, and location, **using solid black or dark gray fills — no gradients, no effects.** Skeuomorphic icons — folders, floppy disks — are distributed throughout as decorative accents. All elements strictly follow pixel grid alignment with hard edges and no anti-aliasing. The overall aesthetic balances warm nostalgia with contemporary refinement — approachable, playful, and full of millennial digital culture charm. Ideal for cultural holiday event promotions that need to evoke retro nostalgia while maintaining a modern design sensibility.

### Indie Game Launch Announcement
Render as a flat image, surface graphic design, pattern design. The composition fills edge to edge — no outer borders, no canvas/paper texture, no framing. Pixel art game interface style, paying homage to the golden age of 8-bit/16-bit gaming. Background is a deep gray-blue (#2A2A3A) fading to deep purple (#1A1A2E), using pixel dithering for a smooth transition. The central primary window simulates a game boot screen, with window borders in bright cyan (#00D4AA) pixel outlines, interior in deep dark (#0D0D15). **Game title in solid white (#FFFFFF) or bright cyan pixel font, extra bold weight — no glow, no gradient, no effects — maintaining the sharp text style of classic pixel games.** Pixelated game characters or item patterns rendered in a limited color palette — maximum 16 colors — with bright accent colors including orange (#FF6B35), yellow (#FFD93D), and pink (#FF69B4). Multiple smaller windows display game screenshots, character portraits, or achievement icons. Progress bar elements display "LOADING..." or a release countdown. Retro OS icons — floppy disks, folders, recycle bin — scattered as decorative elements. The bottom status bar displays the release date and platform information, **using light gray (#C0C0C0) solid-color pixel font.** Optional CRT scanline effect for enhanced retro feel. All visual elements are pixel-perfect, hard-edged, and grid-aligned. The overall atmosphere is a heartfelt tribute to the classic gaming era — full of player community identity and nostalgic charm. Ideal for indie game launches or retro gaming-themed event promotions.

### Community Event Invitation
Render as a flat image, surface graphic design, pattern design. The composition fills edge to edge — no outer borders, no canvas/paper texture, no framing. Y2K retro desktop style, blending early-millennium internet aesthetics with contemporary design sensibility. Background is a gradient from soft pink (#FFE4E1) to mint green (#E0F0E8), using pixel dithering for a gentle transition. Multiple overlapping windows form the primary visual — each with a candy-colored title bar: pink (#FFB6C1), mint green (#98D8C8), lavender (#DDA0DD), lemon yellow (#FFFACD). **Event title in dark gray (#333333) or deep purple (#4B0082) pixel font, medium weight, no effects — clean and legible.** Pixelated decorative patterns — stars, hearts, emoji-style icons — scattered throughout in bright candy colors. Instant messaging window-style elements display event info and dialogue bubbles. Folders, desktop shortcuts, progress bars, and other skeuomorphic elements add to the authentic desktop feel. A browser address bar at the top displays the event URL or hashtag. **Date, venue, and contact information in solid black (#000000) small pixel font for maximum legibility.** Bubble-shaped decorative elements and pixelated arrows guide visual flow. The overall palette is bright and lively without being harsh, maintaining the understated restraint of pixel art. All elements are pixel-perfect, hard-edged, and grid-aligned. The overall aesthetic combines sweet nostalgia with a friendly, welcoming feel — light, fun, and full of the innocent charm of early social networking. Ideal for community events, offline meetups, or creative workshop promotions targeting younger audiences.
