# AI Commercial · Developer Meetup · Venue Selection Criteria Knowledge Base

## Scale Quick Reference

| Scale Level              | Capacity    | Typical Scenarios                                                  |
| ------------------------ | ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Small Technical Salon    | 15–40 pax   | Invitation-only deep technical discussion, enterprise AI deployment sharing |
| Standard Meetup          | 40–80 pax   | Technical talks + product demo + community networking              |

# Selection Criteria

---

## Criterion 1: Technical Infrastructure Reliability (Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
Whether the venue has the stable infrastructure needed to support technical talks and live AI demos. The unique risk of AI Commercial Meetups lies in the extreme sensitivity of LLM API calls and real-time agent execution to network latency and stability — technical failure directly undermines product credibility and the organizer's brand.

**Assessment Areas:**

Network
- Upload + download bandwidth ≥ 100 Mbps (200 Mbps recommended for 80+ attendees)
- Wi-Fi supports high-concurrency connections (estimate 80% of attendees online simultaneously)
- Wired network available at the speaker area (wired connection takes priority for demo presentations)
- No port restrictions or firewall blocking (OpenAI / Anthropic / Azure and other APIs must be accessible)
- Permission to bring external routers or standalone hotspots
- Backup network plan available (4G/5G mobile hotspot)

Presentation Equipment
- HDMI / USB-C ports available with common adapters on hand
- Projector or screen resolution ≥ 1080p (code and UI must be clearly readable)
- Stable microphone with no feedback (wireless preferred)
- Audio system supports video playback

Power
- Independent power outlets at the speaker area and demo area
- Power access at each audience row (developers are sensitive to laptop battery life)
- Total load capacity supports 10+ high-performance devices running simultaneously

⚠️ Network bandwidth and API accessibility must be verified through on-site testing in advance — verbal assurances from the venue are not acceptable.

---

## Criterion 2: Spatial Layout & Interaction Adaptability (Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
AI Commercial Meetups operate in two modes: technical presentations (focused, quiet, directed sightlines) and community networking (free-flowing, informal, multi-point conversations). The space must support seamless transitions between both modes — not just one.

**Assessment Areas:**

Presentation Mode
- Unobstructed sightlines from the speaker area; 100% of seats have screen visibility
- Movable or flexibly rearrangeable seating
- Audience can easily stand to ask questions during Q&A
- Clear visual separation between the speaker area and audience area

Networking Mode
- Standing conversation space available (not all fixed seating)
- Natural gathering points: refreshment stations, high-top tables, corridor areas
- Dedicated demo corner (isolated or semi-isolated from the main venue)
- Minimum ceiling height ≥ 3m (low ceilings suppress willingness to mingle)

⚠️ Key benchmark: After the event ends, do attendees have both a reason and the space to "stay and keep talking"?

---

## Criterion 3: AI Commercial Industry Tone Alignment (High-Weight Criterion)

**Definition:**
Whether the venue's character matches the positioning of AI commercialization products. The audience for AI Commercial Meetups — developers, product managers, enterprise technology decision-makers — has dual expectations: the space should feel both technically credible and commercially mature.

**AI Commercial Tone Positioning:**
> Polished product feel, without over-packaging
> Business credibility, without feeling like a sales floor
> Technical atmosphere, without resembling a hacker basement

**✅ Suitable Venue Types:**
- Tech company offices (especially AI / SaaS companies' own spaces)
- Premium co-working spaces (WeWork-tier and above)
- Independently designed spaces within creative industry parks
- Incubator / accelerator formal event areas

**❌ Venue Types to Avoid:**
- Traditional hotel banquet halls (overly corporate, clashes with developer culture)
- Low-cost basements or warehouses (AI commercialization events require a baseline level of brand credibility)
- Over-decorated Instagram-style venues (developers are sensitive to superficiality)
- Pure consumer-facing commercial spaces (mall event areas, private dining rooms)

---

## Criterion 4: AI Product Demo & Live Presentation Support (High-Weight Criterion)

**Definition:**
The core value of AI Commercial Meetups often lies in the live demo experience. AI product demonstrations have unique technical requirements, particularly around LLM API calls, real-time agent execution, and multimodal output display.

**Assessment Areas:**

General Demo Support
- Dedicated demo corner available, supporting 2–5 booths running simultaneously
- Independent power supply per booth (no shared outlets)
- Supports real-time audio/video playback
- Permits on-site screen recording and livestreaming
- Adequate lighting (dim environments cause screen glare, making content unreadable)

AI Product–Specific Requirements
- Network does not block major cloud services (OpenAI, Anthropic, Azure, AWS, Google Cloud)
- Stable network latency (real-time agent response demos are extremely latency-sensitive)
- Supports multi-tab / multi-window simultaneous display (LLM conversation streams, backend logs, and frontend UI side by side)
- Screen casting supports high refresh rates (streaming output from AI-generated content depends on frame rate)

---

## Criterion 5: Capacity & Comfortable Density Control (Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
The venue maintains an "appropriately lively" atmosphere at the target headcount. A Meetup that feels too empty kills energy; one that's too packed hinders networking and device use.

**Space Reference Standards:**

| Mode                     | Space per Person |
| ------------------------ | ---------------- |
| Theater-Style Talks      | 1–1.2 m²        |
| Standing Social Networking | 1.5 m²         |
| Hack Night               | 2 m²            |

**Assessment Areas:**
- Use comfortable capacity as the benchmark, not the venue's listed maximum occupancy
- Independent HVAC and ventilation controls (enclosed spaces with 50+ people overheat easily)
- Temperature control responsiveness in summer / winter
- Restroom count (recommended: 1 per 50 attendees)
- If outdoor or semi-outdoor areas exist, confirm viability of a wet-weather backup plan

---

## Criterion 6: Scheduling Flexibility & Usage Elasticity (Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
AI Developer Meetups are typically held after work hours, technical discussions are unpredictable in length, and talks plus Q&A frequently run over time. Hack Nights may also require overnight access.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Supports the standard time slot: 18:00–22:00
- Overtime use permitted (with reasonable billing terms)
- Unrestricted nighttime entry and exit (security / access control coordination)
- Clearly defined latest teardown time (can the venue support up to 23:00?)
- Permission for 2–3 hours of advance setup
- Informal networking allowed to continue after the event officially ends

Hack Night scenario: Scheduling flexibility becomes the top hard requirement — confirm overnight availability and whether security and HVAC remain operational.

---

## Criterion 7: Transportation & Accessibility (Flexible Criterion)

**Definition:**
Attendance rate for AI Developer Meetups correlates directly with transportation convenience. The core audience is concentrated in tech parks, and willingness to travel across districts after work is low.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Within a 10-minute walk from a metro station (preferred)
- Located in a major tech park or business district (lowers the cross-district attendance barrier)
- Ride-hailing friendly (clear pickup and drop-off points)
- Safe nighttime departure (surrounding environment, lighting)
- Venue entrance is clearly marked and easy to navigate
- Permission to place event wayfinding signage

⚠️ If the venue's tone is an exceptional match (e.g., the target company's own office space), slightly compromised accessibility is acceptable — but shuttle or navigation instructions must be provided.

---

## Criterion 8: Brand Presentation & Community Building (Flexible Criterion)

**Definition:**
Whether the venue supports the organizer's brand visual identity and long-term community development. AI Commercial Meetups place particular emphasis on the organizer's commercial credibility.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Permission to place branded backdrops (key visual display area)
- Photo-friendly visual zones available (for social media content)
- Permission for full-event photography and content publishing
- Supports community retention touchpoints (QR code displays, group sign-up guidance areas)
- Option for recurring or long-term venue use (helps establish a "regular community hub" identity)
- Venue operator has its own media channels (potential for co-promotion, a bonus)

---

## Criterion 9: Operational Compliance & Usage Restrictions (Flexible Criterion)

**Definition:**
Whether the venue has restrictions that could impact normal event operations. Non-standard venues (lofts, rooftops, converted warehouses) require extra due diligence.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Maximum occupancy limit clearly defined
- Permission to host public technical events (some office buildings have restrictions)
- Any commercial event restrictions or additional approval requirements
- Permission to bring in external catering and beverages
- Venue liability insurance clearly assigned

# Default Priority Ranking

## Hard Requirements (Failure to meet = automatic disqualification)
1. Technical Infrastructure Reliability
2. Spatial Layout & Interaction Adaptability
3. Capacity & Comfortable Density
4. Scheduling Flexibility & Usage Elasticity

## High-Weight Criteria (Strongly influence the decision)
5. AI Commercial Industry Tone Alignment
6. AI Product Demo & Live Presentation Support

## Flexible Criteria (Assessed based on specific circumstances)
7. Transportation & Accessibility
8. Brand Presentation & Community Building
9. Operational Compliance & Usage Restrictions

# Hybrid Scenario Guidelines

### Technical Talks + Reception
- Prioritize evaluating the "theater → social" turnaround time (target ≤ 30 minutes)
- Confirm whether lighting supports brightness adjustment
- Verify whether fixed furniture can be quickly removed or folded

### Meetup + Hack Night
- Network bandwidth standard increases to 200 Mbps
- Power: independent outlet per workstation
- Scheduling flexibility becomes the top hard requirement (confirm overnight feasibility)
- Space calculated at 2 m² per person

### Meetup + Product Launch
- Stage area requirements increase (professional-grade stage or branded backdrop needed)
- Brand presentation capability upgraded to a hard requirement
- AV equipment upgraded to professional grade (professional sound system, minimum 2 large screens)
- Media photography space must be factored into the venue plan
