# AI Commercial · Large-Scale Exhibition / Expo Knowledge Base

## Section 1: Scenario Definition and Characteristics

### Scenario Overview
Large-scale exhibitions and expos are the most impactful event format in the AI commercial sector. They bring together global AI vendors, enterprise clients, developers, and investment institutions, and serve as the core arena for technology showcasing, brand visibility, business matchmaking, and industry trend announcements.

### Core Characteristics
| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| Scale | 2,000–50,000+ attendees; multiple exhibition zones and parallel programming tracks |
| Duration | 2–5 days; includes a main forum, technical breakout tracks, exhibition floor, and developer activities |
| Format | Booth showcases + keynote addresses + technical sessions + business matchmaking + developer events |
| Positioning | Industry bellwether, technology launch platform, ecosystem showcase |

### Landmark Events
- NVIDIA GTC (GPU Technology Conference)
- AWS re:Invent
- Google I/O
- Microsoft Build
- World AI Summit
- AI Summit series (New York, London, San Francisco, etc.)

## Section 5: Key Success Factors

### Planning Phase
- **Product launches**: Major product or feature announcements as the primary draw
- **Speaker lineup**: Technology leaders, industry specialists, client case study presenters
- **Partner involvement**: ISVs, systems integrators, and industry clients as active participants
- **Agenda design**: Technical depth coverage with industry-specific vertical tracks

### Execution Phase
- **Exhibition zone planning**: Product showcases, hands-on experience areas, expert consultation zones
- **Technical content**: Tiered technical sessions from introductory to advanced
- **Developer experience**: Hands-on labs, coding challenges, technical certifications
- **Business matchmaking**: Appointment system, one-on-one meetings, industry roundtables

### Distribution Strategy
- **Pre-event**: Product teasers, speaker announcements, early registration incentives
- **During the event**: Live streaming coverage, real-time updates, trending topic management
- **Long-tail**: Session recordings, technical blog posts, case study development

## Section 6: Key Considerations

### Technical Readiness
- Ensure demo environments are stable and backup plans are in place
- Technical support team on standby throughout the event
- Sufficient network bandwidth and equipment

### Corporate Compliance
- Client data protection and privacy policy adherence
- AI ethics and responsible AI statements
- Export controls and technology compliance review

### Developer Experience
- Avoid over-commercialization; maintain the quality of technical content
- Provide ample hands-on opportunities
- Prioritize community feedback and interaction
