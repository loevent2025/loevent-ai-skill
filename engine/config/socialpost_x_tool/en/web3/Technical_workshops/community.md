# Technical Workshops × Community

## Applicable Scenarios
Community technical training, project development tutorials, contributor skill-building sessions, DAO tooling workshops, community co-learning events, holder-facing technical explainers

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Community peer teaching, project team-led instruction, co-learning discussions, relaxed atmosphere
- **Core Value**: Community skill development, contributor capacity building, member connection, sense of belonging

## Audience Segmentation
- **Primary Audience:** Community members — members who want to learn project-related technical skills
- **Secondary Audience:** Prospective contributors — people who want to contribute by developing relevant skills first
- **Extended Audience:** Community enthusiasts — followers who are curious about the project's technical side
- **Audience Behavior:** Learn skills, prepare to contribute, understand project technology, connect with the community

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Contributor development:** Members who want to get involved in contributing
- **General education:** Broad community membership

## Content Elements
- Community identity hook
- Learning theme (project-related)
- Curriculum content
- Community mentors / instructors
- Contribution pathways
- Community interaction segments

## Tone & Language Guidelines
- Community-native, conversational tone
- Emphasize "we learn together"
- Contribution-oriented framing
- Relaxed and welcoming atmosphere
- Acknowledge and encourage learning effort

## Content Structure Strategy
- Opening hook block: 15%
- Learning value block: 25% — why this matters + skills gained
- Curriculum content block: 30%
- Contribution pathway block: 15% — what you can do after
- Call to action block: 15%
- Emoji usage: 12–16 total, warm community feel

## Tone & Mood
Warm community energy, co-learning spirit. The sense of belonging that comes from "learning and growing together."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Contributor Training
- **Best for:** Building skills needed for community contributions
- **Opening format:** 🛠️💙 {Community Name} Contributor Workshop | Learn this, and you're ready to contribute
- **Tone:** Empowering, contribution-oriented

### Variant B: Project Tech Explainer
- **Best for:** Educating the community about the project's technology
- **Opening format:** 📚 {Project Name} Tech Deep Dive | Understand what we're building
- **Tone:** Educational, transparent, connecting

### Variant C: Peer Co-Learning
- **Best for:** Community members learning from each other
- **Opening format:** 🤝 {Community Name} Study Group | Let's learn {topic} together
- **Tone:** Collaborative, peer-to-peer, mutual growth

## Notes & Best Practices

### Community Tech Workshop Checklist
1. **Lead with community identity**: Use "we," "our community," "together"
2. **Make the contribution pathway explicit**: What can members do after they've learned this?
3. **Keep the atmosphere friendly**: This is co-learning, not an exam
4. **Recognize participation promptly**: Roles, badges, and acknowledgments matter
5. **Lower the barrier to entry**: Make it possible for more community members to participate
