# AI Large-Scale Conference Knowledge Base — Technical Scene

## Scene Overview
Technical AI conferences focus on in-depth exploration of AI technology, hands-on practice, and real-world implementation sharing. They bring together AI developers, machine learning engineers, technical architects, and researchers. These events are the primary venue for learning cutting-edge techniques, building practical capabilities, and connecting with technical peers — with a strong emphasis on technical depth and applicability.

---

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale**: Hundreds to thousands of participants
- **Duration**: Typically 1–3 days
- **Format**: Technical talks + hands-on workshops + hackathons + open source sessions + demo booths
- **Participants**: Developers, ML engineers, architects, engineering managers, researchers, and students
- **Defining Traits**: High technical depth, strong emphasis on hands-on work, code-first culture

## Attendee Tips

### Before the Event
- Confirm your development environment is set up and working
- Review the agenda and speaker lineup in advance
- Prepare specific technical questions you want answered
- Pack your laptop, charger, and any necessary adapters
- Research the background of speakers and attendees you want to meet

### On the Day
- Select the breakout sessions most relevant to your work
- Go hands-on during lab sessions — don't just observe
- Record key code snippets and technical ideas immediately
- Ask questions — of speakers, instructors, and fellow attendees
- Exchange contact information and join the technical groups

### Knowledge Retention
- Organize your notes the same day while everything is fresh
- Apply new techniques to a project right away
- Write up and share your learnings — it deepens your own understanding
- Follow speakers and contributors for their ongoing output
- Join relevant technical communities and stay engaged
