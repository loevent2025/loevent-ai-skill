# Technical Workshops × Investment

## Applicable Scenarios
Investor technical training, technical due diligence methodology, on-chain analytics workshops, investment research tooling tutorials, technical evaluation framework training

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Concept instruction + tool hands-on practice, case studies, framework teaching
- **Core Value**: Technical judgment, due diligence capability, command of analytical tools, support for investment decision-making

## Audience Segmentation
- **Primary Audience:** Investment professionals — investors looking to sharpen their technical judgment
- **Secondary Audience:** Investment research analysts — researchers who need to develop on-chain analytics skills
- **Extended Audience:** Technical investors — investors with a technical background who want a more systematic methodology
- **Audience Behavior:** Build a technical evaluation framework, learn analytical tools, strengthen due diligence capability, understand technical risk

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Non-technical investors:** Need an accessible entry-level technical understanding
- **Technical investors:** Need a systematic methodology and tooling

## Content Elements
- Workshop positioning (investment perspective)
- Learning objectives (tied to investment decision-making)
- Technical concept explanations (investor-accessible)
- Tool usage instruction
- Case studies
- Compliance disclaimers

## Tone & Language Guidelines
- Lead with the investment perspective
- Simplify technical concepts without sacrificing accuracy
- Emphasize the ability "to evaluate," not "to build"
- Tooling hands-on as the primary focus
- NFA disclaimer required

## Content Structure Strategy
- Opening info block: 15%
- Learning value block: 25%
- Curriculum content block: 30%
- Tool and case study block: 20%
- Compliance and call to action block: 10%
- Emoji usage: 8–10 total

## Tone & Mood
Practical and investment-minded. The no-nonsense sensibility of "understand the technology, make a better call."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Technical Understanding
- **Best for:** Helping investors understand technical concepts
- **Opening format:** 📊 {Topic} | Technical Literacy for Investors
- **Tone:** Simplified, practical, decision-oriented

### Variant B: Tool Hands-On
- **Best for:** On-chain analytics and data tooling practice
- **Opening format:** 🔧 {Tool / Method} Hands-On | Essential Skills for Investment Research
- **Tone:** Tool-focused, actionable, efficient

### Variant C: Due Diligence Methodology
- **Best for:** Technical due diligence frameworks and methodology
- **Opening format:** 🔍 Technical Due Diligence Workshop | Evaluating the Technical Dimensions of a Project
- **Tone:** Systematic, framework-driven, risk-aware

## Notes & Best Practices

### Investment-Track Technical Workshop Checklist
1. **Lead with the investment angle**: This is about developing judgment, not building
2. **Simplify concepts without sacrificing accuracy**: Oversimplification that misleads is worse than complexity
3. **Tooling must be immediately usable**: Participants should be able to open the tool and use it the next day
4. **NFA disclaimer is mandatory**: All investment-adjacent content requires it
5. **Use real cases for analysis**: Live project examples make the methodology tangible
