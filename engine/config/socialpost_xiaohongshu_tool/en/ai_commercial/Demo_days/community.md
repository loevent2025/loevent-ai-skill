# AI DEMO DAY × COMMUNITY

## Applicable Scenarios
Community project showcases, open-source contributor demos, side project sharing sessions, AI enthusiast work exhibitions, community member achievement displays

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format:** Community project showcase + member presentations + interactive voting + social gathering
- **Core Value:** Community recognition, work visibility, sense of belonging, social connection

## Audience Profile
- **Primary Audience:** Community members — showcasing work, gaining recognition, making friends
- **Secondary Audience:** Core contributors — sharing experience, supporting newcomers
- **Tertiary Audience:** Prospective members — exploring the community, considering joining
- **Extended Audience:** Partners — discovering talent, finding collaboration opportunities
- **Audience Behavior:** Showcasing work, gaining recognition, social connection, building belonging

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Internal Focus:** Primarily community members — ideal for internal showcases
- **Open Format:** Fully open — ideal for community outreach and promotion

## Content Elements
- Community identity and rallying call
- Member project and work showcase
- Community member voting session
- Experience sharing
- Social activities
- Community resources

## Language Guidelines
- Conversational and community-native
- Emphasize "our community" and "built together"
- Affirming and encouraging tone
- Light, warm, and fun
- Foster a strong sense of belonging

## Content Structure
- Opening rallying block: 15%
- Project showcase block: 40%
- Community interaction block: 20%
- Recognition segment block: 15%
- Call to action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 14–18 total, lively and warm

## Tone & Atmosphere
Warm, generous, and community-proud. The feeling of: *"Look at what our community built."*

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Community Showcase
- **Best for:** Collective display of community member work
- **Opening format:** 🛠️ {Community Name} Showcase | Look at What Everyone Built
- **Tone:** Sharing, encouraging, community-centered

### Variant B: Side Project Show
- **Best for:** Personal projects and passion work outside of day jobs
- **Opening format:** 🌟 Side Project Show | What We're Building Outside of Work
- **Tone:** Relaxed, fun, personal expression

### Variant C: Open Source Contributor Showcase
- **Best for:** Highlighting open-source contributions
- **Opening format:** 💙 Open Source Contributor Showcase | Celebrating Every Contribution
- **Tone:** Grateful, affirming, open-source spirit

## Attendee Tips

### For Presenters
- No need to over-prepare — just talk naturally
- Share the story behind the project and what you learned along the way
- Failed projects are worth sharing too
- Enjoy the moment of being recognized

### For Attendees
- Give presenters encouragement and applause
- Find the projects that interest you and go deep
- You might meet your next collaborator or friend
- Maybe next time, that presenter is you

## Notes

### Key Principles for Community Demo Days
1. **Belonging Is the Core:** Make members feel — "this is ours"
2. **Keep It Casual:** This is friends sharing, not a formal pitch
3. **Recognize Everyone:** Badges, certificates, applause
4. **Allow Enough Social Time:** Making connections is the point
5. **Keep the Bar Low:** Any project is welcome to share
