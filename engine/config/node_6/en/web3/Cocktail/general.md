# Web3 — Cocktail & Social Event Marketing Strategy

## Event Definition

Cocktail and social events are experience-first gatherings centered on organic connection, using distinctive venues and carefully crafted atmosphere to bring people together naturally. The marketing focus is on conveying ambiance and experiential appeal — not functional value. The goal is to make people *want* to attend, not feel like they *should* attend.

## Core Objectives

- Create an atmosphere compelling enough to make people want to show up
- Build brand identity and a sense of community belonging
- Encourage organic UGC sharing from attendees
- Nurture relationships within the core community
- Facilitate relaxed, low-pressure social connections

## Distribution Strategy

### Hashtag Strategy
- Atmosphere tags: #RooftopVibes #SunsetMixer #Web3Social
- Location tags: #HongKongWeb3 #[City]Crypto
- Venue tags: #[VenueName] #Rooftop #Yacht

### Content Strategy

| Phase | Content Type | Posting Frequency |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Warm-up phase | Venue previews, ambiance visuals, past event highlights | Once every 2–3 days |
| Registration phase | Registration open, capacity notice | 1–2 posts |
| Event day | Live Stories, real-time atmosphere updates | Real-time |
| Recap phase | Curated photos, next event preview | 1–2 posts |

### Content Tone
- Relaxed, conversational, and youthful
- Purely positive framing — showcase the appeal, never oversell
- Short sentences, fragmented and rhythmic
- Avoid transactional language (e.g., "insights," "networking," "resources")

## KOL Partnership Strategy

### KOL Profile Types

| Type | Profile Description | Collaboration Format |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Social connectors | Highly active event-goers with broad networks | Guest attendance, on-site sharing |
| Lifestyle creators | Experience-focused, strong visual aesthetic | Venue experience, atmosphere content |
| Core community members | Active community figures with strong influence | Bringing their circle, community amplification |
| Photographers / content creators | Strong visual content production capability | On-site photography and video production |

### Collaboration Formats
- Guest attendance: Participate naturally as invited guests
- On-site content creation: Capture photos and video during the event
- Community referrals: Bring their network along
- Post-event sharing: Publish experience content after the event

## Distribution Timeline

### T–1 Week: Atmosphere Warm-Up
- Venue previews (photos / short video)
- Past event atmosphere highlights
- Internal community preview

### T–3 Days: Registration Opens
- Publish registration details
- Low-key, inviting copy
- Targeted community outreach

### Event Day: Live Coverage
- Live atmosphere Stories
- Encourage attendees to take and share photos
- Real-time engagement

### T+1–3 Days: Recap Distribution
- Publish curated photo selection
- Reshare attendee UGC
- Preview next event

## Visual Content Strategy

### Key Shots to Capture
- Sunset and nightscape moments
- People interacting with the venue
- Social moments (toasting, conversations)
- Drinks and atmospheric details

### UGC Facilitation
- Set up designated photo spots
- Provide brand elements as options, not obligations
- Encourage natural sharing — avoid making it feel like a task

## Copywriting Tone Guide

### Recommended Language
- Relaxed, unwinding, good vibes, interesting, laid-back
- "Come if you feel like it"
- "When the vibe is right"
- "Interesting people"

### Language to Avoid
- Insights, value, learning, growth
- Networking, resources, connections
- "Don't miss out," "rare opportunity"
