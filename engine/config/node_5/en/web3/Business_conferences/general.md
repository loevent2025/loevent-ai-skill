# Web3 Industry — B2B Conference/Summit Partnership Reference Guide

> Applicable to: Industry summits, thematic forums, compliance seminars, investment summits, and similar events

---

## Event Profile

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Scale | 100–500 attendees |
| Duration | Half-day to 2 days |
| Format | Keynote addresses + Panel discussions + Networking breaks + Executive sessions |
| Core Value | Industry insights, in-depth exchange, business collaboration, policy analysis |
| Budget Level | Mid-to-high budget with emphasis on content quality and speaker lineup |

## Benefits Design Reference

### Co-host Benefits

**Brand Benefits:**
- Co-branding across all event materials (equal logo placement)
- Brand exposure on all collateral (invitations, stage backdrop, badges, etc.)
- Joint official announcement
- Co-host positioning in all media coverage

**Content Benefits:**
- Agenda design participation rights
- Opening/closing remarks
- Keynote address (prime time slot)
- Panel moderation/participation

**Networking Benefits:**
- VIP dinner / executive session hosting rights
- Targeted speaker invitation rights
- Priority networking session access

**Data Benefits:**
- Full attendee contact list
- Shared event assets and materials
- Joint content production rights

### Strategic Partner Benefits

**Brand Benefits:**
- Logo on key visuals (secondary to co-host)
- Official social media acknowledgment series
- Branded page in event handbook

**Content Benefits:**
- Keynote address or panel moderation
- Speaker nomination rights
- Topic suggestion rights

**Networking Benefits:**
- VIP dinner seating
- Targeted networking session access

### Partner Benefits

**Brand Benefits:**
- Logo placement on event collateral
- Social media acknowledgment
- Branding at networking break / registration areas

**Content Benefits:**
- Panel participation
- Case study presentation opportunity

## Execution Priorities

### Timeline
1. **Co-host** — Must be confirmed during the planning phase (3–4 months before the event)
2. **Keynote Speakers** — Lineup confirmed 2–3 months before the event
3. **Strategic Partners** — Agreements finalized 2 months before the event
4. **Media / Community Partners** — Outreach begins 1 month before the event

### Key Considerations
- Content quality is the cornerstone of a successful summit; partners must be able to contribute substantive content
- Speaker lineup should have clear seniority levels to avoid redundancy
- Panel topics should be aligned with speakers' perspectives in advance
- Executive sessions require strict control over attendance and confidentiality
- Compliance-focused summit presentations must be reviewed by legal counsel
- Networking breaks and lunch are critical social opportunities — allow ample time
