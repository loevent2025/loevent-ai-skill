# Web3 · Crossover Collaboration Events · Venue Selection Criteria Knowledge Base

## Scale Quick Reference

| Scale Level         | Capacity     | Typical Scenarios                                                        |
| ------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Boutique Crossover  | 30–60 pax    | Deep co-creation workshop, Industry × Web3 invitation-only roundtable    |
| Standard Crossover  | 60–120 pax   | Use-case exploration workshop, RWA dialogue, multi-group parallel co-creation |
| Large Crossover     | 120–250 pax  | Cross-industry innovation marathon, ecosystem conference with co-creation tracks |

## Selection Criteria

### Criterion 1: Spatial Neutrality & Bilateral Comfort

**Definition:** Whether the venue's type, style, and character can make participants from both the Web3 technology side and the vertical industry side feel comfortable. The cultural gap between both sides in Web3 crossover events is significant — neutrality requirements are higher than for AI crossover events, and the "middle ground" is harder to find.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Venue type neutrality (innovation centers / incubators / design-forward co-working spaces > pure Web3 community spaces or pure industry associations / traditional clubs — the former signals "innovation and collaboration" to both sides)
- Web3 side's baseline tolerance: the space cannot be overly traditional, bureaucratic, or institutional in feel (the Web3 community is highly sensitive to this)
- Industry side's baseline tolerance: the space cannot be overly underground, cluttered, or unprofessional in appearance (senior executives and industry experts need to feel respected)
- Safe choice: a space with "modern design sensibility + professional without being rigid + a touch of innovation atmosphere" is acceptable to both sides
- Spatial "equality": roundtable / island-style layouts > classroom / lecture-hall layouts — avoid spatial cues that imply one side is "teaching" while the other is "listening"
- Diversity of past events hosted at the venue (venues with experience hosting cross-industry / cross-domain events are better equipped to handle bilateral differences)
- International attendee friendliness (Web3 side has a high proportion of international participants — English signage and international service capability are important)

---

### Criterion 2: Breakout Co-Creation Spaces

**Definition:** Whether the venue can provide multiple independent or semi-independent small-group co-creation spaces. The core value of crossover events lies in the breakout sessions — mixing Web3 technologists and industry experts into groups for in-depth discussion and collaborative output.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Number of independently usable breakout spaces (calculate based on groups of 6–10 people)
- Degree of isolation between breakout spaces (at minimum semi-isolated — group discussions must not interfere with each other)
- Configuration per breakout space: whiteboards / writable wall surfaces (essential visualization tools for crossover discussions — Web3 concepts frequently require extensive diagramming for the industry side), desk space, power access, screen casting / display
- Ease of transition between breakout spaces and the main hall
- Furniture flexibility in breakout spaces (support for seated discussion and standing whiteboard co-creation)
- Breakout space floor area (each group of 6–10 people requires at least 15–25 m²)
- If no dedicated breakout rooms are available, whether the main space can be partitioned using dividers or furniture rearrangement

---

### Criterion 3: Main Hall Layout Flexibility

**Definition:** Whether the main hall can support layout transitions across the "dialogue → breakout → presentation" phases of a crossover event.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Whether the main hall supports switching between multiple layout modes (roundtable dialogue, island-style breakout, presentation mode)
- Furniture mobility (crossover events require frequent layout changes)
- "Dialogue mode" layout requirements (roundtable / U-shape / dual semi-circle — emphasizing equal exchange between the Web3 side and the industry side, avoiding any layout that implies one side is "leading")
- "Presentation mode" layout requirements (group output presentations need an area visible to all attendees + screen casting)
- Time and labor cost for layout transitions
- Ceiling height and spatial openness (low ceilings cause noise buildup when multiple groups discuss simultaneously)
- Fireside Chat / AMA layout requirements (Web3 crossover events frequently use sofa-style conversations instead of traditional roundtables — more relaxed and conducive to candid exchange)

---

### Criterion 4: AV & Collaboration Equipment

**Definition:** Whether the venue's hardware can simultaneously support dialogue sessions, breakout collaboration, on-chain demos, and group output presentations.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Main hall projector / large screen (used during dialogue and presentation segments)
- Sound system and microphone configuration (roundtable dialogue requires clear amplification)
- Independent screen casting / display in each breakout space (at least one per group, for collaboration and output presentations)
- Number of whiteboards / writable wall surfaces (at least 1–2 per group — explaining Web3 concepts to the industry side relies heavily on visualization)
- Video conferencing equipment (Web3 crossover events frequently include remote international participants)
- Simultaneous interpretation equipment (Web3 crossover events are highly international; Chinese–English bilingual support is often a hard requirement)
- On-chain demo / product presentation requirements (the Web3 side may need to demonstrate smart contract interactions and DApp usage live — requires stable network + high-definition screen casting)

---

### Criterion 5: Network Reliability

**Definition:** Whether the network environment can support on-chain product demos, online collaboration tools, and venue-wide basic internet access. Network requirements for crossover events are lower than for hackathons or Community Days, but on-chain demo segments and online collaboration tools still demand reliable connectivity.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Total bandwidth (must support venue-wide internet access + on-chain demos + online collaboration tools)
- Whether the demo area / speaker podium has dedicated wired network access (on-chain interaction demos have high network stability requirements)
- Full-venue Wi-Fi coverage (breakout spaces need coverage too — groups may use online collaboration whiteboards or document tools)
- **Critical: Network access restrictions** (Web3 product demos depend on specific network environments — RPC nodes, wallet connections, DApp front-ends must be confirmed accessible)
- Concurrent device capacity
- Permission to test the network in advance (on-chain demo failure is especially damaging in crossover events — the industry side will use it to question technical reliability)

---

### Criterion 6: Spatial Capacity

**Definition:** Whether the main hall and breakout spaces have sufficient floor area to meet the capacity requirements of a crossover event across its different phases.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Main hall capacity under roundtable / island-style layout (crossover event dialogue layouts require more space per person than theater-style)
- Whether total breakout space is sufficient (15–25 m² per group × number of groups)
- Refreshment and networking area footprint (the refreshment break is a critical unstructured networking window in crossover events — participants from the Web3 and industry sides may feel reserved during formal sessions but open up during breaks)
- Whether total floor area accommodates all functional zones
- Ceiling height (noise control when multiple groups discuss simultaneously)

---

### Criterion 7: Transportation & Accessibility

**Definition:** How conveniently attendees can reach the venue. Crossover event attendees from both sides may come from entirely different geographic clusters, and the Web3 side often includes international participants.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Whether the venue location is reasonably convenient for both sides (the Web3 side may be concentrated in tech / innovation districts, while the industry side may be concentrated in CBDs or industry-specific areas — choosing a location near a transport hub or a midpoint is more equitable)
- Accessibility for international participants (distance from international airports / major hotels)
- Public transit convenience
- Parking conditions for private vehicles (the industry side may have a higher proportion of drivers)
- Venue entrance visibility (English signage is important for international attendees)
- Nearby lunch and dining options (must accommodate international attendees' dietary diversity)

---

### Criterion 8: Service Quality & Amenities

**Definition:** Whether the venue's services can support the operational needs of a crossover event. Web3 crossover events are operationally complex, and the industry side's expectations for service quality are typically higher than those at purely Web3 events.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Venue cooperation with multi-space parallel usage
- Venue cooperation with frequent layout changes
- Catering and refreshment capability (must match the industry side's quality expectations — the Web3 community may accept simple refreshments, but events attended by industry-side executives require a higher standard)
- Service capability for international attendees (English communication, diverse dietary requirements)
- HVAC and lighting controllability
- On-site technical support
- Advance setup time allowance (crossover events have high setup complexity — bilateral signage systems, breakout materials, multi-room equipment testing)

---

### Criterion 9: Brand Presentation & Tone Communication

**Definition:** Whether the venue can support the organizer's brand identity while conveying the "crossover, openness, collaboration" tone of the event. Brand presentation at Web3 crossover events requires more nuanced balance — the Web3 side and the industry side have different sensitivities around brand visibility.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Brand installation space (backdrops, wayfinding signage, breakout group signage — crossover event signage systems need to guide participants from different backgrounds)
- "Balance" in brand presentation (if there is an industry-side co-organizer, brand visibility must reflect bilateral equality — avoid filling the entire venue with only Web3-side branding, which makes the industry side feel "used")
- Brand material tone management (the Web3 side's brand aesthetic may lean crypto / meme-driven, while the industry side's may be highly formal — the crossover event's key visual identity must find a design language both sides can accept)
- Photography and recording conditions (crossover event output photos have distribution value in both communities)
- Whether the venue itself signals an "innovation / crossover" identity
- Permission for social media posting (the Web3 side will inevitably post on Twitter/X and Farcaster — confirm that both the venue and the industry side allow this)

## Default Priority Ranking

> **Note:** The following is the standard priority ranking for Web3 crossover collaboration events. The model should dynamically adjust based on the specific event's scale, industry focus, and format.

**Hard Requirements (Must be met — failure to meet = automatic disqualification):**
1. Spatial Neutrality & Bilateral Comfort — The cultural gap between Web3 and traditional industries is significant; venue neutrality is the prerequisite for crossover exchange to work
2. Breakout Co-Creation Spaces — Without sufficient breakout spaces, the crossover co-creation sessions cannot be executed
3. Spatial Capacity — Must simultaneously satisfy floor area requirements for both the main hall and breakout spaces
4. Main Hall Layout Flexibility — A space that cannot switch between dialogue / breakout / presentation modes cannot support a crossover event

**Nice-to-Haves (Desirable but negotiable):**
5. AV & Collaboration Equipment — Independent screen casting and whiteboards per breakout group are strong needs, but can be partially addressed by bringing external equipment
6. Network Reliability — Upgraded to a hard requirement when on-chain demo segments are included; downgraded when there are no demos
7. Transportation & Accessibility — Upgraded to a hard requirement when the geographic distribution gap between both sides is large or the proportion of international attendees is high
8. Service Quality & Amenities — The more senior the industry-side attendees (executives / decision-makers), the more important service quality becomes
9. Brand Presentation & Tone Communication — Affects media reach and bilateral satisfaction but does not impact the core event experience

## Hybrid Scenario Guidelines

When a Web3 crossover event is combined with other formats (e.g., "crossover dialogue + co-creation hackathon" or "crossover event + evening networking reception"), the model should:

1. Take the **union** of criteria from each scenario's knowledge base
2. For overlapping criteria, adopt the **higher standard**
3. Special considerations for common combinations:
   - **Crossover Dialogue + Co-Creation Workshop:** The dialogue segment requires a roundtable equality-focused layout; the workshop segment requires hands-on working conditions. Web3 crossover workshops may involve on-chain operations, elevating network reliability requirements.
   - **Crossover Event + Networking Reception:** Structured crossover exchange during the day → unstructured social networking in the evening. The evening reception at a crossover event carries especially high value — the exchanges constrained by professional identity and domain expertise barriers during formal sessions loosen up over drinks. The venue's tone must support this atmosphere transition.
   - **Cross-Industry Innovation Marathon:** Essentially a crossover version of a hackathon. The Web3 side needs an on-chain development environment (network, power, endurance capacity), while simultaneously maintaining the crossover breakout co-creation spaces and bilateral comfort — this places the highest overall demands on the venue.
