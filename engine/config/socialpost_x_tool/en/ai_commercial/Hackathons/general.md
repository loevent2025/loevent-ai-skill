# Hackathon × General (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
AI application development hackathons, LLM application innovation competitions, AI Agent development marathons, large model application challenges, AI product ideation hackathons, AI tooling development contests

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Time-boxed development, team collaboration, demo presentations, judging and Q&A
- **Core Value**: Rapidly validating ideas, hands-on technical practice, showcasing skills, team formation, accessing resources

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: AI developers—engineers who want to build AI projects hands-on
- **Secondary Audience**: AI product builders—product people with ideas they want to validate quickly
- **Tertiary Audience**: AI founders—entrepreneurs looking to form teams or validate a direction
- **Extended Audience**: AI learners—newcomers wanting to learn through hands-on practice
- **Audience Behavior**: Building hands-on, validating ideas, showcasing skills, forming teams, accessing resources

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Builder Core**: Developers + product builders, suited for technically oriented hackathons
- **Startup-Oriented**: Developers + founders, suited for product-focused hackathons
- **Learn by Doing**: Developers + learners, suited for beginner-friendly hackathons

## Content Elements
- Hackathon theme and direction
- Track design
- Judging criteria
- Technical resource support
- Mentor lineup
- Prize description (generalized)
- Schedule
- Registration requirements

## Language Guidelines
- Builder culture atmosphere
- Keep technical terms in English (Agent, RAG, LLM)
- Emphasize "building" over "listening"
- Track directions should be specific
- Generalize prize descriptions (when details are unavailable)
- Resource support is a key selling point

## Structure Strategy
- Opening information block: 15%
- Theme and tracks block: 30%
- Resource support block: 25%
- Participation value block: 20%
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density control: 10-14 total

## Emotional Tone
Builder energy, bias toward action. The hands-on spirit of "ideas are cheap—ship it to make it count."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Multi-Track Innovation
- **Use For**: Multi-track comprehensive hackathons
- **Opening Format**: 🚀 {Hackathon Name} | Turn your AI idea into something real
- **Tone**: Open, innovative, builder culture

### Variant B: Theme-Focused
- **Use For**: Hackathons around a specific theme or direction
- **Opening Format**: 🔧 {Theme} Hackathon | {Core Challenge}
- **Tone**: Focused, deep, problem-driven

### Variant C: Ecosystem Building
- **Use For**: Platform / protocol ecosystem hackathons
- **Opening Format**: ⚡ {Ecosystem Name} Builder Hackathon | Build the ecosystem together
- **Tone**: Ecosystem, co-creation, resource-rich

## Key Considerations

### Hackathon Best Practices
1. **Theme must be clear**: Participants need to know what direction to build in
2. **Resource support is a key selling point**: API credits, mentors, technical support
3. **Judging criteria must be explicit**: Participants need to know how they'll be evaluated
4. **Generalize prize descriptions**: When specific details are unavailable, use "generous prizes"
5. **Emphasize building**: This is not a slide competition—it's a demo competition
