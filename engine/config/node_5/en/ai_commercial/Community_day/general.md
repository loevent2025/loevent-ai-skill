## Event Format Recommendations

### Standard Agenda Template (6 Hours)

| Time | Segment | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 10:00-10:30 | Welcome & Check-In | Registration, collect beverages, open seating |
| 10:30-12:30 | Morning Co-Working Session | Open development, product experience, casual networking |
| 12:30-13:30 | Lunch Break | Self-arranged or light meal provided |
| 13:30-15:30 | Afternoon Co-Working Session | Continue development, product Q&A |
| 15:30-16:00 | Lightning Talks (Optional) | 3-5 minutes × 3-5 speakers, showcasing the day's work |
| 16:00-17:00 | Open Networking | Free networking, wrap-up |

### Core Experience Design

| Experience Element | Description |
|--------------------|-------------|
| Open Atmosphere | No mandatory agenda; developers can come and go freely |
| Deep Product Experience | Product credits provided; participants encouraged to use products on-site |
| On-Site Engineers | Technical staff from product partners available for Q&A |
| Casual Networking | Organic interactions; no forced icebreakers |
| Showcase Opportunity | Encouraged but not required to share the day's output |

### Participation Options

| Type | Duration | Ideal For |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Full Day | 4-8 hours | Builders with specific development tasks |
| Half Day | 2-4 hours | Developers wanting product experience + networking |
| Quick Visit | 30-60 minutes | Those wanting to explore the community and grab a coffee |

## Venue Selection Criteria

### Mandatory Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |

| Seating | Comfortable work seating, not purely lounge sofas |

## Execution Priorities

### Timeline
1. **Venue Confirmed** — 2-3 weeks before (on-site WiFi and power inspection required)
2. **Co-Host Secured** — 2 weeks before
3. **Recruitment Launch** — 10-14 days before
4. **Beverages Finalized** — 1 week before
5. **Materials Ready** — 3 days before

### Key Considerations
- WiFi reliability is the top priority — always test in advance
- Power outlets must be sufficient; bring backup power strips
- Do not require attendees to stay the entire event; maintain a relaxed atmosphere
- Product credits should be pre-prepared with a clear distribution method
- Obtain attendee consent before taking event photos
- Offer non-coffee options (tea / juice) for attendees who don't drink coffee
- Designate separate "quiet zone" and "networking zone" areas
- Send follow-up materials and community invitations promptly after the event
- Hosting as a recurring series builds a loyal community and increases engagement
- Cap attendance to ensure everyone has a comfortable workspace

### Contingency Plans

| Issue | Contingency |
|-------|-------------|
| WiFi Outage | Pre-arrange mobile hotspot backups; confirm venue has a backup network |
| Attendance Exceeds Capacity | Set registration caps in advance; maintain a waitlist |
| Low Attendance | Increase community outreach; lower barriers; enhance product perks |
| Insufficient Power Outlets | Bring multiple power strips and extension cords |
| Attendees Too Quiet | Set up lightweight icebreaker activities; proactively facilitate conversations |
| High Volume of Product Issues | Ensure technical staff are on-site; prepare an FAQ document |
| Last-Minute Venue Change | Pre-identify a backup venue |

## Recommendations for Running a Recurring Series

### Brand Naming
- "AI Coding Cafe" / "Builder Coffee Day"
- "Code & Coffee" / "Cafe Cursor" style
- City-specific branding: "Shanghai Coding Cafe" / "Coder's Coffee Corner"

### Consistent Cadence
- Host on a fixed weekend each month (e.g., the second Saturday of every month)
- Build attendee expectations; reduce per-event recruitment costs
- Cultivate a regular participant base

### Community Building
- Create a dedicated event community group
- Maintain engagement between events
- Extend the co-working atmosphere online

### Content Output
- Curate featured attendee project showcases from each edition
- Collect product experience insights
- Continuously share community stories
