# Web3 Business Conference — Event Planning Knowledge Base

## Overview

Business conferences are professional events in the Web3 industry centered around business networking, strategic discussions, and partnership negotiations. Compared to large-scale exhibitions, business conferences place greater emphasis on content depth, attendee quality, and business outcome conversion.

### Event Characteristics

- **Scale**: 100–500 attendees
- **Duration**: Half-day to 2 days
- **Format**: Keynote addresses + Panel discussions + Business matchmaking + Executive sessions
- **Core Value**: Strategic insights, business development, industry consensus, partnership formation

## Speaker Configuration

### Opening Remarks Speakers

| Role | Profile | Quantity |
|------|---------|----------|
| Host Representative | Senior executive from the host organization | 1 |
| Industry Representative | Association / Leading institution representative | 0–1 |

### Keynote Speakers

| Keynote Type | Speaker Profile | Requirements |
|--------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Strategic Insights | Prominent investors / Industry leaders | Unique perspectives, Industry influence |
| Industry Deep-Dive | Leaders of top projects in the sector | Hands-on experience, Data-backed insights |
| Case Study | Successful project founders / Operators | Actionable takeaways, Willingness to share lessons learned |

### Panel Discussion Speakers

**Standard Configuration (4–5 Panelists)**

| Role | Profile | Quantity |
|------|---------|----------|
| Moderator | Senior media professional / Industry KOL | 1 |
| Industry Leader | Representative from a leading institution | 1 |
| Practitioners | Project founders / Heads of operations | 2 |
| Diverse Perspective | Emerging projects / Cross-sector representative | 1 |

**Panel Design Best Practices**

- Diverse backgrounds to foster constructive debate
- Moderator must be skilled at managing discussion flow
- Prepare a core question framework in advance
- Reserve time for audience Q&A

## Web3-Specific Sessions

### Executive Session Design

| Type | Format | Participants |
|------|--------|--------------|
| Investor-Only Session | Roundtable discussion | VCs / Investors only |
| Founder-Only Session | Themed discussion | Project founders only |
| Technical Session | Workshop format | CTOs / Technical leads only |

**Executive Session Ground Rules**

- Chatham House Rule applies
- No audio or video recording permitted
- Participants may be required to sign NDAs

### On-Chain Engagement

| Element | Format | Description |

| Attendance Credential | POAP / NFT | Differentiated designs (VIP vs. General) |

## Target Audience Analysis

### Audience Tiers

| Tier | Audience | Share | Core Motivation |
|------|----------|-------|-----------------|
| Primary | Investors, Project founders, BD leaders | 50% | Business development, Investment opportunities, Partnership deals |
| Secondary | Mid-to-senior management, Strategy leads | 30% | Strategic insights, Industry trends, Decision-making input |
| Tertiary | Media, Analysts, Service providers | 20% | Content production, Client acquisition |

### Attendee Persona

- **Decision-Making Authority**: Holds decision-making power or organizational influence
- **Information Needs**: In-depth industry insights, Strategic direction
- **Networking Needs**: High-quality connections, Business partnership opportunities
- **Time Sensitivity**: Values efficiency; prefers concise, well-structured agendas

## Conference Tone & Atmosphere

### Tone Positioning

| Dimension | Positioning |
|-----------|-------------|
| Professionalism | High expertise, In-depth content |
| Formality | Business-formal yet approachable |
| Social Atmosphere | Ample networking opportunities |
| Exclusivity | Select sessions are invitation-only |

### Atmosphere Design

- **Venue Selection**: Premium hotels / Conference centers
- **Seating Arrangement**: Roundtables / Small tables to encourage interaction
- **Catering**: Quality-first approach
- **Pacing**: Avoid information overload; allow breathing room

## Communications Strategy

### Messaging Positioning

- Emphasize **professionalism** and **attendee caliber**
- Highlight **exclusive content** and **executive session value**
- Showcase **business outcomes** (partnerships formed, investment deals closed)

### Content Output

| Content Type | Format | Publishing Timeline |
|--------------|--------|---------------------|
| Speaker Highlights | Posters / Social cards | Real-time |
| Key Takeaways | Long-form graphics / Articles | Same day / Next day |
| Full Presentations | Video (select sessions) | 1 week post-event |
| In-Depth Recap | Long-form article / Report | 2 weeks post-event |

## Risk Contingency Plan

| Risk Type | Description | Contingency |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Speaker Cancellation | Key speaker cancels last minute | Backup speakers on standby; Adjust agenda |
| Sensitive Content | Topics touching on regulation / Controversial issues | Pre-align boundaries with speakers; Moderator manages discussion |
| Low Engagement | Discussion lacks energy | Prepare guided questions; Plant audience questions |
| Confidentiality Breach | Executive session content leaked | Require signed NDAs; Clearly communicate ground rules |
| Poor Matchmaking Results | Business matching yields low-quality connections | Pre-screen participants; Manual intervention |

## Execution Checklist

### Venue Requirements

- Main hall appropriately sized to avoid an empty feel
- Dedicated meeting area for 1-on-1 business sessions
- Separate rooms for executive sessions
- Stable internet and sufficient power outlets
- High-quality acoustics

### Materials Checklist

- Attendee directory / Name badges
- Agenda booklet
- Business matchmaking schedule
- Speaker profile sheets
- POAP claim instructions

### Service Standards

- VIP Hospitality: Dedicated liaison, Priority check-in
- Business Services: Printing, Meeting rooms, Interpretation (if needed)
- Catering: Business-grade quality
- Photography & Videography: Professional production team
