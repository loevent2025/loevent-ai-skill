# DEMO DAYS × COMMUNITY

## Use Cases
Community project showcases, DAO contribution demos, ecosystem builder demonstrations, NFT project launches, fan creation showcases, community tool releases

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Community project displays, member presentations, interactive voting, social activities
- **Core Value:** Community recognition, contribution showcase, sense of belonging, social connection

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Community members — showcasing contributions, earning recognition
- **Secondary Audience:** Core team — seeing what the community has produced
- **Tertiary Audience:** Prospective members — discovering community energy and activity
- **Extended Audience:** Partners — identifying collaboration opportunities
- **Audience Behavior:** Showcasing contributions, earning recognition, building social connections, deepening belonging

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Internal focus:** Primarily community members — best for internal showcases
- **Open day:** Fully open to all — best for community outreach and promotion

## Content Elements
- Community identity callout
- Community project / contribution showcase
- Community member voting segment
- Core team feedback and commentary
- Contribution recognition ceremony
- Social activities

## Language Tone
- Community-native, casual register
- Emphasize "our" contributions
- Recognition and appreciation
- Light and engaging
- Foster a sense of belonging

## Content Structure
- Opening callout block: 15%
- Project showcase block: 40%
- Community interaction block: 20%
- Recognition segment block: 15%
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 14–18 total, warm and energetic

## Emotional Tone
Warm, celebratory, community pride. The energy of "look what we built together."

## Notes

### Key Principles for Community Demo Days
1. **Contributors are the stars:** Not the core team — community members take center stage
2. **Recognition matters:** POAP, Discord roles, and official appreciation go a long way
3. **Community voting:** Let the community decide who stands out
4. **Foster belonging:** "We," "together," "ours" — keep the language inclusive and community-owned
