# AI Commercial · Hackathon · Venue Evaluation Criteria Knowledge Base

## Scale Quick Reference

| Scale Level | Headcount | Duration | Typical Scenarios |
|-------------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| Mini Hackathon | 20–50 | 1 day | Internal corporate innovation sprints, Themed sprints |
| Standard Hackathon | 50–150 | 24–48 hours | Ecosystem-themed hackathons, University competitions |
| Large-Scale Hackathon | 150–300 | 48–72 hours | National AI developer competitions |

---

## Functional Zone Reference

A Hackathon venue must accommodate multiple functional zones simultaneously. When evaluating venues, confirm that each zone's area requirements can be met concurrently.

| Functional Zone | Area Share | Core Requirements |
|-----------------|------------|-------------------|
| Development Workspace | 50–60% | Open workstations, Divisible team areas, ≥2 m² per person |
| Pitch / Showcase Area | 15–20% | Main stage or presentation area, Large screen, Judges' seating |
| Rest Area | 10–15% | Lie-down space, Dimmable lighting |
| Dining Area | 10% | Separate zone, No interference with development area |
| Mentoring Area | 5–10% | Semi-private corners or small meeting rooms |

# Evaluation Criteria

---

## Criterion 1: Network & Technical Infrastructure (Primary Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
An AI Hackathon's network demands far exceed those of a typical meetup: dozens of teams simultaneously calling LLM APIs, downloading datasets, pushing code, and accessing cloud compute. Any network bottleneck directly impacts every team's development progress. Network is the lifeline of an AI Hackathon—there is zero room for compromise.

**Evaluation Areas:**

Network Bandwidth & Concurrency
- Total bandwidth ≥500 Mbps (1 Gbps recommended for 150+ attendees)
- Support for 100+ devices making concurrent high-frequency API calls
- Wired backbone network covering the development area (Wi-Fi alone is insufficient)
- Wired access point density: at least 1 wired port per 10 people
- Full Wi-Fi coverage with no dead zones

Access Permissions (AI-Specific Requirements)
- No port restrictions or firewall blocking
- Unrestricted access to mainstream AI services: OpenAI, Anthropic, Azure OpenAI, Google Vertex AI, AWS Bedrock
- Unrestricted access to development dependencies: GitHub, Hugging Face, PyPI, npm, Docker Hub
- Permission to bring personal routers or independent hotspots (as backup or supplementary access)

Backup Plan
- Multiple 4G/5G hotspots available as emergency fallback
- Clearly defined network failure response process and response time

Power (Hard Requirement on Par with Network)
- Dedicated outlet at every workstation—no reliance on daisy-chained power strips
- At least 4–6 outlets per table (participants typically bring 2–3 devices)
- Total circuit capacity sufficient to support simultaneous high-density device operation
- Independent power circuits for the presentation and pitch areas

## Criterion 2: Endurance & Extended-Use Support (Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
The defining characteristic of a Hackathon is 24–72 hours of continuous intensive development. The venue must operate reliably throughout the entire event cycle, including late-night and early-morning hours. Any venue that "closes at a set time" or "shuts down HVAC at night" is automatically disqualified.

**Evaluation Areas:**

Operating Hours
- Explicit support for continuous 24-hour or 48-hour use
- Unrestricted nighttime entry and exit (access control and security coordination)
- HVAC and ventilation systems running throughout the night (no automatic energy-saving shutdowns)
- Cleaning services available on a scheduled basis during the event

Setup & Teardown
- Early access permitted 8–12 hours before the event starts for setup
- Teardown timeline clearly defined—no immediate eviction after the event ends
- Overtime usage billing rules confirmed in advance

## Criterion 3: Development Workspace Quality (Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
The development workspace is where Hackathon participants spend the most time (typically over 70% of the event duration). Workspace comfort and facility quality directly impact project quality and participant experience.

**Evaluation Areas:**

Space & Furniture
- Usable area per person ≥2 m² (including pathways)
- Desk depth ≥60 cm (sufficient for a laptop plus external devices)
- Chairs suitable for extended use (no folding chairs; back support required)
- Team areas divisible using furniture or signage (4–6 people per team)

Lighting & Environment
- Adjustable lighting (reduced brightness needed during late-night hours to protect eyesight)
- No strong glare sources reflecting off screens
- Controllable noise levels (development area and pitch area require spatial separation)
- Independent HVAC control with zone-based temperature adjustment

Display Support
- Participants permitted to use external monitors (confirm desk space and power availability in advance)
- Workspace projection or public displays available for event announcements and countdown timers

---

## Criterion 4: Pitch & Showcase Area Capability (Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
The pitch session is the climax of a Hackathon and the host organization's primary moment for brand presence. The showcase area must deliver professional-grade AV quality while accommodating all participants, judges, and guest observers.

**Evaluation Areas:**

Stage & Visuals
- Dedicated presentation area or stage (distinct from the development workspace)
- Unobstructed sightlines to the main screen from all seats
- Large screen resolution ≥1080p (demo interfaces, code, and UI must be clearly legible)
- Dual-screen output support (presenter preview + audience display)
- Customizable backdrop or branded display area

Audio & Microphones
- Full-venue audio coverage with no dead spots
- At least 2 wireless microphones (one for the presenter, one for roaming Q&A)
- Stable microphone system with no interference

Judges & Timekeeping
- Designated judges' seating area
- Timer display support (physical timer board or on-screen countdown)
- Dedicated device area for judges' scoring

---

## Criterion 5: Living Amenities & Extended-Duration Support (High-Weight Criterion)

**Definition:**
During a 24–72 hour Hackathon, basic needs such as food, rest, and hygiene directly impact participant wellbeing and event completion rates. Living amenities are not add-ons—they are foundational conditions that influence whether the event succeeds or fails.

**Evaluation Areas:**

Dining
- Dedicated dining area (not shared with the development workspace)
- Venue permits external catering teams to operate on-site
- Refrigerator or warming equipment available for storing beverages and late-night snacks
- Continuous coffee and beverage station (24 hours)

Rest
- Dedicated rest area that is physically separated from the development workspace
- Lie-down accommodations permitted in the rest area (air mattresses and sofas are acceptable—confirm venue policy)
- Independently dimmable lighting

Restrooms
- Recommended ratio: 1 restroom per 50 people
- Available 24 hours with no nighttime closures

---

## Criterion 6: AI Commercial Industry Identity Alignment (High-Weight Criterion)

**Definition:**
AI Hackathon participants (developers, product managers, founders) and judges (investors, enterprise technology leaders) expect a venue that is professional, product-oriented, and has a strong technical atmosphere. The venue identity must simultaneously satisfy "a place where developers are willing to pull an all-nighter" and "a setting where judges feel this is a serious event."

**AI Commercial Hackathon Identity Positioning:**
> Hacker culture vibe—but not chaotic or messy
> Professional feel—but not like a corporate training room
> Branded presence—but not like a commercial product launch

**✅ Suitable Space Types:**
- Tech parks / Innovation centers (strong technical atmosphere, robust infrastructure)
- University engineering buildings or innovation labs (ideal for student competitions)
- Corporate R&D centers (strong branding, professional development environment)
- Premium co-working spaces (WeWork-tier and above)

**❌ Space Types to Avoid:**
- Traditional hotel ballrooms (overly formal business atmosphere, incompatible with overnight hacking culture)
- Pure exhibition centers (cavernous, lacking technical atmosphere, infrastructure dependent on external rentals)
- Excessively upscale commercial venues (developers may feel constrained and uncomfortable)

---

## Criterion 7: Space Capacity & Zoning Flexibility (Hard Requirement)

**Definition:**
Hackathons require multiple functions running simultaneously: development area, pitch area, rest area, dining area, and mentoring area must coexist within the same venue without mutual interference. A single large open space is often insufficient—a multi-zone structure with separable areas is needed.

**Area Reference:**

| Event Phase | Area Per Person |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Development Phase | 2 m² |
| Pitch Phase | 1–1.2 m² |

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Whether total area and individual zone areas can support simultaneous operation
- Whether the development area and pitch area can be physically separated (to minimize mutual disruption)
- Whether the rest area can be fully isolated from the development area (to ensure rest quality)
- Whether the furniture layout can be flexibly reconfigured (development phase → pitch phase changeover time ≤2 hours)
- Zone-based HVAC and ventilation control

---

## Criterion 8: Transportation & Accessibility (Weighable Criterion)

**Definition:**
Hackathon participants are generally willing to travel further for a great event, but poor accessibility increases late arrival rates—particularly impacting the opening ceremony's attendance. Late-night and early-morning transportation safety must also be considered.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Within 15 minutes' walk from the nearest metro station (preferred)
- Ride-hailing pickup and drop-off accessible (with a clear designated point; large equipment must be loadable)
- Safe nighttime and early-morning departure (surrounding environment, lighting)
- Clear venue entrance signage (large venues require pre-planned wayfinding)
- Participants permitted to bring large equipment on-site (some venues have restrictions)

---

## Criterion 9: Brand Presentation Capability (Weighable Criterion)

**Definition:**
For the host organization, a Hackathon is also an opportunity for brand exposure and community building. The pitch session and awards ceremony are the core moments for brand visibility.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Pitch area permits placement of branded backdrops
- Designated areas with strong visual appeal for photography and video production
- Full event photography and livestreaming permitted
- Whether the venue has its own promotional channels (co-promotion is a bonus)
- Whether long-term recurring use is permitted (establishing a branded event venue)

---

## Criterion 10: Operational Compliance & Usage Restrictions (Weighable Criterion)

**Definition:**
Hackathons involve non-standard usage scenarios including overnight operation, external catering on-site, and high-volume device connectivity. Usage restrictions must be confirmed in advance to prevent compliance issues mid-event.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Whether the maximum occupancy is clearly defined and meets requirements
- Whether public competition events are permitted (some office buildings or parks have restrictions)
- Whether external catering teams are allowed to operate on-site
- Whether noise restrictions apply (particularly during late-night hours)
- Whether venue liability insurance responsibilities are clearly defined

# Default Priority Ranking

## Hard Requirements (Disqualify if Not Met)
1. Network & Technical Infrastructure (especially: bandwidth, API access permissions, power density)
2. Endurance & Extended-Use Support (24-hour continuous operation)
3. Development Workspace Quality
4. Pitch & Showcase Area Capability
5. Space Capacity & Zoning Flexibility

## High-Weight Criteria (Strongly Influence Decision)
6. Living Amenities & Extended-Duration Support
7. AI Commercial Industry Identity Alignment

## Weighable Criteria (Evaluate Based on Specific Circumstances)
8. Transportation & Accessibility
9. Brand Presentation Capability
10. Operational Compliance & Usage Restrictions

# Hybrid Scenario Guidelines

### Hackathon + Opening Launch Event
- Pitch area requires upgrade to a professional-grade stage
- Brand Presentation Capability elevates to a hard requirement
- AV equipment upgrade (professional audio system, at least 2 large screens)
- Media coverage zone must be incorporated into the venue plan
- Venue must support rapid changeover from launch event mode to development mode after the opening ceremony (changeover time ≤1 hour)

### Hackathon + Demo Day (Post-Competition Pitch)
- Pitch area and judges' reception area must be planned simultaneously
- External audience admission must be accommodated (capacity and entrance flow require reassessment)
- Brand Presentation Capability elevates to a high-weight criterion
- Network must support livestream broadcasting

### Mini Hackathon (1 Day)
- Time flexibility requirements are reduced (overnight support not required)
- Rest area requirements are reduced
- Dining only needs to cover lunch and refreshments
- Core focus areas: network, power, and workspace comfort
