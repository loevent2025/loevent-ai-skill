# Cross-Community Collaboration × Community Type (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Cross-community joint events, multi-community collaboration, cross-domain open-source collaboration, community alliance co-building, cross-circle Builder meetups, ecosystem cross-pollination and co-creation

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format**: Multi-community joint events, cross-circle exchange, cross-domain co-building, community alliance activities
- **Core Value**: Breaking community boundaries, cross-circle connections, ecosystem convergence, co-building culture

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: Members from each community—active members from different communities/circles
- **Secondary Audience**: Community operators—organizers looking to expand their community's reach
- **Tertiary Audience**: Cross-domain explorers—people who want to be part of multiple communities simultaneously
- **Extended Audience**: Ecosystem partners—collaborators interested in cross-community initiatives
- **Audience Behavior**: Cross-circle exchange, building connections, participating in co-creation, expanding networks

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Community Collaboration**: Community A members + Community B members
- **Cross-Circle Co-Building**: Core contributors from multiple communities
- **Ecosystem Convergence**: Developers/users from different ecosystems

## Cross-Community Content Enhancement Elements (Required)

### 1. Contrast and Tension Building
**Narrative Formula**:
"When [Community A] meets [Community B], what sparks will fly?"

**Required Elements**:
- Clearly identify the distinct characteristics of each community/circle
- Use contrast phrasing: "Community A excels at..., Community B excels at..."
- Emphasize the novelty and possibilities of this community collaboration

### 2. Chemistry Description
**Use Dynamic Verbs**:
- ❌ "Cooperate," "combine," "coordinate"
- ✅ "Collide," "converge," "cross paths," "resonate," "co-create"

**Core Question**: "Why does the union of these two communities create value that neither could generate alone?"

### 3. Participant Collision Description
- [Community A] Builders meet [Community B] Builders
- [Tech community] members cross paths with [product community] members
- [Local community] and [global community] cultural collision

### 4. Scene and Atmosphere Building (Choose at Least 2)
- **Tangible scenes**: The collision of insider jargon from two communities, moments where different cultures merge
- **Sense of time flowing**: The journey from strangers to ice-breaking to deep conversation
- **Dynamic interaction**: Cross-community team formation, joint co-creation, new communities emerging
- **Emotional energy**: The thrill of breaking boundaries, the surprise of finding kindred spirits

### 5. Unique Outcome Imagination
List projects, content, or connections that only cross-community collaboration can produce

### 6. Transformation Arc (Recommended)
Describe the shift from "We're from different worlds" to "Turns out we can build together"

## Key Considerations

### Cross-Community Collaboration Essentials
1. **Preserve community identity**: This isn't about erasing differences—it's about collaboration built on mutual respect
2. **Ice-breaking matters**: Help people from different communities get acquainted quickly
3. **Co-creation outputs are essential**: Don't stop at conversation—produce something together
4. **Follow-up collaboration mechanisms**: One event isn't enough—build ongoing engagement
5. **Community operators must be involved**: Requires buy-in and advocacy at the community level
6. **Avoid becoming a single-community event**: Maintain balanced participation ratios across communities
