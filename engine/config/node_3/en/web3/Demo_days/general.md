# Web3 Projects · Demo Day / Pitch Events · Venue Evaluation Criteria Knowledge Base

| Scale Level | Headcount | Typical Scenarios |

## Evaluation Criteria

---

### Criterion 1: Narrative Impact & Stage Presence

**Definition:** A Web3 Demo Day is fundamentally about narrative construction and trust building. The venue must deliver the stage presence needed to amplify a project's vision.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether immersive LED or visually expressive stage capabilities are available
* Whether multi-screen synchronization is supported (for tokenomics, roadmap presentations)
* Whether a lighting system is available to enhance pacing and dramatic rhythm
* Whether ceiling height is adequate (a low ceiling creates a compressed feel that undermines narrative delivery)
* Whether the space is suited for creating a "mainnet launch ceremony" atmosphere
* Whether live countdowns and launch rituals can be staged

> Web3 is not a product briefing—it's a conviction-building experience.

---

### Criterion 2: Network Stability & Livestream Capability

**Definition:** Web3 events are highly dependent on global reach and online viewership.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether upload bandwidth is ≥50 Mbps (mandatory for livestreaming)
* Whether multi-platform streaming is supported (X, YouTube, Telegram)
* Whether bringing an external production and switching team is permitted
* Whether live on-chain interaction demos can be supported
* Whether deploying temporary node equipment on-site is permitted
* Whether backup network connectivity is available

---

### Criterion 3: Community Interaction Space

**Definition:** A Web3 Demo Day emphasizes community participation, not one-way presentations.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether open interactive spaces are available
* Whether the layout supports an AMA zone
* Whether a POAP/NFT on-site claim area can be set up
* Whether natural gathering points exist (to facilitate KOL interactions)
* Whether a signature wall or community photo area can be accommodated
* Whether the space supports crowd flow management for airdrop activities

---

### Criterion 4: Investor & KOL Experience

**Definition:** Web3 events must cater to the experience of VCs, exchanges, and KOLs simultaneously.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether a dedicated VIP area is available
* Whether private meeting rooms are available
* Whether a media interview zone can be conveniently set up
* Whether quiet spaces for business discussions are available
* Whether a dedicated backstage green room is available

> If VCs and KOLs have a poor experience, downstream distribution and coverage will be directly diminished.

---

### Criterion 5: Spatial Atmosphere & Social Signal

**Definition:** In Web3, the venue choice itself is a statement of community identity and caliber.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether the space conveys a sense of technology and futurism
* Whether it is suitable for evening events (most Web3 events skew toward nighttime)
* Whether it has loft, industrial, or tech-forward spatial character
* Whether it feels "overly corporate" (traditional hotel ballrooms risk appearing too Web2)
* Whether it offers a cityscape view or landmark-level presence
* Whether the venue has previously hosted blockchain or tech events

---

### Criterion 6: Token / Sensitive Content Compliance Risk

**Definition:** Web3 events involve token launches and funding disclosures, carrying elevated compliance risk.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether the venue has restrictions on hosting crypto industry events
* Whether open discussion of token economic models is permitted
* Whether recording and distribution of content is allowed
* Whether the venue is located in a regulatory-sensitive jurisdiction
* Whether advance filing or notification with authorities is required

---

### Criterion 7: Space Capacity & Density Management

**Definition:** Web3 events require high social density, but conditions must remain controlled.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether a standing plus social hybrid format is supported
* Whether the space can accommodate high attendance volatility
* Whether adequate ventilation is available
* Whether nighttime extended use is supported
* Whether a sufficient number of restrooms are available

---

### Criterion 8: Time Flexibility & Nighttime Policies

**Definition:** Web3 events typically take place in the evening, with after parties often extending late into the night.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether events are permitted after 10:00 PM
* Whether DJs and live music are allowed
* Whether overtime extensions are available
* Whether noise restrictions apply
* Whether venue transitions are supported (Demo → After Party)

---

### Criterion 9: Safety & Crowd Management

**Definition:** Web3 event attendees are a diverse and complex mix, requiring attention to safety and order.

**Evaluation Areas:**

* Whether security staffing is supported
* Whether entrance access control is available
* Whether verified check-in is supported
* Whether emergency evacuation routes are available
* Whether access can be restricted to invitation-only attendees

---

## Default Priority Ranking

### Hard Requirements

1. Network & Livestream Capability
2. Narrative Impact & Stage Presence
3. Compliance Risk Management
4. Nighttime Use Flexibility

### Scenario-Based Elevation to Hard Requirement

* If mainnet launch → Narrative Impact & Stage Presence elevates to top priority
* If fundraising-focused → Investor & KOL Experience (VIP and private spaces) elevates to top priority
* If community-focused → Community Interaction Space and social features elevate to top priority

---

## Hybrid Scenario Guidelines

### Web3 Demo Day + After Party

* Prioritize a single venue with convertible modes
* Must support lighting transitions
* Furniture must allow rapid changeover
* Music playback must be legally permitted

### Web3 Demo Day + Summit

* Walkable proximity is preferred over requiring transit between venues
* Assess the risk of KOL drop-off during transitions
* Evaluate the venue's ability to run multiple zones in parallel
