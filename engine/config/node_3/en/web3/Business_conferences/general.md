# Web3 B2B Conference · Venue Selection Criteria Knowledge Base

## Scale Quick Reference

| Scale Level          | Capacity      | Typical Scenarios                                                      |
| -------------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Private Roundtable   | 10–30 pax     | Investor executive session, project BD meeting, DAO governance discussion |
| Boutique Conference  | 30–100 pax    | Ecosystem partner meeting, protocol launch event, Builder roundtable   |
| Mid-Size Summit      | 100–300 pax   | Vertical industry summit, chain/protocol ecosystem conference          |
| Large Summit         | 300–1,000 pax | Annual industry summit, mainnet launch event                           |

## Selection Criteria

### Criterion 1: Business Image & Venue Tone

**Definition:** Whether the venue's external image and spatial character simultaneously meet the standards for business professionalism and the cultural identity of the Web3 industry.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Web3 audiences have low tolerance for "overly traditional corporate" venues — a balance between professionalism and industry culture is essential
- Whether the venue conveys a sense of innovation, technology, or forward-thinking design (e.g., modern cultural spaces, tech parks, design-forward hotels) rather than a purely traditional five-star business hotel
- Whether the location is easy for international attendees to identify and reach (Web3 conferences typically have a high proportion of international participants)
- Whether the venue has previously hosted tech / Web3 / venture capital events (industry familiarity)
- Whether the venue's exterior and common areas are suitable for social media content (the Web3 community places high value on online visibility)
- For invitation-only sessions targeting high-profile investors or institutional audiences, a more traditional premium business venue may be appropriate

---

### Criterion 2: AV & Presentation Infrastructure

**Definition:** Whether the venue's hardware can support keynotes, product demos, on-chain interaction demonstrations, video conferencing, and livestreaming — the core event components.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Projector / LED screen resolution and size (must match the room depth; the last row must have clear visibility)
- Sound system type (built-in venue system vs. external professional system required)
- Microphone configuration (handheld, lavalier, and desktop mic count)
- Video conferencing equipment (cameras, codecs, compatibility with mainstream platforms)
- Livestream readiness (Web3 conferences frequently run simultaneous livestreams or Twitter/X Spaces — a stable streaming environment is essential)
- Multi-camera recording conditions (power outlet placement, camera positioning space, lighting coordination)
- Simultaneous interpretation equipment and interpreter booths (Web3 conferences are highly international; Chinese–English bilingual support is often required)
- Whether the demo area supports multiple devices presenting simultaneously (multiple projects running live on-chain interaction demos at the same time)

---

### Criterion 3: Network Reliability

**Definition:** Whether the network environment can support on-chain interaction demos, wallet connections, real-time data displays, large-scale livestreaming, and other core Web3 conference scenarios.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Total bandwidth and upload bandwidth (must simultaneously support livestream output + on-chain demos + venue-wide internet access)
- Whether the speaker podium / demo area has dedicated wired network access
- Network stability and latency (on-chain interactions, wallet signing, and smart contract calls are extremely sensitive to connection drops — network failure during a demo is a live incident)
- Concurrent device capacity (Web3 attendees carry an above-average number of devices, often using phone + laptop simultaneously)
- Support for dedicated SSIDs / VLANs (isolate the demo network from public Wi-Fi to prevent mutual interference)
- VPN or special network access restrictions (some Web3 tools and on-chain services require specific network environments — check whether the venue network has blocks or restrictions)
- 4G/5G signal coverage (as an emergency backup)
- Permission to conduct on-site network stress testing in advance

---

### Criterion 4: Spatial Layout Flexibility

**Definition:** Whether the venue can flexibly accommodate a main stage, breakout sessions, project showcase area, and networking zones — matching the Web3 conference model of "formal agenda + open networking" running in parallel.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Whether the main hall supports multiple layouts (theater-style, roundtable, fireside chat with dual sofas)
- Number of independent breakout rooms (Web3 summits frequently run multiple parallel tracks)
- Sound isolation between spaces (breakout sessions must not interfere with each other)
- Location and size of the project showcase area (whether independent booths can be configured, each with dedicated power and network)
- Whether the networking area is sufficient and naturally integrated into the traffic flow (in Web3 conferences, "hallway conversations" are often as valuable as — or more valuable than — main stage content)
- Whether the space includes formats suited for informal interaction (lounge areas, terraces, bar counters — not just conference tables and chairs)
- Whether the registration and reception area is positioned at the entrance with a clear traffic flow
- Furniture mobility (whether tables and chairs are fixed; whether rearrangement is permitted)
- Whether the space supports evening Side Event conversion (feasibility of switching from a daytime conference layout to an evening social / party layout)

**Layout Type Quick Reference:**

| Layout Type    | Suitable Scenarios                    | Capacity     | Characteristics                                     |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Roundtable     | Investor executive session, DAO discussion | 10–25 pax   | Facilitates in-depth discussion                     |
| Theater        | Keynote, summit main stage            | 50–500 pax   | High capacity, focused on presentations             |
| Fireside Chat  | Conversations, AMAs                   | 30–200 pax   | Sofa + coffee table setup, relaxed atmosphere       |
| Exhibition     | Project showcase, ecosystem expo      | 100–500 pax  | Multiple booths in parallel, free-flowing traffic   |
| Hybrid         | Summit + showcase + networking        | 100–500 pax  | Zoned: main stage + showcase area + open networking |

### Criterion 5: Spatial Capacity

**Definition:** Whether the floor area and occupancy limits of each functional zone can meet the event's scale requirements.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Actual capacity of the main hall under the target layout (not maximum capacity — calculate based on the actual layout)
- Whether individual breakout room capacity matches breakout session headcount
- Project showcase area footprint (estimated as number of booths × area per booth)
- Whether the networking zone can accommodate a large proportion of attendees simultaneously (networking areas at Web3 conferences see extremely high utilization — plan for 60–70% of total attendance)
- Whether ceiling height meets requirements for stage construction and rigging for lighting and sound
- Fire code occupancy limit (official venue-certified capacity)
- If evening Side Events are planned, whether the available evening capacity matches daytime capacity

---

### Criterion 6: Transportation & Accessibility

**Definition:** How conveniently attendees can reach the venue, with special consideration for the international attendee experience at Web3 conferences.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Walking distance to the nearest metro / bus station
- Travel time from major international transport hubs — international airport, high-speed rail station (Web3 conferences have a high proportion of international attendees)
- Convenience for private cars and ride-hailing
- Nearby hotel availability and price range (must cover budgets from budget travelers to premium business travelers)
- Nearby dining and nightlife options (Web3 community networking often extends well beyond the event itself)
- Entrance visibility (international attendees are unfamiliar with the local environment — signage must be clear)
- The host city/district's friendliness toward Web3 and its regulatory environment

---

### Criterion 7: Service Quality & Amenities

**Definition:** Whether the venue's services can support the operational needs of a Web3 B2B conference.

**Assessment Areas:**
- On-site technical support responsiveness (whether a dedicated AV technician is stationed on-site)
- Catering and refreshment services (in-house catering vs. external delivery permitted vs. designated vendor — must account for international attendees' dietary diversity)
- Venue cooperation on event setup (whether advance setup access is permitted; whether external production teams are allowed on-site)
- Independent HVAC controls (zone-based temperature adjustment, self-service control during sessions)
- Adjustable lighting (zone-based control, brightness switching to support presentation and demo segments)
- Venue usage time flexibility (Web3 events frequently extend into the evening — check whether overtime or overnight use is supported)
- Venue cooperation for evening events (noise restrictions, security coordination, alcohol licensing)
- Venue contingency response capability (protocols for power outages, network failures, equipment malfunctions)

---

### Criterion 8: Brand Customization & Media Conditions

**Definition:** Whether the venue can support the organizer's brand identity and event media needs. Web3 events are highly dependent on social media reach — the venue's "content-readiness" and brand customization potential are critical considerations.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Whether large-scale brand customization is permitted (backdrops, wayfinding signage, custom lighting colors)
- Whether the venue itself has visual appeal for content creation (distinctive architecture, signature spaces with inherent "share-worthy" quality)
- Whether there are designated areas for photography / recording (brand walls, group photo zones, livestream rooms)
- Lighting and acoustic conditions for livestreaming / recording (Web3 events frequently run simultaneous Twitter/X Spaces and YouTube livestreams)
- Whether the venue's own brand identity conflicts with the organizer's (e.g., a space named after a competitor)
- Whether posting interior photos on social media is permitted (some premium venues have restrictions)

---

### Criterion 9: Compliance & Security

**Definition:** Whether the venue meets the information security, compliance, and attendee safety requirements of Web3 events.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Whether conference spaces are fully enclosed and private (executive sessions may involve sensitive topics such as project fundraising and token economic models)
- Sound isolation (whether discussion content could leak to external areas)
- Network security (whether encrypted networks and public network isolation are supported — prevent venue Wi-Fi from being exploited for phishing attacks)
- The regulatory stance toward Web3 / cryptocurrency events in the venue's jurisdiction (whether legal restrictions or additional approvals are required)
- Security staffing for large events (Web3 events involve high-net-worth individuals and crypto assets — security requirements exceed those of typical B2B conferences)
- Whether a dedicated VIP corridor or private entrance is available
- The venue's rules on recording and livestreaming
- Whether executive session areas can be independently secured with restricted access

## Default Priority Ranking

> **Note:** The following is the standard priority ranking for Web3 B2B conference scenarios. The model should dynamically adjust based on the specific event's audience profile, project positioning, and event format.

**Hard Requirements (Must be met — failure to meet = automatic disqualification):**
1. Spatial Capacity — If it can't hold the crowd, nothing else matters
2. Network Reliability — Network failure during an on-chain demo is a live incident; network is top priority for Web3 events
3. AV & Presentation Infrastructure — The hardware foundation for keynotes, demos, and livestreaming
4. Transportation & Accessibility — Convenience for international attendees directly impacts attendance rate

**Nice-to-Haves (Desirable but negotiable):**
5. Business Image & Venue Tone — The Web3 community is tone-sensitive; overly traditional venues reduce community buy-in — though investor-only executive sessions may lean toward traditional premium venues
6. Spatial Layout Flexibility — Web3 conferences have a strong networking emphasis; layout flexibility matters more than in typical B2B conferences
7. Service Quality & Amenities — Affects experience but can be supplemented by bringing an external production team; upgraded to a hard requirement when evening Side Events are involved
8. Brand Customization & Media Conditions — Web3 events rely on social media distribution; "content-readiness" is a practical requirement, not a luxury
9. Compliance & Security — Lower requirements for open summits; upgraded to a hard requirement for events involving fundraising discussions or high-net-worth attendees

## Hybrid Scenario Guidelines

When a Web3 B2B conference is combined with other event formats (e.g., "daytime summit + evening Side Event party" or "summit with a hackathon track"), the model should:

1. Take the **union** of criteria from each scenario's knowledge base
2. For overlapping criteria, adopt the **higher standard**
3. For conflicting criteria, flag the **priority and trade-off recommendation** (e.g., if the summit requires a professional business feel while the Side Event requires a party atmosphere, resolve through spatial zoning or time-based segmentation rather than selecting a compromised middle-ground venue)
