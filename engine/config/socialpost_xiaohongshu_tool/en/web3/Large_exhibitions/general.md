# Web3 Large-Scale Conference Knowledge Base — General Scene

## Scene Overview
General Web3 large-scale conferences are comprehensive, cross-industry events that bring together public chain and protocol projects, crypto investment institutions, developers, enterprise users, and enthusiasts. These events offer the broadest coverage in the industry and are the premier destination for gauging the direction of the Web3 market, discovering business opportunities, and building global networks. Major product launches, strategic partnerships, and fundraising announcements are typically timed to coincide with these events — making them true bellwethers for the entire industry.

---

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale**: Thousands to tens of thousands of participants; top-tier events (Token2049, Consensus, etc.) can reach tens of thousands
- **Duration**: Typically 2–3 days; top-tier events with a surrounding Blockchain Week can span a full week
- **Format**: Exhibition booths + keynote presentations + breakout forums + Side Events + After Parties
- **Participants**: Project teams, investors, developers, media, enthusiasts, KOLs, and enterprise representatives
- **Defining Traits**: High information density, exceptional networking opportunities, global reach, significant industry influence

## Attendee Tips

### Before the Event
- Research the exhibitor and project list, and flag your priority booths and Side Events
- Prepare a digital contact card (Telegram / Twitter/X / ENS)
- Download the official event app and plan your schedule
- Pre-schedule 1-on-1 time with booths and Side Events you're most interested in
- Track Side Event and After Party details early and register in advance (many sell out)
- Prepare a sharp 30-second self-introduction and project pitch

### On the Ground
- Wear comfortable shoes — the venue is large
- Bring a power bank — social media and photography drain batteries fast
- Prioritize high-demand booths and Side Events and avoid peak crowds
- Be proactive — Web3 is a friendly community, don't be shy
- Record the key takeaways from each conversation for easy Telegram follow-up
- Manage your time intentionally: main floor vs. Side Events vs. After Party

### Networking Tips
- Know your intro: who you are, what you're building, what you're looking for
- Exchange Telegram / Twitter/X handles on the spot and follow each other immediately
- Organize your contacts that evening and follow up within 24 hours while it's fresh
- Side Events and After Parties have a more relaxed atmosphere — ideal for deeper conversations
- Use queue time, coffee breaks, and elevator moments to connect with people around you
- Post a daily Twitter/X thread sharing your highlights and tag the people you met

### Side Event Strategy
- Side Events often deliver more value and alpha than the main conference floor
- Collect a comprehensive Side Event schedule well in advance
- Prioritize registration for high-quality, limited-capacity or closed-door sessions
- Don't overcommit — pick 2–3 high-quality events per day and go deep
- After Parties are the prime social setting — don't head back to the hotel too early

## Safety & Compliance Notes
- **Security first**: Protect your wallet and private keys; avoid unknown WiFi networks and DApps
- **Stay scam-aware**: Be alert to phishing links, fake airdrops, and social engineering attempts during the event
- **Compliance awareness**: Investment-related content requires NFA / DYOR disclaimers
- **Pace yourself**: Consecutive days of conference + Side Events + parties are physically demanding — rest matters
- **Filter information**: The information volume is overwhelming — stay focused on your core objectives and don't try to do everything
