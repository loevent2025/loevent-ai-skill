# AI Hackathon Knowledge Base – General Track

## Overview
General AI hackathons are open, all-inclusive development competitions welcoming developers and AI enthusiasts of all backgrounds. With no restrictions on tech stack or application domain, participants are free to build whatever they envision. These events offer broad reach, an accessible entry bar, and serve as an ideal platform for AI development skill-building, technical exploration, and early-stage project incubation.

---

## Event Profile
- **Scale**: Tens to hundreds of participants; large-scale hackathons can reach thousands
- **Duration**: Typically 24–72 hours of intensive development + showcase and judging
- **Format**: Team building + mentor guidance + product demo + judging and awards
- **Participants**: Developers, product managers, designers, founders, students
- **Defining characteristics**: Highly open format, diverse tracks, wide creative space

## Participation Tips

### Pre-Event Preparation
- Study the API documentation for the LLMs you plan to use
- Understand the distinct capabilities of different models
- Set up your development environment and toolchain
- Brainstorm a few backup project directions
- Collect useful Prompt Engineering techniques and resources

### Project Selection
- Solve a real problem rather than show off technical complexity
- Leverage the specific strengths of the LLM you're working with
- Make sure the demo clearly communicates the value delivered
- Think about the project's scalability potential
- Differentiation is critical — what makes your approach unique?

### Team Formation
- Ideal mix: development + product + design
- Prompt Engineering capability is highly valuable
- Teammates with domain expertise are a big plus
- Complementary skills beat headcount every time

### Demo Tips
- Lead with the problem you're solving
- Show a real, relatable use case
- Prepare for unexpected scenarios
- Highlight the concrete value AI brings
- Acknowledge limitations honestly — judges respect that
