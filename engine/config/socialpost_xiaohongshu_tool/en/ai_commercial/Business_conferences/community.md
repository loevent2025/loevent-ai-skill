# AI BUSINESS CONFERENCES × COMMUNITY

## Use Cases
AI developer community conferences, open-source project annual meetups, AI practitioner gatherings, tech community offline events, AI entrepreneur communities, user community conferences

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Community sharing + member exchange + project showcase + social gathering
- **Core Value:** Community belonging, experience exchange, network building, growing together

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Community Members — gaining information, building connections, sense of belonging
- **Secondary Audience:** Core Contributors — showcasing achievements, earning recognition
- **Tertiary Audience:** Prospective Members — learning about the community, considering joining
- **Extended Audience:** Partners — understanding the community, exploring collaboration
- **Audience Behavior:** Gaining information, social connection, sharing experiences, building belonging

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Internal-oriented:** Community members + contributors — best for internal cohesion events
- **Open-oriented:** Fully open — best for community growth events

## Content Elements
- Community identity callout
- Community / project progress updates
- Member experience sharing
- Contributor recognition segment
- Open networking time
- Future plans and roadmap

## Language Tone
- Community-native, casual register; emphasize "we"
- Warmth, authenticity, and recognition
- Warm, genuine, relaxed
- Emphasize exchange and sharing
- Genuine, community-first tone

## Content Structure
- Opening callout block: 15% — community identity affirmation
- Content sharing block: 30% — updates + actionable insights
- Member sharing block: 25% — real experiences
- Social interaction block: 20% — exchange + recognition
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 14–18 total, warm and energetic

## Emotional Tone
Warm, belonging, growing together. The feeling of "being with people who are on the same journey."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Developer Community
- **Best for:** AI developer communities, tech communities
- **Opener format:** 👨‍💻 {Community Name} Developer Meetup | Catching up with fellow builders
- **Tone:** Technical exchange, relaxed, builder culture

### Variant B: Open Source Community
- **Best for:** Open-source project community annual gatherings
- **Opener format:** 🌟 {Project Name} Community Meetup | A gathering for the people building together
- **Tone:** Open-source spirit, contribution recognition, co-building

### Variant C: Founder Community
- **Best for:** AI entrepreneur community gatherings
- **Opener format:** 🚀 AI Founder Meetup | Coffee with fellow travelers
- **Tone:** Empathetic, supportive, solidarity

## Attendance Tips

### Before the Event
- Think about what you want to talk about and who you want to meet
- Prepare a simple, natural introduction
- Come to community events with curiosity and an open mind

### On-Site Tips
- Say hello to people first — everyone here is part of the community
- Find the people you've been chatting with online
- Don't just stick with people you already know — meet new faces
- Exchange contact information

### Post-Event Follow-Up
- Share your experience in the community group
- Stay in touch with new connections
- Stay active in the community

## Notes

### Key Principles for Community AI Business Conferences
1. **Belonging is the core:** Make members feel "this is ours"
2. **Connection matters more than content:** The value of a community event is in the relationships it creates
3. **Recognize contributors:** Make sure the people who put in the work feel seen
4. **Keep the atmosphere relaxed:** This isn't a formal conference — it's a gathering of friends
5. **Keep it to the right size:** Too large and the community feeling disappears
