## I. DEMO DAYS × Cross-Community Collaboration × Community

### Use Cases
Cross-community joint showcases, multi-ecosystem contributor exhibitions, Web2 × Web3 community exchange shows, joint pitches across project communities, DAO alliance achievement showcases, cross-cultural / cross-regional community joint events

### Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Cross-community project showcases, dual-community member interaction, joint recognition mechanisms, cross-community networking
- **Core Value:** Breaking community silos, cross-community connections, dual belonging, combined influence

### Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Members of both communities — active contributors from each side
- **Secondary Audience:** Cross-community actives — people already participating in both communities
- **Tertiary Audience:** Curious members — those who want to learn more about "the other community"
- **Extended Audience:** Prospective new members — people drawn in by the joint event, considering joining
- **Audience Behavior:** Showcasing contributions, meeting people from the other community, building cross-community connections, expanding their sense of belonging

#### Common Audience Configurations
- **Joint showcase format:** Roughly equal contributors from both communities — ideal for joint achievement exhibitions
- **Community visit format:** One community as host, one as visiting guest — ideal for community exchange days

### Content Elements
- Dual-community identity callout
- Cross-community context (why these two communities are coming together)
- Project / contribution showcases from both sides
- Cross-community voting / peer review mechanism
- Joint recognition (dual-community POAP, co-branded badge)
- Cross-community networking segment

### Language Tone
- Dual-community identity affirmation
- "You + us = a bigger us"
- Emphasize mutual learning and mutual appreciation
- Reduce the "outsider" feeling for newcomers
- Foster a shared sense of belonging

### Content Structure
- Opening callout block: 15% — dual-community identity + meaning of coming together
- Cross-community context block: 15% — why we're here together
- Project showcase block: 35% — contribution displays from both sides
- Cross-community interaction block: 20% — peer review + networking
- Call-to-action block: 15% — joint community entry points
- Emoji density: 14–18 total, warm and inclusive tone

### Emotional Tone
Inclusive, celebratory, boundary-breaking. The warmth of "two communities meeting and becoming a bigger home."

### Style Variants

#### Variant A: Cross-Community Joint Showcase
- **Best for:** Two project communities showcasing together
- **Opener format:** 💙🤝💜 {Community A} × {Community B} Joint Showcase Day | Two communities, celebrating together
- **Tone:** United, celebratory, boundary-breaking

#### Variant B: Ecosystem Alliance
- **Best for:** Multiple ecosystem communities joining forces
- **Opener format:** ⚡🔗 {Alliance Name} Community Showcase Day | Cross-ecosystem contributors, assembled
- **Tone:** Alliance, collaborative, co-building

#### Variant C: Community Visit
- **Best for:** One community visiting another
- **Opener format:** 🌉💙 {Community A} visits {Community B} | Dropping by, making new friends
- **Tone:** Friendly, curious, open exchange

### Notes

#### Key Principles for Cross-Community Demo Days
1. **Dual identity recognition:** Make members from both sides feel equally respected
2. **Mutual appreciation, not comparison:** Emphasize learning from each other, not competing
3. **Cross-community recognition mechanisms:** Co-branded POAP, cross-community roles
4. **Intentionally design mixed networking:** Break the "everyone sticks to their own side" pattern
5. **Follow-up connections:** Cross-community channels, joint community spaces
