## 📝 Required Content Elements

### 1. Contrast & Tension Building (required)

**Formula**:
```
"What happens when [diverse forces representing the traditional world] meet [diverse ecosystems representing Web3]?"
```

**Guidelines**:
- Clearly identify the crossover dimensions the event brings together (capital, technology, talent, community, industry)
- Use contrasting sentence structures to highlight the characteristics of each world
- Emphasize the scarcity and unique value of this multi-dimensional crossover combination

---

### 2. Chemical Reaction Description (required)

**Dynamic verbs**:
- ✅ Use: "collide," "converge," "intersect," "spark," "reshape," "break barriers"
- ❌ Avoid: "cooperate," "combine," "partner," "connect"

**Multi-Dimensional Convergence Formula**:
```
[Traditional world strengths] × [Web3 world capabilities] × [Multi-track coverage] = [Unique crossover value]
```

**Guidelines**:
- Describe what multi-dimensional crossover convergence can produce that no single dimension can achieve alone
- Explain why participants from different backgrounds should all attend

---

### 3. Participant Collision Description (required)

**Multi-Dimensional Collision Scenarios**:
```
"Here, traditional VCs and Crypto Funds judge side by side, big-tech architects and protocol developers exchange ideas in the same room, Web2 communities and Web3 communities vote together"
```

**Crossover Convergence Value**:
```
"People from different backgrounds arrive with different questions and find different answers in the same space"
```

---

### 4. Scene & Atmosphere Building (choose at least 2)

**a) Concrete Scenes**
- Don't write: "The event brings together participants from multiple backgrounds"
- Do write: Specific descriptions of participants from different backgrounds colliding in the same space
- Convey the richness of multi-dimensional crossover threads woven into a single event

**b) Time Flow**
- Describe the rhythm of different crossover collisions unfolding throughout the event
- Show the progression from initial unfamiliarity to genuine convergence by the end

**c) Dynamic Interaction**
- Don't write: "Participants can network freely"
- Do write: Specific descriptions of participants from different backgrounds discussing the same project from different perspectives

**d) Emotional Energy**
- Opening: Anticipation, Curiosity, Diversity
- Collision: Surprise, Discovery, Perspective shift
- Closing: Convergence, Connection, Continuation

---

### 5. Unique Crossover Value Proposition (required)

**Describe outcomes that only multi-dimensional crossover convergence can produce**:
- List the value that can only be achieved through this specific combination of backgrounds, tracks, and communities
- Explain why participants from both the traditional world and the Web3 world benefit
- Emphasize the irreplaceability of a comprehensive crossover event

---

### 6. Transformation Arc (recommended)

**Participant Perspective Journey**:
- **Start**: Arriving with [a single-domain perspective / curiosity about the other world]
- **Process**: Seeing convergence solutions in [technical tracks], hearing crossover logic in [investment sessions], feeling cultural collision in [community interactions]
- **End**: Leaving with [multi-dimensional perspectives / cross-community connections / integrated understanding]

---

### 7. Compliance Disclaimer (required)

```
"⚠️ Project showcases do not constitute investment advice (NFA)"
```

## ⚠️ Prohibited Actions

- ❌ Belittling any background or community
- ❌ Framing project showcases as investment recommendations
- ❌ Overstating the maturity or immediate value of crossover convergence
- ❌ Leaking undisclosed project information
- ❌ Promising project future performance or crossover premium
- ❌ Ignoring crossover convergence value — writing it as a generic Demo Day
- ❌ Using passive terms like "cooperate" or "combine" instead of conveying collision energy
- ❌ Over-hyping the crossover concept without substantive multi-dimensional value to back it up
- ❌ Emphasizing only one crossover dimension while neglecting the comprehensive nature of the event
