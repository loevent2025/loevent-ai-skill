# Web3 Tech Workshop Knowledge Base – Community

## Scene Overview
Community Web3 tech workshops are organized by developer communities, DAOs, or technical guilds, with a strong emphasis on peer learning, on-chain hands-on practice, and a sense of belonging. These events are typically free or funded by ecosystem grants, with a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere. They serve as a vital channel for community members to learn smart contract development together, share lessons learned from on-chain experience, and build meaningful Builder connections — a core expression of Web3 technical community vitality.

---

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale**: Typically 10–50 participants, enabling interaction and collaborative debugging
- **Duration**: 2–4 hours, flexibly arranged
- **Format**: Community sharing + hands-on practice + open networking + peer learning
- **Participants**: Community Builders, tech enthusiasts, independent developers, learners
- **Key Traits**: Welcoming atmosphere, low barrier to entry, strong peer-support culture, social engagement

## Community Operations Guide

### Event Preparation
- Identify volunteer instructors and facilitators (community OGs or grant recipients)
- Prepare hands-on on-chain content designed for collaborative learning
- Select a venue with good WiFi, power outlets, and space for open discussion and coding
- Arrange refreshments and snacks
- Create a Telegram / Discord group in advance for pre-event engagement

### Atmosphere Building
- Frame the event as "building together," not "attending a lecture"
- Encourage questions and collaborative debugging
- Volunteers should warmly welcome newcomers
- Include icebreaker and introduction segments
- Maintain a light, friendly, and inclusive Builder culture throughout

### Peer Support Mechanisms
- Encourage experienced Builders to assist beginners
- Establish a mutual contract code review process
- Set up learning pairs and pair programming sessions
- Recognize and appreciate members who help others (POAP / shoutout)
- Foster a WAGMI culture of mutual encouragement and support

### Ongoing Operations
- Maintain a consistent event cadence (weekly / bi-weekly)
- Keep the Telegram / Discord learning group active and engaged
- Curate and share learning resources and reusable code templates
- Celebrate members' Build milestones (deployed a contract, won a Hackathon)
- Encourage members to step up and host their own sessions and demos
