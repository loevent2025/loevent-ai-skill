# Cafe Coworking × Investor / Event Playbook

## Applicable Scenarios
Founder coworking days, investor office hours (cafe edition), founder community workdays, FA cafe sessions, accelerator cohort workdays, fundraising prep workshops

## Scenario Profile
- **Format:** Founders/entrepreneurs working together at a cafe — investors may be present — informal interaction
- **Duration:** 3–5 hours
- **Core Value:** Founder community building, informal investor engagement, work + low-key networking

## Audience Definition
- **Primary Audience:** Founders / entrepreneurs — actively fundraising or preparing to fundraise
- **Secondary Audience:** Investors — looking to meet founders in a relaxed setting
- **Tertiary Audience:** Early-stage team members — attending alongside their founders
- **Audience Behavior:** Working, casually discussing their ventures, seeking advice, building relationships

## Content Elements
- Venue atmosphere (relaxed but with character)
- Participant breakdown (founder-majority / a few investors attending)
- Explicit "not a pitch event" framing
- Balance between focused work and organic conversation
- Compliance disclaimer (required)

## Language Guidelines
- ✅ Relaxed but professional
- ✅ "Not a pitch event," "not a deal-matching session"
- ✅ Emphasize working atmosphere, conversation as a byproduct
- ✅ Relationships before transactions
- ❌ Do not create fundraising anxiety
- ❌ Avoid "elite," "top-tier," "exclusive," "scarce"
- ⚠️ Must include NFA disclaimer

## Content Structure
- Scene-setting intro block: 15%
- "Not a pitch event" framing block: 20%
- Participant overview block: 25%
- Event format block: 20%
- Compliance + CTA block: 20%
- Emoji density: 6–10 total — professional and restrained

## Tone & Atmosphere
Relaxed and professional. Work-first, conversation as it happens. The feeling of: "Everyone's heads-down — chat if you want."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Founder Cowork
- **Use case:** Founders working together
- **Opening format:** ☕💼 Founder Cafe | Work hard, meet people along the way
- **Tone:** Peer, productive, low-key

### Variant B: Investor Hours
- **Use case:** Investor-present founder workday
- **Opening format:** ☕🤝 Founder × Investor Cafe | Not a pitch — just working in the same room
- **Tone:** Informal, relationship-first, low-pressure

### Variant C: Accelerator Cafe
- **Use case:** Accelerator/incubator cohort workday
- **Opening format:** ☕🚀 [Accelerator Name] Cafe Workday | Cohort heads-down session
- **Tone:** Cohort solidarity, mutual support, output-focused

## Notes & Best Practices

### Key Principles for Investor Cafe Coworking Events

1. **Reinforce "Not a Pitch Event" — repeatedly**
   - Work first, conversation second
   - No pitch segment
   - Relationships before transactions

2. **NFA Disclaimer is non-negotiable**
   - Conversations do not imply commitment
   - Nothing constitutes investment advice
   - Keep it casual in tone, but make sure it's there

3. **Investor presence is a feature, not the headline**
   - Investors are there to work too
   - Conversation is optional, not expected
   - Do not create a "compete for investor attention" dynamic

4. **Be clear about who's in the room**
   - Ratio of founders to investors
   - Stage of founders attending
   - Type/focus of investors present

5. **Relaxed but deliberate**
   - Venue should have character and quality
   - Atmosphere should be casual
   - But the right people need to be in the room
