# Café × Investment (AI / Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Investor cowork cafés, founder coffee hours, VC office hours at a café, portfolio café days, early-stage project café conversations

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Relaxed café setting, informal conversation, chat over coffee, drop-in format
- **Core Value**: Low-stakes conversation in a relaxed atmosphere, informal introductions, reducing the pressure of a formal meeting

## Audience Segmentation
- **Primary Audience:** Early-stage founders — founders who want to meet investors without the formality
- **Secondary Audience:** Investors — VCs and angels who prefer to discover projects in a low-key setting
- **Tertiary Audience:** Startup ecosystem participants — financial advisors, incubators, people curious about early-stage projects
- **Audience Behavior:** Have coffee and chat, informal conversation, learn about a project or a fund

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, informal, low-pressure
- ✅ The casual atmosphere of a café
- ✅ "Let's talk" — not "pitch"
- ✅ Strip out the tension of a formal pitch setting

### Absolute Prohibitions
- ❌ Don't write it like a formal pitch event or fundraising session
- ❌ Don't use "fundraising opportunity" or "investor connections"
- ❌ Don't make it feel formal or high-stakes
- ❌ Avoid language like "top-tier," "high-net-worth," or "elite"

---

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Café Scene Opening Hook (Required)
**Formula**: [Café atmosphere] + [relaxed conversation] + [informal feel]

**Examples**:
- "Talking about your project over coffee beats a conference room every time"
- "Not a pitch. Just coffee and a conversation"
- "Investors are people too. They drink coffee"

### 2. De-Formalization Language (Required)
- "No decks. No timer."
- "Not a pitch — a conversation"
- "Just tell them what you're building, like you'd tell a friend"

### 3. Time Flow Description (Recommended)
**Example**:
```
14:00  Investors arrive, grab a coffee
14:30  Founders filter in — find someone interesting and start a conversation
16:00  Some conversations are on their third round, others are working quietly in the corner
17:00  Keep going if it's flowing — head out if it's time
```

### 4. Live Interaction Snapshot (Required)
- ❌ "Founders can introduce their projects to investors"
- ✅ "Some conversations are about product direction, some about market fit, and some people are just quietly enjoying their coffee"

### 5. Office Hours Format (If Applicable)
- An investor is at the café during a set window
- Founders can drop in and start a conversation
- No formal appointment needed — just show up and talk

### 6. Low-Pressure Invitation (Required)
- ✅ "No deck required — just be ready to talk"
- ✅ "If the conversation flows, great. If not, no worries"
- ✅ "Think of it as a first introduction"

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Investor Office Hours @ Café
- **Best for:** Investors holding regular open office hours at a café
- **Opening format:** ☕ [Investor / Fund Name] Office Hours | Talk about your project over coffee
- **Tone:** Open, relaxed, informal

### Variant B: Founders × Investors Café Mixer
- **Best for:** Casual gatherings bringing founders and investors together
- **Opening format:** ☕ Founders × Investors Café | Coffee and introductions
- **Tone:** Mutual, peer-to-peer, relaxed

### Variant C: Portfolio Café Day
- **Best for:** A fund's relaxed gathering with its portfolio companies
- **Opening format:** ☕ [Fund Name] Portfolio Café | The family gets coffee
- **Tone:** Family feel, warm, low-key

## Notes & Best Practices

### Investment Café Checklist
1. **De-formalization is the core**: Emphasize "no pitch," "no deck required"
2. **Café atmosphere**: Relaxed, casual — like talking to a friend
3. **Reduce pressure for both sides**: Founders aren't nervous, investors aren't bored
4. **NFA disclaimer is required**: Even in casual settings, investment-adjacent content needs it
5. **Don't name-drop investor credentials**: No track records, no "top-tier" labels

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Don't write it like a fundraising pitch event
- ❌ Don't emphasize "fundraising opportunities"
- ❌ Don't make it feel formal or high-stakes
- ❌ Don't create a pressure-filled atmosphere
