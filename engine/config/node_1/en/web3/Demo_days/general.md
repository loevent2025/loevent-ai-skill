# Web3 · Demo Day Knowledge Base

## Section 1: Scenario Definition and Characteristics

### Scenario Overview
Demo Days are the core format for project-investor matchmaking in the Web3 sector. Through structured project showcases, they give founders a platform for visibility and provide investors with a structured deal-sourcing channel. They are the signature event format for accelerators, incubators, and investment institutions.

### Core Characteristics
| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| Scale | 50–300 attendees; primarily investors and presenting teams |
| Duration | Half-day to 1 day; compact and high-efficiency format |
| Format | Project pitches + investor feedback + one-on-one matchmaking |
| Positioning | Fundraising matchmaking, project visibility, investment discovery |

### Typical Event Formats
- Accelerator graduation Demo Days (Y Combinator, Alliance Demo Day)
- VC portfolio days
- Hackathon final-round pitches
- Vertical track project showcases (DeFi, GameFi, AI + Crypto, etc.)
- Regional startup pitch events

## Section 5: Key Success Factors

### Project Curation
- **Quality control**: Rigorous selection criteria to ensure consistent project quality
- **Diverse coverage**: Balanced mix across different tracks and stages
- **Information sharing**: Project materials shared with investors in advance
- **Presentation order**: Sequenced by quality and anticipated audience interest

### Pitch Preparation
- **Content coaching**: Business model clarity, data presentation, differentiated positioning
- **Delivery training**: Time management, pacing, Q&A handling
- **Materials refinement**: Deck design, demo preparation, supporting materials
- **Mock sessions**: Multiple rounds of practice pitches and iterative feedback

### Matchmaking Mechanics
- **Precision matching**: Investor-project pairing based on track, stage, and investment preferences
- **Booking system**: One-on-one meeting scheduling and time management
- **Meeting space**: Private meeting rooms and quiet conversation zones
- **Information access**: Project materials shared in advance; contact exchange facilitated

### Post-Event Follow-Up
- **Matchmaking data**: Document and organize meeting outcomes and investment intent
- **Continued facilitation**: Actively advance introductions after the event
- **Progress tracking**: Monitor fundraising progress; develop successful case studies
- **Investor relations**: Regular project updates and ongoing relationship maintenance

## Section 6: Key Considerations

### Project Protection
- Execute NDAs to protect core project information
- Control the distribution of materials
- Respect each team's fundraising timeline and information disclosure preferences

### Investor Experience
- Avoid information overload; cap the number of pitches accordingly
- Provide adequate time for one-on-one meetings
- Efficient scheduling and disciplined time management

### Fairness
- Transparent project selection criteria
- Equal visibility opportunities for all presenting teams
- Avoid organizer conflicts of interest

### Information Integrity
- Verify the accuracy of all project information before the event
- Prohibit misrepresentation or exaggerated claims
- Establish a credibility tracking and feedback mechanism
