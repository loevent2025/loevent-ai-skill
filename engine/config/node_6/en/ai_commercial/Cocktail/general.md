# AI Industry — Cocktail & Social Event Marketing Strategy

## Event Definition

Cocktail and social events are relationship-first experiences centered on atmosphere and informal connection. They create low-pressure environments that facilitate organic networking among industry professionals. The marketing focus is on generating a compelling "I want to be there" feeling — emphasizing experiential appeal over functional value.

## Core Objectives

- Cultivate a high-quality social atmosphere
- Attract precise engagement from the target community
- Establish brand identity and community culture
- Generate shareable social media content
- Build a sense of belonging within the core circle

## Distribution Strategy

### Hashtag Strategy
- Atmosphere tags: #AIBuilderNight #EveningUnwind
- Venue tags: #[City]Rooftop #RooftopVibes
- Community tags: #AICircleGathering #BuilderNetworking

### Content Strategy

| Phase | Content Type | Posting Frequency |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Teaser phase | Venue ambiance, date/time/location, community tone | 1–2 posts |
| Invitation phase | Past event highlights, how to attend | 1–2 posts |
| Event day | Live atmosphere, social moments | Real-time updates |
| Recap phase | Curated photos, next event preview | 1 post |

### Cocktail Event Content Tone
- Relaxed, conversational, and youthful
- Experience-first; minimize functional messaging
- Short sentences, fragmented and rhythmic
- Purely positive framing — no comparisons, no criticism

## Distribution Timeline

### T–1 Week: Teaser Phase
- Venue ambiance preview
- Date and location announcement
- Tone-setting copy: "rooftop, unwinding, interesting people"

### T–3 Days: Invitation Phase
- Send targeted invitations to core attendees
- Open registration (if applicable)
- Share past event atmosphere highlights

### Event Day: Live Phase
- Real-time atmosphere sharing
- Encourage attendees to take and post photos spontaneously
- Sync to Jike and WeChat Moments

### T+2 Days: Recap Phase
- Publish curated photo selection
- Light-touch recap copy
- Tease next event

## Invitation Strategy

### Invitation Tiers

| Tier | Method | Message Focus |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Core circle | Direct message / phone call | Personal invitation, confirm attendance |
| Extended network | Group message / Moments | Atmosphere showcase, registration link |
| Open audience | Public channels | Tone description, screening criteria |

### Invitation Copy Tone
- ✅ "Come or don't — but if you happen to be free..."
- ✅ "No agenda. Just chill."
- ❌ Avoid: "Don't miss out," "Once-in-a-lifetime opportunity"

## Atmosphere & Content Design

### Photo Opportunity Planning
- Map out photo spots in advance
- Prepare atmospheric props
- Assign photographer to capture candid moments
- Encourage attendees to share spontaneously

### Content Asset Types
- Venue b-roll (sunset, nightscape, lighting)
- Social moments (toasting, conversations, laughter)
- Detail close-ups (drinks, décor, brand elements)

## Performance Metrics

| Metric | Measurement Method |
|--------|--------------------|
| Invitation conversion rate | Invites sent vs. registrations received |
| Attendance rate | Registrations vs. check-ins |
| Social media share volume | Hashtag and tag tracking |
| Organic attendee content (UGC) | UGC post count |
| Return engagement intent | Sign-ups for subsequent events |
