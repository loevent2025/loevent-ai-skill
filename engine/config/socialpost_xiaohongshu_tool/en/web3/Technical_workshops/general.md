# Web3 Tech Workshop Knowledge Base – General

## Scene Overview
General Web3 tech workshops are hands-on learning events open to a broad range of participants. Under the guidance of experienced instructors, participants learn blockchain development and complete real on-chain projects. These events prioritize the principle of "learn it, deploy it" — making them one of the most efficient formats for quickly mastering smart contract development, DApp building, and on-chain interaction skills. Suitable for participants with varied backgrounds and experience levels.

---

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale**: Typically 20–80 participants, balanced for interaction quality and debugging support
- **Duration**: Half-day to 2 days
- **Format**: Instruction and demonstration + hands-on practice + Q&A + testnet deployment
- **Participants**: Developers, product managers, enterprise employees, Web3 enthusiasts
- **Key Traits**: Highly practical, immediately applicable, clear on-chain deliverables

## Curriculum Design Recommendations

### Time Allocation
- **2–3 hours**: Single skill focus, beginner level (e.g., write your first contract)
- **4–6 hours**: Complete end-to-end workflow, with break (e.g., contract development + testing + deployment)
- **Multi-session series**: Systematic learning, 2–3 hours per session (e.g., full-stack DApp development)

### Content Structure
- **Introduction** (10–15%): Why this matters — what you'll be able to build after this
- **Instruction** (30–35%): Core concepts and principles (grounded in real on-chain examples)
- **Hands-on Practice** (40–45%): Building and testnet deployment
- **Q&A** (10–15%): Address individual questions and live debugging

### Difficulty Design
- Define clear prerequisites upfront — avoid leaving participants behind
- Prepare extension challenges at varying difficulty levels
- Beginner-friendly does not mean unchallenging — set appropriate expectations
- Advanced participants can take on optional security and gas optimization challenges
