## 🔥 Cross-Disciplinary Core Elements (Module-Specific)

### Building Multi-Domain Collisions
**Narrative Formula:**
> "When [Domain A] meets [Domain B] meets [Domain C], what kind of spark will fly?"

**Required Elements:**
- ✅ Clearly highlight the differences and complementary strengths across participant backgrounds
- ✅ Use collision framing: "A brings…, B excels at…, C contributes…"
- ✅ Emphasize the rarity and innovation potential of this multi-domain mix

### Dynamic Verbs for Cross-Disciplinary Interaction
Use energetic verbs when describing cross-disciplinary dynamics:
- ❌ "Co-hosted," "Jointly participated," "Multi-party collaboration"
- ✅ "Collide," "Fuse," "Break boundaries," "Catalyze," "Co-create," "Cross-pollinate"

### Articulating Fusion Value
Answer the core question: "Why can this specific multi-domain combination create value that no single domain could achieve alone?"

## 🎭 Cross-Disciplinary Atmosphere Building Guide

### 1. Capturing Multi-Domain Collision Moments
```
"In the meeting room, a healthcare expert explains a clinical workflow — the engineer next to them lights up with ideas"
"During the coffee break, a finance professional and a creative start talking and realize how different their worlds are"
"After the demo, the panel — a mix of technical experts and industry mentors — offers completely different feedback"
```

### 2. Cross-Disciplinary Value Contrast
```
❌ Single-background team: Strong tech skills, but no domain context
❌ Single-background team: Deep industry knowledge, but can't build it
✅ Cross-disciplinary team: Tech + Domain + Experience = A product that actually works
```

### 3. Fusion Innovation Emotional Language
- Cognitive spark: "I never thought of it that way"
- Complementary wins: "We couldn't have done it without each other"
- Fusion energy: "This is something we built together"
- Boundary-breaking joy: "I met people from a completely different world"

## ⚠️ Prohibited Actions

- ❌ Creating a hierarchy or bias between backgrounds/industries
- ❌ Letting a single background dominate while marginalizing other participants
- ❌ Over-emphasizing tech to the point of intimidating non-technical participants
- ❌ Over-emphasizing industry knowledge to the point of sidelining technical participants
- ❌ Overstating guaranteed outcomes of cross-disciplinary fusion
