# Large-Scale Exhibition × General (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
AI industry summits, large model technology conferences, AI Agent ecosystem expos, enterprise intelligent transformation forums, AI infrastructure exhibitions, generative AI application conferences, AI product and growth summits, cross-disciplinary AI innovation expos

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Keynote addresses, breakout sessions, exhibition area, panel discussions, demo showcases, networking
- **Core Value**: Industry trend insights, technical solution comparison, real-world implementation experience, high-quality professional connections

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: AI engineers and technology leaders—frontline builders deploying AI systems in production
- **Secondary Audience**: AI product builders—founders and product leaders building AI-native products
- **Tertiary Audience**: Enterprise AI decision-makers—CTOs and VPs of Engineering evaluating and driving enterprise AI transformation
- **Extended Audience**: AI investors and analysts—investment firms and industry researchers tracking the AI space
- **Audience Behavior**: Acquiring industry insights, evaluating technical solutions, identifying partnership opportunities, building professional networks

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Builder Core**: AI engineers + product builders, suited for technically deep exhibitions
- **Business Decision**: Enterprise decision-makers + investors, suited for industry trend summits
- **Full Coverage**: All audiences, suited for comprehensive AI conferences

## Content Elements
- Exhibition theme and industry positioning
- Core topic design (Agent / LLM / Infra / Product / Enterprise Implementation)
- Keynote speaker lineup
- Breakout session / track design
- Exhibition and demo areas
- Networking sessions
- Technical community access

## Language Guidelines
- Contrast phrasing to highlight differentiation ("We're not here to talk about X—only Y")
- Technical terms integrated naturally (Agent, RAG, Fine-tuning, Embedding, etc. kept in English)
- Problem-oriented framing to spark resonance (❓ format)
- Verb-first expression ("dissect", "deploy", "evaluate" rather than "learn about", "understand")
- Avoid AI marketing buzzwords (no "empower", "disrupt", "revolutionary")
- Concrete value descriptions (specific data rather than vague claims)

## Structure Strategy
- Opening information block: 15% — Exhibition name + positioning + date and venue
- Background trends block: 20% — Industry landscape + core questions
- Content highlights block: 35% — Topics + breakout sessions + speakers + exhibition area
- Participation value block: 20% — Who it's for + what you'll gain
- Call-to-action block: 10% — Registration details
- Emoji density control: 8-12 total

## Emotional Tone
Pragmatic yet ambitious, technically rigorous yet commercially sharp. The overall message conveys "we're talking about things that actually ship"—anti-hype, anti-demo-magic, production-first.

## Background Pain Point Templates

### AI Agent Direction
**Trend Overview:**
AI Agents are moving from impressive demos to production environments. From customer service automation to complex workflow orchestration, Agent commercialization is redefining enterprise efficiency. But teams that have actually deployed Agents to production know the truth: between "it runs" and "it works," there are countless 3 AM on-call incidents.

**Core Questions:**
❓ How do you balance Agent autonomy with controllability?
❓ Where's the boundary between human oversight and AI decision-making?
❓ Which use cases have validated ROI, and which are still experimental?
❓ How many pitfalls lie between POC and production deployment?

### LLM / Large Model Direction
**Trend Overview:**
The large model landscape is evolving rapidly—open-source vs proprietary, general-purpose vs vertical, cloud vs on-premise. Enterprises must make tough trade-offs between model selection, fine-tuning strategy, and cost optimization.

**Core Questions:**
❓ When do you use an API, and when do you self-host?
❓ How do you evaluate a model's real-world performance in a specific business scenario?
❓ Fine-tuning vs prompt engineering—what's the true cost comparison?
❓ How do you build a sustainable model operations framework?

### Enterprise AI Implementation Direction
**Trend Overview:**
In 2024, nearly every enterprise ran an AI POC. But the share of projects that actually made it to production and delivered business value is likely under 20%. The chasm from POC to production is harder to cross than most expect.

**Core Questions:**
❓ How do you identify use cases that are genuinely suited for AI?
❓ What do you do when internal data quality isn't good enough?
❓ Build or buy? Where's the boundary between in-house development and procurement?
❓ How do you prove AI project ROI to leadership?

## Key Considerations

### Large-Scale Exhibition Best Practices
1. **Content depth is everything**: Topics must be specific—no vague generalities
2. **Speaker quality must be rock-solid**: Emphasize frontline hands-on experience over titles
3. **Breakout session design must be clear**: Help attendees quickly find the content they care about
4. **Avoid AI marketing buzzwords**: No "empower," "disrupt," or similar terms
5. **Exhibition area value must be clear**: Not just displays—include demos and direct engagement

### Authenticity Principles
- Only list specific speaker names after confirmation
- Case study presentations must be backed by real data
- Avoid over-promising—maintain a pragmatic tone throughout
