# B2B Conference × Technical (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
AI Technical Architecture Summits, LLM Engineering Practice Forums, AI Agent Technical Seminars, AI Infra Leadership Conferences, Model Selection & Optimization Forums, RAG/Fine-tuning Practice Conferences

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Technical talks, architecture sharing, panel discussions, case study retrospectives, technical Q&A
- **Core Value**: Technical depth, architecture decisions, production experience, technical networking

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: AI technology leaders — Tech Leads/CTOs responsible for AI system architecture and technical decisions
- **Secondary Audience**: Senior AI engineers — engineers with production experience seeking in-depth exchange
- **Tertiary Audience**: AI architects — architects designing end-to-end AI system solutions
- **Extended Audience**: Technical founders — AI entrepreneurs with technical backgrounds
- **Audience Behavior**: Gaining architecture insights, solving technical challenges, evaluating technology selections, building technical networks

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Technical Decision Layer**: Technology leaders + architects, suited for architecture and selection topics
- **Engineering Depth**: Primarily senior engineers, suited for hands-on experience sharing
- **Technical-Business**: Technology leaders + technical founders, suited for topics bridging technology and business

## Content Elements
- Technical topic positioning
- Architecture decision sharing
- Real production environment data
- Technology selection comparisons
- Lessons learned and solutions
- Technical panels
- Technical community entry points

## Language Guidelines
- Precise technical terminology (Agent, RAG, Embedding, Fine-tuning remain in English)
- Data-driven expressions (latency, cost, token consumption with specific figures)
- Problem-oriented openings
- Verb-first phrasing ("break down," "optimize," "debug")
- Avoid marketing language

## Structure Strategy
- Opening information block: 15%
- Technical challenge block: 25%
- Topic content block: 35%
- Participation value block: 15%
- Call to action block: 10%
- Emoji density control: 8–10 total

## Emotional Tone
Technically rigorous, hands-on oriented. Credibility of "validated in production." Peer exchange, not preaching.

## Key Considerations

### Essentials for Technical B2B Conferences
1. **Depth first**: Attendees are experienced — no introductory content needed
2. **Let data speak**: Share real performance and cost figures
3. **Peer perspective**: This is an exchange, not a lecture
4. **Actionable takeaways**: Shared content should be reusable and implementable
5. **Avoid marketing**: Maintain the credibility of the technical community
