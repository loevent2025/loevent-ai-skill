# Café × Community Type (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Product community Café pop-ups (e.g., Café Cursor), open-source project coffee meetups, user community Café Days, Builder coffee sessions, tool user IRL meetups

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format**: Café venue takeover / pop-up, user cowork, product team on-site, swag distribution
- **Core Value**: Community belonging, meeting like-minded people, chatting with the team, picking up swag, building together

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: Product/tool users—people who use a specific product day-to-day
- **Secondary Audience**: Community members—people active in the community
- **Tertiary Audience**: Potential users—people curious about a specific product
- **Audience Behavior**: Cowork, chat about what they're building, talk with the team, grab stickers

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style (Referencing Café Cursor)

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, chill, Builder culture
- ✅ "Come build with us" atmosphere
- ✅ Strong product community belonging
- ✅ Short sentences, fragmented, easy to scan
- ✅ Scene-first—the café is the main character

### Strictly Prohibited
- ❌ Don't write it like a meetup or sharing session
- ❌ No agenda or speaker segments
- ❌ Don't emphasize "learning" or "actionable takeaways"
- ❌ Avoid transactional language like "networking" or "expanding your network"

---

## Content Enhancement Elements (Referencing the Café Cursor Model)

### 1. Community Identity + Café Opening (Required)
**Formula**: [Product/Community Name] + [Café] + [Build Together]

**Examples**:
- "We've taken over a café and invited Cursor users to come build"
- "Café [Product Name] | Bring your laptop, grab a coffee, code together"
- "[Product Name] users—let's meet IRL"

### 2. Cowork as the Core (Required)
- "Bring your laptop. Come build."
- "You're not here to watch talks—you're here to code"
- "Everyone around you is using the same tool"

### 3. Time Slot Batches (Recommended, Referencing Cursor)
**Example**:
```
9:00–12:00 Morning session – grab a table and cowork
12:00–15:00 Afternoon session – grab a table and cowork
15:00–18:00 Evening session – grab a table and cowork
Or just drop in anytime for a coffee and say hi
```

### 4. Team On-Site (If Applicable)
- "The product team will drop in—ask them anything"
- "Engineers will be around—chat about any issues you've run into"
- "Feel free to share what you're building"

### 5. Swag Culture (Café Cursor Signature)
- Free coffee (espresso drinks on the house)
- Stickers and pins (free for the taking)
- Custom swag (branded coffee beans, tab keys, etc.)
- Credits / free trial quota

### 6. Social Dynamic Snapshots (Required)
Describe what's actually happening:
- ❌ "Attendees are free to mingle"
- ✅ "Someone's tweaking a prompt. Someone's debugging. Someone's peeking at the screen next to them. Someone's swapping GitHub handles."

### 7. Drop-In Friendly (Required)
- "Stay all day, or just drop in for a coffee"
- "Pop by for coffee and to say hi—totally fine"
- "Stay as long as you want"

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Official Café Pop-Up (Café Cursor Style)
- **Use for**: Product-official café events
- **Opening Format**: ☕ Café [Product Name] [City] | Come build with us
- **Tone**: Official but chill, strong community feel, build together

### Variant B: Community-Organized Café Meetup
- **Use for**: Café gatherings organized by community members
- **Opening Format**: ☕ [Product Name] User Coffee Hangout | Meet Your Fellow Builders
- **Tone**: Grassroots, like-minded, relaxed

### Variant C: Campus Café (Campus Edition)
- **Use for**: Product user gatherings on campus
- **Opening Format**: ☕ [Product Name] on Campus | Campus Builders, Let's Hang
- **Tone**: Student-driven, youthful, exploratory

## Key Considerations

### Community Café Essentials (Referencing Café Cursor)
1. **Cowork is the core**: You're not here to watch talks—you're here to build
2. **Community belonging**: Emphasize "people who use the same tool as you"
3. **Team presence is a bonus**: Being able to chat with the team is a highlight
4. **Swag culture**: Stickers, coffee, and swag are community event staples
5. **Flexible scheduling**: Time slots + drop-in option
6. **Low pressure**: Want to code? Code. Want to chat? Chat. Want to drop in? Drop in.

### Café Cursor Success Factors
- Full café takeover, drinks on the house
- Users bring their own laptops to cowork
- Team members on-site and available to chat
- Stickers, pins, custom coffee beans, and other swag
- Time slot registration with drop-in welcome
- No agenda, no talks—just build together

### Prohibited Practices
- ❌ Don't write it like a tech meetup
- ❌ No sharing or speaker segments
- ❌ Don't emphasize "learning" or "actionable takeaways"
- ❌ Don't make it too formal
