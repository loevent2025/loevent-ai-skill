# Cross-Sector Collaboration × Investment / Event Playbook

## Applicable Scenarios
Web2 × Web3 fundraising pitches, traditional industry + blockchain investment showcases, corporate capital × crypto fund joint deal reviews, cross-background founder fundraising sessions, dual-capital-backed startup hackathons, CVC + VC co-incubation events

## Scenario Profile
- **Format:** Cross-sector project pitches, dual-perspective investor panels, cross-sector 1-on-1 meetings, dual-capital networking
- **Core Value:** Dual-capital introductions, cross-domain feedback, emerging track validation, cross-sector resource integration

## Audience Definition
- **Primary Audience:** Cross-sector founders — teams operating across two domains who need capital from both sides
- **Secondary Audience:** Dual-background investors — VCs, CVCs, and Crypto Funds active in both spaces
- **Tertiary Audience:** Corporate/strategic capital — enterprise investment arms exploring blockchain applications in traditional industries
- **Extended Audience:** Cross-sector FAs / analysts — intermediaries and researchers serving hybrid verticals
- **Audience Behavior:** Presenting cross-sector solutions, gathering dual-perspective feedback, connecting with both capital types, validating cross-domain business logic

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Dual-capital matching:** Cross-sector projects + traditional VC + Crypto Fund
- **Corporate + financial:** Cross-sector projects + CVC + financial investors

## Content Elements
- Cross-sector fundraising context (why these projects require dual capital)
- Project's cross-sector positioning
- Dual-background investor lineup
- Dual-perspective evaluation criteria
- Two-sided matching mechanism
- **Compliance disclaimer (required)**

## Language Guidelines
- Lead with "the intersection of two capital worlds"
- Use dynamic language — collision, convergence, fusion — to articulate cross-sector value
- Emphasize "problems only this combination can solve"
- Clearly articulate the perspective differences between the two investor types
- **NFA disclaimer is required**
- Introductions ≠ funding commitments — manage expectations accordingly

## Content Structure
- Opening info block: 10%
- Cross-sector context block: 20% — why these two worlds need to meet + the chemistry created
- Project / content overview block: 25%
- Investor lineup block: 20% — dual-background configuration + perspective differences
- Matching mechanism block: 15%
- Compliance disclaimer block: 10%
- Emoji density: 8–12 total — professional and restrained

## Tone & Atmosphere
Opportunity-driven, bilateral connection, cross-sector breakthrough. The underlying tension: "Traditional capital evaluates the business; Crypto capital evaluates the on-chain value — you need to convince both."

## Notes & Best Practices

### Key Principles for Cross-Sector Investment Events

1. **Compliance disclaimer is non-negotiable**
   - NFA (Not Financial Advice)
   - Introductions ≠ funding commitments
   - Incubation / POC arrangements require further evaluation

2. **Dual capital is the core value proposition**
   - Clearly articulate what differentiates each investor type and what they bring
   - Design distinct matching mechanisms for each capital type
   - Represent both perspectives throughout the program

3. **Expectation management is critical**
   - Cross-sector fundraising typically has a longer timeline
   - Requirements from the two capital types may conflict
   - Do not over-promise outcomes

4. **The cross-sector tension must be authentic**
   - Be specific about why these two types of capital need to intersect
   - Project selection must be genuinely cross-sector — not just relabeled
   - Articulate the value that only this combination can generate

5. **Post-event follow-up mechanisms**
   - Bilateral matching requires differentiated follow-up approaches for each side
   - Organizers must coordinate to avoid conflicting outreach
   - Provide ongoing cross-sector resource support beyond the event itself
