# Web3 Mixer Scene – Community Event Knowledge Base

## 🎯 Scene Characteristics Overview

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Format | Open socializing + lightweight rituals + party vibes + community connection |
| Duration | 2–4 hours, relaxed and free-flowing |
| Participants | Community Builders, ecosystem developers, DAO members, community newcomers, OGs |
| Core Objectives | Community connection, Builder downtime, fostering belonging, culture building |

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style Override

When a mixer-style community event is detected:

✅ Relaxed, party energy, community warmth
✅ Conversational, rhythmic, Builder culture
✅ Scene-first, atmosphere-led
✅ Community connections happen naturally—don't over-emphasize "networking"
✅ WAGMI spirit throughout, but don't overdo the slogans

❌ Do not over-emphasize "resource matchmaking," "partnership opportunities," or "expanding your network"
❌ Don't write it like a formal community meeting or work update
❌ Avoid transactional descriptions—keep the community spirit pure
❌ Don't create distance between OGs and newcomers

**Core Principle**:
🎯 This is a community party. Frens are the main characters. Connections happen naturally. Belonging is the best takeaway.

## 📝 Required Content Elements

### 1. Scene-First Opening (Required)

Lead with the "party vibe" elements of the venue:

**Formula**:
```
[Venue atmosphere] + [Social/party elements] + [Community feel]
```

**Guidelines**:
- ❌ Don't write: "We will be hosting a community gathering"
- ✅ Do write: The venue, the drinks, that feeling of "frens all in one place"
- Make it feel like a community party, not a community meeting

---

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (Include at Least 3)

**Visual**:
- Lighting: "warm glow," "neon," "candlelight," "city lights through the windows"
- Spatial feel: "rooftop terrace," "industrial loft," "open bar"
- Community elements: "community memes scrolling on the projector," "walls covered with Builder creations"

**Taste**:
- Be specific: "ice-cold craft beer," "signature cocktail," "pizza and finger food"
- ❌ Avoid: "Refreshments will be provided"
- ✅ Do write: "Beers are on ice. Pizza is on its way."

**Sound**:
- Music: "chill beats," "lo-fi hip hop," "DJ spinning a crowd-voted playlist"
- Ambient sound: "glasses clinking," "laughter," "someone in the corner chatting about their latest project"

**Touch**:
- Temperature: "that warm, tipsy glow," "evening breeze on the rooftop"
- Connection: "finally shaking hands with someone you've been chatting with online for a year," "hugging the person who helped you debug"

**Atmosphere**:
- Energy level: "chill," "relaxed," "tipsy," "the vibe is just right," "energy keeps building"
- Social dynamics: "small clusters forming," "someone just hit it off with a stranger," "OGs and newcomers all mixed together"

---

### 3. Passage of Time (Recommended)

Use fragmented timelines to create a party rhythm:

**Format**:
```
[Time] [What's happening] – brief, with community feel
```

**Guidelines**:
- Capture the natural flow from "arriving" to "not wanting to leave"
- Weave in community highlights (raffles, group photos, spontaneous sharing)
- Make it feel like a community party with its own rhythm

---

### 4. Social Dynamic Snapshots (Required)

Describe "what's happening," not "what could happen":

**Guidelines**:
- ❌ "Attendees are free to mingle"
- ✅ "Someone at the bar just hit it off with a stranger. Someone on the couch is finally meeting a fren they've been chatting with online for a year."

- ❌ "Networking opportunities will be available"
- ✅ "Conversations happen naturally—you might meet your next collaborator while grabbing a refill"

**Use dynamic verbs**:
"Clinking glasses," "hugging," "finally meeting IRL," "swapping Twitter handles," "chatting until no one wants to leave," "heading out together to keep the party going"

---

### 5. Organic Community Connection (Recommended)

Don't force it—let the connections emerge as background:

**Guidelines**:
- ❌ "Expand your professional network"
- ✅ "That person you've been chatting with online for a year? Tonight, you finally clink glasses face to face"

- ❌ "Find partnership opportunities"
- ✅ "You start talking and realize you could actually build something together"

- Connection happens organically—no transactional language needed
- Use details and scenes to convey community warmth

---

### 6. Community Rituals (Recommended)

Lightweight but warm community moments:

**Guidelines**:
- Raffle moment: "Not a formal raffle—just everyone raising their drinks and shouting out a number"
- Group photo moment: "Everyone squeezes in, shouts 'WAGMI,' then one more for good measure"
- Gratitude moment: "Not a speech—just raising a glass and saying thanks to everyone"
- Spontaneous sharing: "Want to say a few words? Step up anytime. No pressure."

---

### 7. Newcomer-Friendly Atmosphere (Required)

Make newcomers feel welcome:

**Guidelines**:
- ✅ "OGs and newcomers all mixed together—nobody cares when you joined"
- ✅ "No need to be shy—everyone here is a fren"
- ✅ "Don't know anyone? No worries—you will by the time you finish your drink"
- ❌ Don't create distance between OGs and newcomers

---

### 8. Zero-Pressure Participation Framework (Required)

Make attendees feel relaxed and low-commitment:

**Guidelines**:
- ✅ "No prep needed—just bring yourself"
- ✅ "Want to talk projects? Go for it. Just want to drink and unwind? That works too."
- ✅ "Come or don't—but if you happen to be free"
- ✅ "Stay as long as you want—when the vibe hits, you won't want to leave"

- ❌ "This is a rare community gathering"
- ❌ "Don't miss out"

---

### 9. Community Spirit Expression (Recommended)

Weave it in naturally—don't overdo the slogans:

**Guidelines**:
- ✅ "GM frens," "WAGMI," "LFG"—use naturally
- ✅ "A group of people building interesting things in Web3"
- ✅ "The community exists because of you"
- ❌ Don't put a slogan in every sentence—keep it natural

---

### 10. Crowd Description – Focus on "Frens" (Recommended)

Don't describe by job title—describe by community identity:

**Guidelines**:
- ❌ "Founders, investors, developers"
- ✅ "A group of frens building in this ecosystem"
- ✅ "Coders, designers, community builders, and some who are purely vibing"
- ✅ "You'll meet some interesting people—what exactly they do? You'll find out when you talk to them"

## ✅ Mixer-Style Community Event Copy Quality Checklist

- [ ] **Scene-first**: Venue/party atmosphere is the opening hook, not "We will be hosting..."
- [ ] **Five-sense details**: At least 3 sensory details included
- [ ] **Party feel**: Overall atmosphere reads as community party, not community meeting
- [ ] **Dynamic portrayal**: Social scenes are presented as happening now, not as potential possibilities
- [ ] **Organic connection**: Not forced—let the details speak for themselves
- [ ] **Newcomer-friendly**: Newcomers feel welcome; no OG gatekeeping
- [ ] **Relaxed tone**: Casual, community-driven, conversational throughout
- [ ] **Non-transactional**: No over-emphasis on "connections," "resources," or "opportunities"
- [ ] **Low pressure**: Participation framed as relaxed and optional
- [ ] **Community spirit**: WAGMI/GM woven in naturally, not overdone
- [ ] **Belonging**: Implies this is a community gathering with continuity

---

## ⚠️ Prohibited Practices

- ❌ Writing the community party as a formal community meeting or work update
- ❌ Over-emphasizing "network resources," "partnership opportunities," or other transactional language
- ❌ Creating distance between OGs and newcomers
- ❌ Using pressure tactics like "Don't miss out" or "Rare opportunity"
- ❌ Writing a rigid agenda instead of a rhythmic party flow
- ❌ Over-promoting projects or products, disrupting the social atmosphere
- ❌ Excessive sloganeering that kills the natural vibe
- ❌ Any implication of excessive drinking or inappropriate behavior
- ❌ Over-commercializing a community event

---

## 🚫 Banned Vocabulary

**Transactional language**:
❌ "Expand your network," "resource matchmaking," "partnership opportunities"
❌ "Deep exchange," "high-quality networking," "make connections"
❌ "Actionable takeaways," "value," "key gains"

**Pressure tactics**:
❌ "Don't miss out," "rare opportunity," "limited spots"
❌ "Community mega-event," "event of the year" (unless it actually is)

**Distance-creating language**:
❌ "Star-studded lineup," "top-tier roster," "VIP guest list"
