# Photography Hybrid / Brand-Led Style (Revised Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Large-scale conferences, institutional events, professional summits, brand launches, regional events with a strong emphasis on venue identity

## Color Scheme
- **Brand Color Blocks:** Vivid, saturated (typically green, purple, or blue), occupying 30–50% of the composition
- **Photo Treatment:** Heavy color grading — duotone, gradient map overlays, desaturation with selective color accents
- **Text Zones:** High-contrast areas (white text on dark backgrounds, or dark text on light/colored backgrounds)
- **Light Leak Colors:** Dynamics sourced from the brand palette — lighter/brighter variants or complementary tones

### Common Color Combinations
- **Corporate Brand Green:** `#00C853` or `#4CAF50`, paired with black and white
  - Light leaks: Bright lime green (`#C6FF00`), mint green (`#00E676`), light green (`#76FF03`)
- **Corporate Brand Purple:** `#6A1B9A` or `#9C27B0`, paired with charcoal gray and off-white
  - Light leaks: Bright magenta (`#E040FB`), violet (`#B388FF`), soft pink (`#EA80FC`)
- **Tech Brand Blue:** `#0277BD` or `#2196F3`, paired with midnight blue and silver
  - Light leaks: Bright cyan (`#00E5FF`), electric blue (`#40C4FF`), sky blue (`#82B1FF`)

---

## Composition Strategy (Structure > Chaos)
- **Adjustment: Shallower diagonal split (30–45°)** — allows photography to occupy a larger share of the visual space
- Asymmetric balance with defined zones (photo zone + brand color zone + text zone)
- Clear visual hierarchy with well-delineated regions
- Photography provides atmosphere; geometry provides structure
- Layering via color overlays and gradient maps applied to photos

### Typical Layouts
- **30–45° diagonal split (shallower angle, photo-dominant)**
- Vertical bold panel (30–40% width)
- L-shaped color block
- **Photo: 70–80% | Brand color: 20–30%**

---

## Photography Elements
- Nighttime cityscapes with glowing windows and light trails
- Iconic architectural silhouettes (modern, recognizable)
- Bokeh backgrounds with softly blurred light sources
- Strongly stylized natural elements (mountains, bodies of water)
- Abstract architectural details with strong geometric quality
- Motion-blurred urban scenes
- City skyline silhouettes against gradient skies

---

## Photo Treatment Techniques
- **Duotone Effect** — Apply brand-color duotone grading
- **Gradient Map** — Overlay brand-color gradient maps
- **Selective Desaturation** — Strip most color; preserve selective color accents
- **Light Leak** — Emit light from panel edges using lighter/brighter brand-palette variants or harmonious complementary tones
- **Color Wash** — Layer a translucent brand-color overlay on the photo
- **High Contrast** — Enhance blacks and highlights
- **Blur / Bokeh** — Background defocus for added depth

---

## Light Leak Color Strategy

- **Derived from brand colors:** Use lighter, brighter, or more saturated variants
- **Complementary harmony:** If the brand color is purple, light leaks may include bright magenta/pink
- **Color temperature shift:** Cool brand color → add warm-toned light leaks (e.g., blue brand → cyan + subtle warm yellow)
- **Opacity variation:** 20–40% transparency for a subtle bleed effect
- **Placement:** Edge transitions between brand panel and photo, corner accents, diagonal stripe overlays

---

## Geometric Overlays
- Subtle geometric line work in thin white
- Suggestive of connectivity and digital networks
- Abstract network patterns — non-literal
- Diagonal line dividers
- Angular panel separators

---

## Text Zone Strategy
- **Brand color blocks:** Maximum contrast — white type for headlines
- **Dark overlay zones on photos:** Light text for secondary information
- **Unified gradient zones:** Spanning both the panel and photo areas

---

## Text Styling

- **Color Selection:** Choose solid colors that form high contrast with the zone they appear in:
  - On brand color blocks: Pure white (`#FFFFFF`)
  - On dark photos / overlay zones: Pure white (`#FFFFFF`)
  - On light zones: Pure black (`#000000`) or primary brand color
- **Fill Style:** Solid color fill only — single color, no gradients
- **Prohibited Effects:**
  - No outer glow / inner glow
  - No stroke / outline
  - No drop shadow / shadow
  - No gradient fill
  - No metallic or glossy treatment
  - No text effects of any kind
- **Overall Principle:** Text remains clean, professional, and highly legible — consistent with the refined aesthetic of the photography hybrid style. Brand identity is established through typeface selection and typographic hierarchy, not decorative effects.

---

## Mood & Atmosphere
Professional and contemporary, conference-appropriate, brand-centric. Balances corporate credibility with forward-looking aesthetics. Refined, with broad international appeal.

---

## Key Principles

> **Core Technique:** Photography serves as atmosphere and backdrop — not the subject. Heavy treatment via color overlays and graphic elements.

> **Adjustment Focus:** Shallower diagonal split (30–45°) positions photography as the dominant visual element, with brand color blocks as an accent rather than the primary subject.

> **Light Leak Principle:** Colors dynamically follow the brand palette, maintaining cohesive overall color harmony.

## Sample Prompt 1 — Brand Green (Revised)

Render the graphic as a flat image, surface graphic design, pattern design. Fill the canvas edge to edge — no external border, no canvas or paper texture, no mockup. Use a photography hybrid approach with bold, brand-led composition. **A subtle diagonal split runs from top-left to bottom-right at approximately 30–40°**, with the upper-left occupied by a vibrant emerald green (`#00C853`) solid panel, **covering roughly 25–30% of the composition**. The majority of the canvas (70–75%) features a stylized photo of an iconic nighttime cityscape — illuminated windows of modern buildings forming a luminous pattern — treated with a deep-blue duotone gradient map and subtle desaturation. A color-wash overlay allows the green to bleed into the photo edges, creating a smooth transition. **Light leak effects use harmonious colors sourced from the green brand palette: bright lime green (`#C6FF00`) and mint green (`#00E676`), at 25–35% opacity**, evoking a sense of modernity at panel edges and diagonal corners. Additional subtle light green (`#76FF03`) glow accents reinforce overall color cohesion. The green panel provides a clean space for white typography — event title and date — while the darkened photo treatment area accommodates secondary information. All text uses pure white (`#FFFFFF`) solid fill — single color, no glow, no stroke, no shadow, no gradient, no text effects. The overall aesthetic is refined, conference-appropriate, and internationally appealing, enhancing strong photographic expression through strategic brand color emphasis and harmonious light leak treatment.
