# VAPORWAVE — Visual Style Guide

## Applicable Scenarios
Web3 conference key visuals, hackathons, NFT drops, crypto art festivals, satirical brand campaigns, nostalgia-driven community events, anti-corporate movements

## Color Palette
- **Primary tones:** Soft pink, sky blue, muted purple
- **Background:** Off-white, soft cream, or light-toned gradient backgrounds
- **Accent colors:** Mint green, lavender, peach, aqua blue
- **Saturation:** Low to medium saturation with a faded, nostalgic quality
- **Color behavior:** Soft tonal values, washed-out aesthetics, vintage color grading

### Recommended Color Combinations
- Classic Vaporwave: Soft pink + sky blue + muted purple (on a cream background)
- Nostalgic Fade: Lavender + mint green + peach (with a vintage washed effect)
- Digital Dream: Aqua blue + pale pink (on off-white, with a retro filter)

## Visual Elements
- Vintage computer graphics and pixel art elements
- Ancient Greek and Roman statues and busts
- Early internet and Windows 95/98 UI elements
- Palm trees and tropical imagery
- Checkerboard floors and grid patterns
- Dolphins, Egyptian pyramids, and surrealist juxtapositions
- Vintage Japanese text and characters
- Satirically manipulated corporate logos
- Glitched low-resolution imagery and dithering
- Retro advertisements and consumer products

## Typography Guidelines

### Color Usage
- **Solid flat fill:** Text must use a single flat color — no gradients, no textures, no special effects
- **High contrast:** Select colors that create strong visual contrast against the background
  - Light / cream backgrounds: Pure Black (#000000), Deep Purple (#2D1B4E), Deep Blue (#0A1628)
  - Dark / saturated areas: Pure White (#FFFFFF), Fluorescent Pink (#FF10F0), Electric Blue (#00D4FF), Mint Green (#00FFAA)
- **Prohibited effects:** No stroke, no shadow, no glow, no 3D effects, no gradient fills

### Placement Strategy
- **Top center:** The preferred position for headline text — leave approximately 5–10% of the frame height as a top margin
- **Frame center:** Suitable for a single primary tagline or event name as the visual focal point
- **Dynamic angle:** Tilted 15°–30° to add retro energy and movement
- **Symmetrical placements:**
  - Upper-left / upper-right diagonal pairing (suitable for primary and secondary headlines)
  - Center-left / center-right horizontal symmetry (suitable for date and venue information)
  - Four-corner distribution (suitable for decorative phrases or labels)

## Post-Processing & Effects
- VHS tracking distortion and artifacts
- Low-resolution pixelation and dithering
- Soft Gaussian blur for a dreamlike quality
- Color channel separation and glitch effects
- Vintage photo grain and fading
- Scanline overlay at reduced opacity
- Duotone and tritone processing
- Screen door effect and interlaced scanning
- Soft vignette with warm color toning
- JPEG compression artifacts

## Composition Strategy
- Collage-style layering with incongruous elements
- Centered symmetrical compositions
- Vintage computer window frames and UI borders
- Juxtaposition of classical sculpture against digital elements
- Repeating patterns and tessellation
- Text overlays using retro computer typefaces
- Asymmetrical balance with surrealist placement
- Grid layouts reminiscent of early web design
- Negative space treated with minimal retro aesthetics

## Mood & Atmosphere
Satirical nostalgia, a critique of consumerism, dreamlike detachment. The overall atmosphere is melancholic yet playful — evoking the optimism of the early internet alongside postmodern irony. Through the surreal beauty of decaying digital imagery, it celebrates obsolete technology while questioning the aesthetics of capitalism.
