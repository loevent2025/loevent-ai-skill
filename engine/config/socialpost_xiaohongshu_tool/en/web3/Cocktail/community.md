# Web3 Cocktail Party × Community Knowledge Base

## 🎯 Scene Overview

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Format | Free socializing + lightweight rituals + party atmosphere + community connection |
| Duration | 2–4 hours, relaxed and free-flowing |
| Participants | Community builders, ecosystem developers, DAO members, community newcomers, OGs |
| Core Goals | Community connection, builder decompression, fostering belonging, cultural expression |

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style Overrides

When this is detected as a cocktail-party-style community event:

✅ Relaxed, party-forward, community warmth
✅ Conversational, rhythmic, builder culture native
✅ Scene-first, vibe-led
✅ Community connection happens naturally — never force "networking"
✅ WAGMI spirit woven throughout, without over-slogan-ing

❌ Never over-emphasize "resource matching," "partnership opportunities," or "network building"
❌ Do not write it like a formal community meeting or status update
❌ Avoid transactional language — keep the community feel genuine
❌ Do not create a sense of distance between OGs and newcomers

**Core Principle:**
🎯 This is a community party. Frens are the main characters. Connections happen naturally. Belonging is the best thing anyone takes home.

## 📝 Required Content Elements

### 1. Scene-First Opening (Required)

Lead with the "party feel" elements of the venue:

**Formula:**
```
[Venue atmosphere] + [Social / party element] + [Community feel]
```

**Key points:**
- ❌ Don't write: "We will be hosting a community gathering"
- ✅ Do write: The venue, the drinks, that feeling of frens all in one place
- Make it feel like a community party, not a community meeting

---

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (At Least 3 Required)

**Visual:**
- Lighting: "warm lighting," "neon," "candlelight," "city lights through the window"
- Space: "rooftop terrace," "industrial loft," "open bar"
- Community touches: "community memes on the projector," "builders' work on the walls"

**Taste:**
- Be specific: "ice-cold craft beer," "signature cocktail," "pizza and finger food"
- ❌ Avoid: "food and beverages will be provided"
- ✅ Write: "The beers are already chilled. Pizza's on the way."

**Sound:**
- Music: "chill beats," "lo-fi hip hop," "DJ playing a crowd-voted playlist"
- Ambient: "the clink of glasses," "laughter," "someone in the corner already deep in a project convo"

**Touch:**
- Temperature: "a warm, gentle buzz," "evening breeze on the terrace"
- Connection: "finally shaking hands with someone you've been chatting with online for a year," "hugging the person who helped you debug that nightmare"

**Atmosphere:**
- Energy: "chill," "easy," "slightly buzzing," "vibe is just right," "getting livelier by the hour"
- Social dynamic: "people gravitating toward each other," "someone just met and already deep in it," "OGs and newcomers mixed together"

---

### 3. Time Flow Narrative (Recommended)

Use a fragmented timeline to create the rhythm of a party:

**Format:**
```
[Time] [What's happening] — short, community-native
```

**Key points:**
- Show the natural arc from "just arrived" to "don't want to leave"
- Weave in community highlight moments (raffle, group photo, spontaneous sharing)
- Let the reader feel this is a party with its own rhythm, not a schedule

---

### 4. Social Snapshot (Required)

Describe what's happening — not what "might" happen:

**Key points:**
- ❌ "Participants can network freely"
- ✅ "Someone just met at the bar and they're already deep in it. Someone on the couch just put a face to the fren they've been DMing for a year."

- ❌ "Networking opportunities will be provided"
- ✅ "Conversations start themselves — you might find your next collaborator while you're grabbing a refill."

**Use active, present-tense verbs:**
"clink glasses," "finally meet IRL," "swap Twitter handles," "talk until they don't want to leave," "head out together for round two"

---

### 5. Community Connection — Let It Flow Naturally (Recommended)

Don't push it — let connection be the backdrop:

**Key points:**
- ❌ "Expand your professional network"
- ✅ "The person you've been DMing for a year — tonight you finally get to buy them a drink"

- ❌ "Explore collaboration opportunities"
- ✅ "Somewhere between the second and third drink, you realize you could probably build something together"

- Connection is organic — no need to make it transactional
- Use scenes and details to let the community warmth speak for itself

---

### 6. Community Ritual Moments (Recommended)

Light, but with community warmth:

**Key points:**
- Raffle moment: "Not a formal raffle — just everyone raising their glass and shouting a number"
- Group photo: "Everyone piles in, shouts 'WAGMI,' then we do one more"
- Gratitude moment: "Not a speech — just lifting a glass and saying thanks for being here"
- Spontaneous sharing: "Say a few words whenever you feel like it. Nobody's forcing you."

---

### 7. Newcomer-Friendly Atmosphere (Required)

Make newcomers feel genuinely welcomed:

**Key points:**
- ✅ "OGs and newcomers all mixed in together — nobody cares how long you've been around"
- ✅ "No need to be awkward about it — everyone here is a fren"
- ✅ "Don't know anyone? That's fine. One drink in and you will."
- ❌ Never create a sense of hierarchy between OGs and newcomers

---

### 8. Low-Pressure Participation (Required)

Help people feel relaxed and uncommitted:

**Key points:**
- ✅ "Nothing to prepare — just bring yourself"
- ✅ "Talk shop if you want. Or just drink and chill. Either's great."
- ✅ "Come if you can — no pressure if you can't"
- ✅ "Stay as long as you like. When the vibe is right, you won't want to leave."

- ❌ "This is a rare community gathering"
- ❌ "Don't miss out"

---

### 9. Community Spirit — Naturally Expressed (Recommended)

Weave it in — don't shout it:

**Key points:**
- ✅ "GM frens," "WAGMI," "LFG" — use naturally, not performatively
- ✅ "A group of people doing interesting things in Web3"
- ✅ "The community exists because of you all"
- ❌ Don't end every sentence with a slogan — keep it authentic

---

### 10. Describe People as "Frens" (Recommended)

By community identity, not job title:

**Key points:**
- ❌ "Founders, investors, and developers"
- ✅ "A group of frens building in this ecosystem"
- ✅ "Engineers, designers, community leads, and people who are purely vibing"
- ✅ "You'll meet interesting people. What exactly they do? You'll find out when you talk to them."

## ✅ Quality Checklist for Cocktail-Party Community Copy

- [ ] **Scene-first:** Venue and party atmosphere are the hook — not "we will be hosting..."
- [ ] **Five-sense detail:** At least 3 sensory elements present
- [ ] **Party feel:** Overall vibe is community party, not community meeting
- [ ] **Dynamic framing:** Social scenes are presented as happening now, not as potential possibilities
- [ ] **Natural connection:** Don't over-push it — let the details carry the warmth
- [ ] **Newcomer-friendly:** Newcomers feel welcomed; no OG hierarchy implied
- [ ] **Relaxed tone:** Casual, community-native, conversational throughout
- [ ] **De-transactionalized:** "Network," "resources," "opportunities" not over-used
- [ ] **Low pressure:** Participation framed as easy and optional
- [ ] **Community spirit:** WAGMI / GM used naturally, not as performative slogans
- [ ] **Sense of belonging:** Implies this community gathers regularly and has continuity

---

## ⚠️ Prohibited

- ❌ Writing a community party like a formal meeting or status report
- ❌ Over-emphasizing "networking resources," "collaboration opportunities," or similar transactional terms
- ❌ Creating a hierarchy between OGs and newcomers
- ❌ Pressure phrases like "don't miss out" or "rare opportunity"
- ❌ Writing it as a structured agenda rather than a party with its own rhythm
- ❌ Over-promoting a project or product in ways that break the social vibe
- ❌ Over-use of slogans that feel performative rather than genuine
- ❌ Any implication of excessive drinking or inappropriate behavior
- ❌ Over-commercializing community events

---

## 🚫 Banned Terms

**Transactional language:**
❌ "network building," "resource matching," "partnership opportunities"
❌ "in-depth exchange," "high-quality networking," "make connections"
❌ "takeaways," "value," "gains"

**Pressure language:**
❌ "don't miss out," "rare opportunity," "limited spots"
❌ "community blockbuster event," "party of the year" (unless it genuinely is)

**Distance-creating language:**
❌ "packed with industry leaders," "top-tier lineup," "VIP guest roster"

---

## ✅ Recommended Language

**Community / party feel:**
✅ "frens," "builders," "community OGs"
✅ "clink glasses," "catch up," "meet IRL," "finally face-to-face"
✅ "chill," "easy," "slightly buzzing," "vibe is just right"

**Community spirit (use naturally):**
✅ "GM frens," "WAGMI," "LFG"
✅ "people building together," "frens from the same ecosystem"

**Low-pressure invitations:**
✅ "come if you can," "stay as long as you like"
✅ "nothing to prepare," "just bring yourself"
✅ "no pressure either way — but if you happen to be free"
✅ "See you frens (or not, no pressure)"
