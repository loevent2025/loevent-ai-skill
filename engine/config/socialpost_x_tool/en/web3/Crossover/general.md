## I. DEMO DAYS × Cross-Sector Collaboration

### Applicable Scenarios
Web2 × Web3 project showcases, traditional industry + blockchain solution demos, cross-ecosystem joint Demo Days, vertical industry + Crypto pitches, projects presenting across both technical and business backgrounds

### Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Cross-background project pitches, dual-perspective judging, cross-sector networking
- **Core Value**: Exposure for cross-sector projects, resource connections across two domains, breaking down mental silos, validating emerging verticals

### Audience Segmentation
- **Primary Audience:** Cross-sector project teams — startups operating at the intersection of two domains
- **Secondary Audience:** Dual-background investors — VCs / CVCs focused on cross-sector opportunities
- **Tertiary Audience:** Traditional industry decision-makers — enterprise representatives exploring blockchain applications
- **Extended Audience:** Cross-sector practitioners — professionals with meaningful experience in both domains
- **Audience Behavior:** Showcase cross-sector solutions, gather dual-perspective feedback, identify cross-domain resources, validate market fit

#### Common Audience Combinations
- **Capital-focused:** Cross-sector projects + traditional VCs + Crypto funds
- **Industry-focused:** Cross-sector projects + enterprise partners + technology service providers

### Content Elements
- Cross-sector context (why these two domains need to converge)
- The cross-sector nature of pitching projects
- Dual-background judge lineup
- Cross-sector evaluation criteria
- Bilateral networking mechanisms
- Post-event cross-sector resource follow-up

### Tone & Language Guidelines
- Highlight "the intersection of two worlds"
- Use dynamic language — collision, convergence, fusion
- Emphasize "problems only this combination can solve"
- Articulate value clearly for both sides
- Avoid messaging that only resonates with one domain

### Content Structure Strategy
- Opening info block: 10%
- Cross-sector context block: 20% — why these two worlds need to talk
- Project overview block: 30% — pitching projects + cross-sector attributes
- Judge lineup block: 20% — dual-background judges + evaluation perspectives
- Participation value block: 15% — value for attendees from each background
- Call to action block: 5%
- Emoji usage: 10–14 total

### Tone & Mood
Energetic, discovery-driven, frontier-expanding. A sense of occasion — "witnessing two worlds collide."

### Style Variants

#### Variant A: Industry × Crypto
- **Best for:** Traditional industry + blockchain solution showcases
- **Opening format:** 🔀 {Industry} × Web3 Demo Day | When {Industry} Meets Blockchain
- **Tone:** Industry-grounded, implementation-focused, barrier-breaking

#### Variant B: Web2 × Web3
- **Best for:** Internet companies / projects making the transition to Web3
- **Opening format:** 🌉 Web2 → Web3 Project Showcase | The Step Across
- **Tone:** Transition, iteration, convergence

#### Variant C: Cross-Ecosystem
- **Best for:** Joint showcases across multiple ecosystems / protocols
- **Opening format:** ⚡ {Ecosystem A} × {Ecosystem B} Demo Day | Cross-Ecosystem Collision
- **Tone:** Collaborative, interoperable, combinatorial innovation

### Notes & Best Practices

#### Cross-Sector Demo Day Checklist
1. **Balance value for both sides**: The event must be genuinely compelling for attendees from each domain
2. **Judges must be cross-background**: Dual-background panels are the only way to deliver dual-perspective feedback
3. **Projects must be truly cross-sector**: Real integration — not surface-level rebranding
4. **Networking must be intentionally mixed**: Break up domain-based clustering to create meaningful cross-sector encounters
5. **Cross-sector narrative must be authentic**: Clearly articulate why this specific combination creates real value
