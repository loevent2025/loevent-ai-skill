# Developer Meetup × Community

## Use Cases
Project community developer meetups, ecosystem developer events, DAO contributor gatherings, protocol developer community events, NFT project technical exchanges, regular developer community meetups

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Community exchange + project updates, contributor interaction, casual socializing, belonging-focused activities
- **Core Value:** Community belonging, contributor connections, project awareness, community network

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Community developers — developers who are active within the community
- **Secondary Audience:** Contributors — people who are already contributing or want to contribute to the project
- **Extended Audience:** Community followers — developers interested in the project's technical direction
- **Audience Behavior:** Community connection, contribution exchange, project discovery, building a sense of belonging

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Core contributors:** Primarily active contributors
- **Open community:** All developers interested in the project

## Content Elements
- Community identity callout
- Event theme
- Project updates (if applicable)
- Contributor exchange
- Social segment
- Community connection

## Language Tone
- Community-native, casual register
- "We" — foster a sense of belonging
- Contribution recognition
- Light and welcoming
- Newcomer-friendly

## Content Structure
- Opening callout block: 15%
- Event overview block: 25%
- Community value block: 30%
- Social segment block: 20%
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 12–16 total, warm community feel

## Emotional Tone
Community belonging, builder culture. The feeling of home — "finally hanging out with the people from the community."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Community Hangout
- **Best for:** Regular meetups for community developers
- **Opener format:** 💙 [Community Name] Developer Meetup | Community builders, IRL
- **Tone:** Belonging, relaxed, connected

### Variant B: Contributor Exchange
- **Best for:** Exchanges between active contributors
- **Opener format:** 🛠️ [Project Name] Contributor Meetup | Let's talk about contributing
- **Tone:** Contribution, recognition, co-building

### Variant C: Newcomer Welcome
- **Best for:** Welcoming new developers into the community
- **Opener format:** 🌟 [Community Name] Developer Welcome | New builders, come on in
- **Tone:** Welcoming, guiding, enthusiastic

## Notes

### Key Principles for Community Developer Meetups
1. **Belonging is the core:** "We," "our community," "together" — keep it inclusive
2. **Welcome newcomers:** Make new members feel genuinely accepted
3. **Recognize contributors:** Make contributors feel seen and valued
4. **Core team presence:** Give community members direct access to the people behind the project
5. **Follow-up connections:** Community entry points and ongoing engagement
