# Café × Technical (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
AI Tool User Cafés, Developer Cowork Days, Coding Cafés, Tech Product User Gatherings, Open-Source Project Café Meetups, Builder Cafés

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Café takeover / pop-up, cowork + casual socializing, bring your laptop and build
- **Core Value**: Code over coffee, meet like-minded people, chat with product teams, grab some swag

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: Tool/product users — developers who use a specific tool day-to-day
- **Secondary Audience**: Tech enthusiasts — people interested in a particular technology area
- **Tertiary Audience**: Potential users — people curious about a specific tool
- **Audience Behavior**: Coworking, coding, chatting about current projects, collecting stickers

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Casual, chill, builder culture
- ✅ "Code over coffee" atmosphere
- ✅ Product community belonging
- ✅ Short sentences, fragmented, scannable
- ✅ Scene first — the café atmosphere is the star

### Absolute Don'ts
- ❌ Don't make it sound like a tech talk or meetup
- ❌ Don't emphasize "learning" or "insights"
- ❌ Don't be too formal
- ❌ Avoid transactional words like "networking" or "connections"

---

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Café Scene Opening (Required)
**Formula**: [Café atmosphere] + [Tool/product name] + [Build together]

**Examples**:
- "Coffee, code, a bunch of Cursor users"
- "We've taken over a café and invited [Product Name] users to come build"
- "Bring your laptop, grab a coffee, and ship something while you're at it"

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (Include at least 3)
- **Visual**: Warm café lighting, tables full of laptops, sticker-covered lids
- **Taste**: Espresso, pour-over, lattes, small bites
- **Sound**: Keyboard clacking, quiet conversations, the hum of the espresso machine
- **Touch**: Wooden tables, the warmth of a coffee cup, the feel of your keyboard
- **Atmosphere**: Focused but relaxed, chill, build vibe

### 3. Time-Flow Description (Recommended)
**Example**:
```
9:00 AM   Arrive, grab a coffee, find a spot
10:00 AM  Start coding — the person next to you is coding too
12:00 PM  Lunch break — chat about what everyone's building
3:00 PM   Someone's already shipped a small feature
5:00 PM   Wrap up, exchange GitHubs
```

### 4. Cowork Dynamic Snapshots (Required)
Describe what's happening:
- ❌ "Attendees can freely code"
- ✅ "Someone's tuning a prompt, someone's debugging, someone's sneaking a look at their neighbor's screen for inspiration"

### 5. Product Team on Site (If applicable)
- "The product team will be there — ask them anything directly"
- "Engineers will drop in — bring up any issues you've run into"
- "Not customer support — it's builders talking to builders"

### 6. Swag / Giveaways (Recommended)
- Stickers, pins, custom merch
- Free coffee
- Credits / trial allowances
- Custom coffee beans (if available)

### 7. Low-Pressure Participation (Required)
- ✅ "Stay all day or just drop in for a coffee"
- ✅ "No presentations, no demos — just come and build"
- ✅ "Chat if you want, focus if you want"

## Style Variants

- **Use for**: Gatherings for users of a specific tool/product
- **Opening format**: ☕ Café [Product Name] | Bring your laptop, come build
- **Tone**: Product community feel, chill, build together

### Variant B: Developer Cowork Day

- **Use for**: Open-source community in-person gatherings
- **Opening format**: ☕💙 [Project Name] Café | Contributors meet IRL

## Key Considerations

### Essentials for Technical Cafés
1. **The café atmosphere is the core**: This is a café, not a conference room
2. **Coworking is the main format**: Bring your laptop and build — not here to sit through talks
3. **Product/community belonging**: Emphasize "people who also use X"
4. **Low pressure**: Focus on coding, chat with others, or just drop in — all fine
5. **Swag culture**: Stickers, coffee, small giveaways are a bonus
6. **Flexible timing**: Pick a time slot or show up whenever

### Prohibited
- ❌ Don't make it sound like a tech meetup or talk session
- ❌ No agenda, no speeches, no demo segments
- ❌ Don't emphasize "learning" or "insights"
- ❌ Don't be too formal
