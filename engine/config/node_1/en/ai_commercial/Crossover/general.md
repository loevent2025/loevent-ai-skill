# AI Commercial · Cross-Domain Collaboration Event Knowledge Base

## Section 1: Scenario Definition and Characteristics

### Scenario Overview
Cross-domain collaboration events are a format within the AI commercial sector that breaks down industry silos and catalyzes productive encounters between heterogeneous groups. By bringing together AI technical talent and vertical industry specialists, they accelerate real-world implementation and scenario innovation. They represent a critical channel for ecosystem expansion, product implementation validation, and co-creating industry-specific solutions.

### Core Characteristics
| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| Scale | 50–300 attendees; cross-domain mixed-group format |
| Duration | Half-day to 2 days; can incorporate hackathon elements |
| Format | Cross-domain dialogue + mixed-team formation + scenario co-creation + demo showcase |
| Positioning | Scenario discovery, solution validation, ecosystem connection, market education |

### Typical Event Formats
- AI × Finance Innovation Day (AI + banking / insurance / investment scenario co-creation)
- AI × Healthcare Summit (AI technology + clinical specialists + healthcare IT dialogue)
- AI × Manufacturing Transformation Workshop (AI engineers + plant managers + automation specialists)
- AI × Legal / Compliance Roundtable (LLM capabilities + legal practitioners + compliance experts)
- AI × Creative Industries Collision (AI technology + designers + content creators)
- AI × Education Innovation Camp (AI developers + educators + EdTech product managers)

### Cross-Domain Participant Profiles

**AI-Side Participants**
- Background: Strong AI / ML development experience; limited industry scenario understanding
- Core challenge: "I have strong technical skills, but I don't know what products to build"
- Expected outcomes: Real-world pain points, industry know-how, implementation direction

**Industry-Side Participants**
- Background: Deep vertical domain expertise; unclear on the boundaries of AI capability
- Core challenge: "AI is everywhere, but I don't know what it can actually solve for me"
- Expected outcomes: AI capability awareness, feasibility judgment, partnership resources

## Section 5: Key Success Factors

### Cross-Domain Design
- **Bilateral recruitment**: Equal emphasis on AI-side and industry-side recruitment; target ratio of 4:6 to 6:4
- **Pain points surfaced in advance**: Industry-side participants submit real business pain points before the event; AI-side participants review them ahead of time
- **Matching mechanism**: Intelligent or manual matching based on technical capabilities and industry requirements
- **Shared vocabulary**: Prepare "translation" materials to help each side understand the other's terminology

### Collision Mechanics
- **Icebreaker design**: Mandatory cross-domain group assignments to prevent clustering by background
- **Structured dialogue**: Provide conversation frameworks to guide requirements discovery and solution discussion
- **Pacing**: Dialogue → co-creation → output, with defined deliverables at each stage
- **Facilitator role**: Assign cross-domain coordinators to facilitate communication and remove barriers

### Output Orientation
- **Scenario templates**: Provide standardized templates for scenario descriptions and solution design
- **Review mechanism**: Cross-domain judging panel evaluating both technical feasibility and business value
- **Resource commitment**: Strong proposals receive API credits, technical support, and business development follow-up
- **Post-event follow-up**: Defined post-event follow-up process to convert intent into action

### Atmosphere
- **Equal dialogue**: Avoid technical condescension or dismissal of industry expertise; emphasize complementary value
- **Cultivate curiosity**: Encourage "naive questions"; break the curse of knowledge
- **Make progress visible**: Display cross-domain outputs in real time to build momentum
- **Celebrate connections**: Award the best cross-domain pairings, not just single-dimension winners

## Section 6: Key Considerations

### Cross-Domain Balance
- Maintain strict bilateral composition targets; prevent one side from dominating
- Monitor the experience of the minority group to prevent marginalization
- Incorporate multiple cross-domain dimensions (technical × industry; large enterprise × startup; domestic × international)

### Expectation Management
- Clearly frame the event as a "collision and inspiration" context, not an "immediate deal-closing" context
- For AI-side participants: Industry problems are more complex than expected — patience is required
- For industry-side participants: AI is not a universal solution — boundaries must be discovered together

### Intellectual Property
- Establish IP ownership rules clearly before the event
- Anonymize sensitive industry information where required
- Execute basic NDAs as appropriate

### Post-Event Operations
- Establish a cross-domain community to sustain ongoing connections
- Regularly follow up on partnership progress; collect success stories
- Document failure cases as well — they build industry knowledge over time
