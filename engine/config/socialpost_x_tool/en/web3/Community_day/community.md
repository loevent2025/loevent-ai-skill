# Cafe Coworking × Community

## Use Cases
Community cafe days, DAO member coworking sessions, NFT community work meetups, ecosystem developer cafes, community contributor workdays, holder build days

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Community members working/building together at a cafe; fostering belonging and IRL connection
- **Duration:** 3–6 hours
- **Core Value:** Community cohesion, in-person meetup, building together, sustaining community culture

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Community members — online all the time, finally working together IRL
- **Secondary Audience:** Community contributors — core builders, collaborating on projects
- **Tertiary Audience:** Community-curious — people who want to learn more about the community
- **Audience Behavior:** Co-working, community conversations, project collaboration, IRL meetup

## Content Elements
- Venue atmosphere
- Community identity callout
- Build theme (optional)
- Who from the community will be there
- Community culture elements (merch, POAP, community logo)
- Online community continuation (Discord channel, group chat)

## Language Tone
- ✅ Community-native, casual, sense of belonging
- ✅ "Our people," "build together"
- ✅ Emphasize the special feeling of meeting IRL
- ✅ Community memes and culture references
- ❌ No overly formal language
- ❌ Avoid "exchange," "learning," "seminar"-type phrasing

## Content Structure
- Scene opener block: 15%
- Community callout block: 25% — community identity, who's showing up
- Build content block: 25% — what we're working on together
- Community elements block: 15% — merch, POAP, etc.
- Participation details block: 10%
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 10–14 total, community emojis welcome

## Emotional Tone
Community belonging, building together, IRL connection. The excitement of "we've been chatting online forever — today we actually get things done."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Community Build
- **Best for:** Community members working together
- **Opener format:** 💙☕ [Community Name] Cafe Build | Let's get things done today
- **Tone:** Community vibe, building, together

### Variant B: Contributor Day
- **Best for:** Community contributors doing focused work
- **Opener format:** 🔧☕ [Community Name] Contributor Day | Core team building together
- **Tone:** Core team, collaborative, output-driven

### Variant C: Ecosystem Cafe
- **Best for:** Ecosystem-specific developers/users coworking
- **Opener format:** ⚡☕ [Ecosystem Name] Cafe | Ecosystem builders working together
- **Tone:** Ecosystem, builders, connected

## Notes

### Key Principles for Community Cafe Coworking

1. **Community Identity Is Everything**
   - Lead with "our people"
   - Use the community's own language and inside references
   - Feature community logo, colors, and cultural elements

2. **Make IRL Meetup Feel Special**
   - Discord handles becoming real faces
   - From online-only to working side by side
   - Capture the excitement of finally meeting IRL

3. **Building Together Is the Point**
   - This isn't pure socializing — it's working together
   - Members can work on their own projects
   - Or collaborate on community projects together

4. **Community Elements Must Be Present**
   - Merch, stickers, POAP
   - Community playlist, decorations
   - Photo ops for the group chat

5. **Contributor Days Are More Focused**
   - Tied to specific projects to push forward
   - Attendees are relatively core contributors
   - Output-oriented format
