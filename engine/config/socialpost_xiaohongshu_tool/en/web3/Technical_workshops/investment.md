# Web3 Tech Workshop Knowledge Base – Investment

## Scene Overview
Investment-focused Web3 tech workshops are designed for crypto VCs, investors, non-technical founders, and enterprise decision-makers. These sessions help participants understand the capability boundaries of blockchain technology, evaluate the technical feasibility and security profile of Web3 projects, and make more informed investment and business decisions. Programming experience is not required. Instead, participants build technical literacy through on-chain product exposure, structured case analysis, and decision-making frameworks — equipping them with a technology-informed lens for investment due diligence and business judgment.

---

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale**: Typically 15–40 participants, curated small-group format
- **Duration**: Half-day to 1 day
- **Format**: Technical briefing + on-chain product experience + case analysis + panel discussion
- **Participants**: Crypto VCs, angel investors, non-technical founders, C-suite executives, strategic decision-makers
- **Key Traits**: Understanding-focused rather than coding-focused, business perspective, decision-oriented, NFA-compliant

## Curriculum Design Recommendations

### Content Planning
- Explain technical concepts using business language and analogies — use diagrams, not code
- Rely heavily on real project case studies (both successes and high-profile failures)
- Focus on the technical knowledge most directly relevant to decision-making — no programming required
- Deliver practical, usable technical assessment frameworks and checklists participants can keep
- Prepare a rich library of on-chain data analysis examples and case studies

### Audience Analysis
- Understand participants' investment focus areas and sectors of interest in advance
- Collect participant questions and knowledge gaps before the session
- Adjust content depth to match the specific audience (VC vs. founder vs. executive)
- Prepare sector-specific cases (DeFi / Infrastructure / Consumer / DePIN)
- Identify and specifically address common knowledge blind spots

### Instructional Approach
- Use clear, accessible language — avoid code and technical insider jargon
- Favor interactive discussion over one-way delivery
- Include an on-chain product experience segment (let non-technical participants actually interact with DeFi / NFT protocols)
- Build in ample time for Q&A and panel discussion
- Provide decision-making tools and assessment frameworks

### Value Delivery
- Leave participants with a practical technical evaluation checklist and decision framework
- Establish a channel for ongoing advisory access post-program
- Package and distribute key takeaways and case studies from the session
- Build an investor community group for ongoing peer exchange
- Provide continued updates on technical developments, industry trends, and analysis

## Important Disclaimers

### Key Points for Investment-Focused Workshops
1. **NFA Declaration**: Not Financial Advice — all content is for technical literacy and educational purposes only
2. **DYOR Reminder**: Encourage participants to conduct their own in-depth independent research
3. **No Project Recommendations**: Do not recommend specific investment targets — teach technical assessment methodology only
4. **Risk Awareness**: Consistently emphasize the technical risks and inherent uncertainties of Web3 investment
5. **Regulatory Awareness**: Be mindful of and note the varying regulatory requirements across different jurisdictions
