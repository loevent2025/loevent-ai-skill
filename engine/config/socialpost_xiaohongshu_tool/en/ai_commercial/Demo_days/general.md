# AI DEMO DAY × GENERAL

## Applicable Scenarios
AI accelerator graduation showcases, AIGC project demos, AI startup project demo days, comprehensive AI demo days, ecosystem project exhibitions

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format:** Project pitches + product demos + judge commentary + networking
- **Core Value:** Project visibility, product showcase, feedback gathering, investor connections

## Audience Profile
- **Primary Audience:** AI startup teams — showcasing projects, gathering feedback, seeking investment
- **Secondary Audience:** Investors — discovering projects, evaluating opportunities
- **Tertiary Audience:** AI practitioners — tracking startup trends, finding collaboration opportunities
- **Extended Audience:** Corporate venture and BD teams — identifying investment or partnership targets
- **Audience Behavior:** Showcasing products, gathering feedback, making connections, discovering opportunities

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Investment Focus:** Startup teams + investors — ideal for fundraising-oriented demo days
- **Ecosystem Focus:** Startup teams + practitioners — ideal for ecosystem showcase events

## Content Elements
- Demo day theme and positioning
- Presenting project overview and count
- Judge and guest lineup
- Pitch + demo format explanation
- Networking session
- Post-event follow-up mechanism

## Language Guidelines
- Project-driven; lead with the showcase opportunity
- Emphasize the value of live demos over slide presentations
- Judge and reviewer lineup is a key draw
- Professional but engaging tone
- Investment-related content must include a disclaimer

## Content Structure
- Opening info block: 15% — name, time, location, scale
- Project overview block: 30% — presenting projects + track distribution
- Judge lineup block: 25% — reviewer backgrounds + commentary format
- Value proposition block: 20% — who it's for + what they'll gain
- Call to action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 10–14 total

## Tone & Atmosphere
Opportunity-driven, product-focused. The anticipation of: *"The next AI unicorn might be in this room."*

## Style Variants

### Variant A: General Showcase
- **Best for:** Multi-track comprehensive AI demo days
- **Opening format:** 🔥 {Demo Day Name} | {N} AI Projects Taking the Stage
- **Tone:** Comprehensive, opportunity-driven

### Variant B: Accelerator Graduation
- **Best for:** Accelerator and incubator graduation showcases
- **Opening format:** 🎓 {Accelerator Name} Demo Day | Cohort {X} Final Presentations
- **Tone:** Achievement showcase, milestone validation

### Variant C: Track Focus
- **Best for:** Showcasing AI projects in a specific vertical (Agents, vertical AI, etc.)
- **Opening format:** 🤖 {Track} Demo Day | The Latest Projects in the Space, All at Once
- **Tone:** Vertical-specific, professional, in-depth

## Attendee Tips

### For General Attendees
- Review the presenting projects in advance and flag ones that interest you
- Come with questions ready — you can ask after each demo
- Use the networking session to find the teams you want to talk to

### For Investors
- Review project materials before arriving; come with specific questions
- Focus on the completeness and authenticity of the live demo
- Follow up promptly with teams that caught your attention

### For Founders
- Prioritize your demo — it's the most important part
- Be ready to answer both technical and business questions
- Make time to connect with other founders in the room

## Notes

### Key Principles for General Demo Days
1. **Demo Is Non-Negotiable:** AI projects must have a live, working demonstration
2. **Be Clear About Project Quality:** How many projects, which tracks, what stage
3. **Reviewer Lineup Matters:** It determines the quality of feedback
4. **Value Both Sides:** Compelling for both founders and attendees
5. **Investment Disclaimers Are Required:** NFA; matchmaking ≠ commitment
