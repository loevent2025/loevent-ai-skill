# Web3 Large-Scale Conference Knowledge Base — Technical Scene

## Scene Overview
Technical Web3 large-scale conferences focus on deep exploration and hands-on practice in blockchain infrastructure, smart contract engineering, and on-chain application development. They bring together protocol core developers, smart contract engineers, security researchers, and technical architects. These events are the primary venue for learning cutting-edge on-chain technology, building smart contract capabilities in a real-world context, and connecting with global technical builders — with a strong emphasis on technical depth, code-first culture, and open source principles.

---

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale**: Hundreds to thousands of participants
- **Duration**: Typically 1–3 days; events with a hackathon can extend to a full week
- **Format**: Technical talks + hackathons + workshops + open source sessions + demo booths
- **Participants**: Smart contract developers, protocol engineers, security auditors, technical architects, researchers, and students
- **Defining Traits**: High technical depth, strong on-chain hands-on component, code-first culture, open source ethos

## Attendee Tips

### Before the Event
- Confirm your development environment is set up (Foundry / Hardhat / Node.js / wallet)
- Review the agenda and speakers' technical backgrounds in advance
- Prepare specific technical questions you want answered
- Pack your laptop, charger, and any necessary adapters
- If participating in the hackathon, form your team and brainstorm ideas beforehand

### On the Day
- Select the breakout sessions most relevant to your work (note difficulty levels)
- Be hands-on during hackathons and workshops — don't just observe
- Record key contract code, architecture diagrams, and Gas data immediately
- Ask questions — of speakers, protocol developers, and fellow attendees
- Exchange Telegram / Twitter/X / Discord handles and join the technical groups

### Knowledge Retention
- Organize your notes and code the same day while everything is fresh
- Reproduce the contracts and techniques you learned in your local environment
- Share your learnings on Twitter/X or a technical blog — it deepens your own understanding
- Follow speakers' GitHub repositories and ongoing technical output
- Join the developer Discord servers of relevant protocols and stay engaged
