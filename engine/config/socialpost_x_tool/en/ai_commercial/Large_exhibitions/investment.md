# Large-Scale Summit × Investment (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
AI Investment Summit, LLM Sector Analysis Conference, AI Startup Fundraising Forum, AI Industry Capital Networking Summit, AI Unicorn Pitch Day, AI M&A and Exit Forum, AI Sector Deep Dive Conference

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Trend assessment, sector analysis, project pitches, investor roundtables, private networking sessions
- **Core Value**: Investment insights, sector assessment, deal flow, venture networking

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: AI investors — VC/PE investors focused on the AI sector
- **Secondary Audience**: AI founders — AI company founders currently fundraising or planning to fundraise
- **Tertiary Audience**: Corporate strategic investment — Corporate investment divisions exploring AI investment and M&A
- **Extended Audience**: Industry analysts — Research institutions and media tracking the AI sector
- **Audience Behavior**: Identifying investment trends, discovering opportunities, evaluating project value, building investor networks

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Investor Core**: VC/PE + Corporate Strategics — ideal for executive private sessions
- **Venture Networking**: Investors + Founders — ideal for fundraising-oriented events
- **Research & Assessment**: Investors + Analysts — ideal for in-depth sector discussions

## Background & Pain Point Templates

### AI Agent Sector
**Trend Description:**
AI Agents are widely considered the next major opportunity after ChatGPT. From AutoGPT to Devin, from consumer to enterprise, the Agent sector is evolving rapidly. But hype and real value coexist.

**Core Questions:**
❓ Where are the moats in the Agent sector — model, data, or use case?
❓ What kind of Agent products can sustain a viable business model?
❓ Enterprise vs. consumer — which holds more investment value?
❓ Is now a good time to invest in Agents?

### LLM Sector
**Trend Description:**
The LLM landscape is entering a shakeout phase. Winner-take-most dynamics are clear, and smaller players are under pressure. The rise of open-source models is reshaping the competitive landscape. Meanwhile, application-layer opportunities are beginning to emerge.

**Core Questions:**
❓ Are there still investment opportunities in foundation models?
❓ Model layer vs. application layer — how does the investment logic differ?
❓ How significant is the impact of open-source models on commercial viability?
❓ Where do the opportunities lie for vertical models?

### AI Application Layer
**Trend Description:**
The AI application layer is flourishing, yet products that have truly achieved product-market fit remain scarce. Copilot-style, Agent-style, AI-native products... the debate over product form factors is far from settled.

**Core Questions:**
❓ What kind of AI applications can build a real moat?
❓ Copilot vs. Agent vs. AI-native — which form factor holds the most investment value?
❓ How should we interpret growth and retention metrics for AI applications?
❓ How do pricing strategies impact the business model?

## Key Considerations

### Investment Summit Essentials
1. **NFA Disclaimer Is Required**: All content does not constitute investment advice
2. **Analytical Framework Oriented**: The value proposition is methodology, not investment advice
3. **Avoid FOMO Marketing**: Maintain a rational, analytical tone throughout
4. **Participant Quality**: Emphasize the verified backgrounds of investors/founders
5. **Networking Mechanisms Must Be Clear**: If fundraising networking is involved, spell out the process
