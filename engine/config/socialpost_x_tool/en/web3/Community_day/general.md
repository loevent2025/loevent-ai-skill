# Cafe Coworking × General

## Applicable Scenarios
Industry cafe coworking days, Builder Cafe, Code & Coffee, Founder Workdays, Product user meetups, Remote worker cowork sessions, Freelancer meetups

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Private buyout or reserved section at a café, bring your laptop and work, casual conversations, possible short sharing segments
- **Duration**: 3–8 hours, typically split into time slots (morning / afternoon / full-day drop-in)
- **Core Value**: A focused work atmosphere, organic interactions, and the comfort of being around like-minded people

## Audience Segmentation
- **Primary Audience:** Practitioners who need a work environment — want a place to focus while also meeting people
- **Secondary Audience:** Freelancers / remote workers — tired of working alone all day
- **Tertiary Audience:** Curious newcomers — want to hang out, meet people, and get a feel for the vibe
- **Audience Behavior:** Bring a laptop and work, occasional conversations, organic networking, may seek advice or collaboration

## Content Elements
- Venue description (café atmosphere and setting)
- Time slot options (morning / afternoon / full-day / flexible drop-in)
- What's provided (coffee, WiFi, power outlets, light snacks)
- What to expect (work, connect, optional short sharing)
- Intro Circle (optional: brief opening introductions)
- How to join (just bring your laptop)

## Tone & Language Guidelines
- ✅ Relaxed, low-pressure, and inclusive
- ✅ "Just bring your laptop," "Work when you want, chat when you feel like it"
- ✅ Emphasize atmosphere and the feeling of companionship
- ✅ All skill levels welcome
- ❌ Avoid formal or corporate-sounding language
- ❌ Avoid phrases like "takeaways," "learning opportunities"

## Content Structure Strategy
- Opening scene block: 20% — café atmosphere
- Time slot / format block: 20% — how to participate
- What to expect block: 25% — work, connect, optional sharing
- What's provided block: 15% — coffee, environment
- Audience description block: 10%
- Call to action block: 10%
- Emoji usage: 8–12 total, keep it light and clean

## Tone & Mood
Relaxed and focused, a sense of companionship, and organic conversation. The comfortable feeling of: "Find a place to work, and maybe meet a few people along the way."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Open Coworking
- **Best for:** Café coworking open to everyone
- **Opening format:** ☕ Cafe Cowork | Bring your laptop and work together for a few hours
- **Tone:** Open, relaxed, and inclusive

### Variant B: Product / Tool Community Cowork
- **Best for:** Coworking for users of a specific product (e.g., Cursor, Figma, Notion)
- **Opening format:** ☕💻 Cafe [Product Name] | [Product] users building together
- **Tone:** Product community feel, user connection, team may be present

### Variant C: Regular Coworking Series
- **Best for:** Recurring café cowork sessions (weekly / biweekly)
- **Opening format:** ☕ [City] Code & Coffee | Biweekly café workday
- **Tone:** Community-driven, consistent, sense of belonging

## Time Slot Design Templates

### Full-Day Multi-Slot Format
```
⏰ Pick the slot that works for you:
- 9:00–12:00   Morning session (early risers)
- 12:00–15:00  Afternoon session A
- 15:00–18:00  Afternoon session B
- Or: Drop in anytime and stay as long as you like
```

### Half-Day Format
```
⏰ 14:00–18:00
Come and go as you please — stay however long works for you
```

### Intro Circle Template (Optional)
```
🗣️ Opening Intro Circle (14:30)
Quick round of introductions:
- Who you are
- What you're working on today
- What help you're looking for / what you can offer others
After that, it's self-organized — work if you want, chat if you want
```

## Notes & Best Practices

### Café Coworking Checklist

1. **Keep time slots flexible**
   - Offer multiple slot options
   - Allow drop-in (come and go freely)
   - Clearly state capacity per slot

2. **Be clear about venue details**
   - WiFi reliability
   - Number of available power outlets
   - Whether a quiet zone is available
   - Coffee / drink arrangements

3. **Intro Circle is optional**
   - If included, specify the time and format clearly
   - Emphasize that participants can pass
   - After the circle, everything is self-organized

4. **Reinforce the low-pressure feel**
   - Work if you want, chat if you want
   - Not a meeting, not a course
   - Just bring your laptop

5. **Special notes for Product Community Cowork**
   - Team presence is a key selling point
   - Swag / credits are perks, not the focus
   - Workshops are optional, not the main event
