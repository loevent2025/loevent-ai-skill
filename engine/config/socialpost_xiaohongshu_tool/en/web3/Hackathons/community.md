# Web3 Hackathon Knowledge Base – Community Track

## Overview
Community-focused Web3 hackathons emphasize social connection and a sense of belonging, cultivating a relaxed and fun BUIDL atmosphere. These events serve as a key vehicle for Builder gatherings, community building, and Web3 culture. Beyond the joy of building, participants forge meaningful connections with like-minded peers. Compared to competitive or commercially oriented hackathons, the community track prioritizes participant experience and community engagement — and is the truest expression of Web3's "community first" ethos.

---

## Event Profile
- **Scale**: Tens to hundreds of participants; community core members
- **Duration**: Typically 24–48 hours; flexible format
- **Format**: Team building + social activities + project showcase + celebration party
- **Participants**: Web3 enthusiasts, community members, open-source contributors, indie Builders
- **Defining characteristics**: Relaxed atmosphere, strong social element, inclusive and diverse, fun-first mindset

## Community Operations Recommendations

### Creating the Right Atmosphere
- Emphasize "fun" over "competition"
- Provide plenty of food and drinks
- Play relaxed background music
- Organizers should warmly welcome every attendee — greet people with GM
- Actively encourage socializing and mingling

### Event Design
- Include icebreaker and dedicated social sessions
- Create fun, memorable award categories (Most Degen Project, Most Meme-worthy, etc.)
- Don't mandate all-nighters — respect individual pace
- Make the demo session encouraging, not evaluative
- Organize an afterparty to close the event

### Community Sustainability
- Establish a long-term Discord/Telegram community group after the event
- Organize regular online or in-person meetups
- Encourage members to share project updates
- Foster a culture of mutual support
- Celebrate members' on-chain achievements
