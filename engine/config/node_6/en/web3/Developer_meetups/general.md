# Web3 Developer Meetup — Marketing Strategy

## Event Overview

A developer meetup is a lightweight community gathering designed for local developer ecosystems, typically featuring technical talks, project showcases, and networking sessions. The format is flexible and informal, making it an effective venue for building local developer communities and facilitating technical knowledge exchange.

## Core Objectives

- Build and strengthen the local developer community
- Facilitate peer-to-peer exchange among developers
- Share technical expertise and best practices
- Identify and engage local technical talent
- Foster a strong sense of community belonging

### Content Strategy

| Phase | Content Type | Posting Frequency |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Recruitment Phase | Event preview, session topics, venue introduction | Once every 2 days |
| Warm-up Phase | Speaker spotlights, agenda release | Once every 2 days |

### Content Tone
- Casual and welcoming
- Strong community voice

- Beginner-friendly

## Distribution Timeline

### T-1 Week: Launch Recruitment
- Publish event announcement
- Reveal session topics
- Open registration
- Community sharing push

### T-3 Days: Warm-up Phase
- Publish speaker spotlights
- Release full agenda
- Send registration reminders
- Share venue and transportation info

### T-Day: Event Day
- On-site check-in
- Live-post session highlights
- Encourage attendees to share photos
- Facilitate networking sessions

### T+1 Day: Recap Phase
- Publish event recap
- Compile and share session materials
- Collect attendee feedback

## Venue Selection Guidelines

### Venue Types
- Co-working spaces
- Project team offices
- Cafés / restaurants
- University facilities

### Selection Criteria
- Accessible transportation
- Appropriate capacity
- AV equipment (projector / speakers)
- Adequate space for networking

## Session Design

### Session Formats
- Technical Talk: 15–20 minute themed presentation
- Project Showcase: 10-minute project introduction
- Lightning Talk: 5-minute rapid-fire share
- Panel Discussion: Moderated topic conversation

### Recommended Agenda Structure
- Opening Remarks: 5 minutes
- Main Sessions: 1–2 talks, 40–60 minutes total
- Lightning Talks: 2–3 slots, 15–20 minutes total
- Open Networking: 30–60 minutes

## Newcomer-Friendly Practices

### Welcome Mechanisms
- Dedicated newcomer introduction segment
- Senior members take initiative to break the ice
- Prepare beginner onboarding materials
- Establish optional mentor pairing

### Content Design Considerations
- Include introductory-level content in each event
- Minimize use of technical jargon
- Actively encourage questions
- Provide post-event Q&A support
