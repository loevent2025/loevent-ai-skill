# WEB3 BUSINESS CONFERENCES × GENERAL

## Use Cases
Web3 industry summits, crypto conferences, blockchain ecosystem forums, annual Web3 showcases, comprehensive events such as Token2049 and Consensus, digital asset summits

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Keynotes + panel discussions + fireside chats + business networking + side events
- **Core Value:** Industry insights, ecosystem overview, commercialization trends, high-value connections

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Web3 Practitioners / Project Leads — staying on top of industry trends and ecosystem developments
- **Secondary Audience:** Enterprise Executives / Traditional Industry Decision-Makers — understanding Web3, evaluating entry opportunities
- **Tertiary Audience:** Web3 Product Managers / BD — understanding market needs, finding partners
- **Extended Audience:** Investors / Media / Analysts — discovering opportunities, reporting on industry developments
- **Audience Behavior:** Gaining insights, learning from case studies, building connections, finding collaboration, discovering alpha

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Industry-oriented:** Practitioners + investors — best for industry trends summits
- **Ecosystem-oriented:** Projects + builders + partners — best for ecosystem conferences

## Content Elements
- Summit theme and positioning
- Speaker lineup (projects + VCs + KOLs + traditional industry)
- Agenda highlights
- Ecosystem project showcase area
- Networking / after party / side events
- Registration details + ticket type overview

## Language Tone
- Conversational, genuine sharing
- Highlight speaker lineup and content quality
- Emphasize "actionable insights," "alpha," "conviction"
- Let case studies and on-chain data do the talking
- Moderate FOMO, avoid hard-sell language
- Cycle-aware framing: bull market focuses on opportunity, bear market focuses on building

## Content Structure
- Opening hook block: 15% — attention-grabbing headline
- Value proposition block: 25% — why this is worth attending
- Speaker and agenda block: 30% — speaker lineup + agenda highlights
- Attendance guide block: 20% — who it's for + what you'll get
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 10–14 total, professional but not dry

## Emotional Tone
Professional, high-caliber, opportunity-driven. The feeling of "top-tier minds in Web3, in the same room."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Industry Summit
- **Best for:** Large-scale comprehensive Web3 industry summits (Token2049 / Consensus, etc.)
- **Opener format:** 🔥 {Summit Name} | The Web3 industry's must-attend summit of the year is here
- **Tone:** High-caliber, authoritative, comprehensive

### Variant B: Ecosystem Conference
- **Best for:** L1 / protocol / platform ecosystem conferences
- **Opener format:** ⚡ {Conference Name} | The full ecosystem picture, all in one place
- **Tone:** Comprehensive, ecosystem perspective, builder-oriented

### Variant C: Annual Summit
- **Best for:** Annual industry showcases, awards ceremonies
- **Opener format:** ✨ {Summit Name} | The year's biggest Web3 gathering
- **Tone:** Grand, retrospective and forward-looking, a sense of occasion

## Attendance Tips

### Before the Event
- Research speakers' backgrounds and projects — prepare targeted questions
- Define your attendance goals and who you want to meet
- Prepare your introduction (30-second version, including project / role)
- Join the event's TG group or Discord in advance to warm up
- Check the side event schedule and register early

### On-Site Tips
- Sit up front — easier to engage and to be remembered
- Make the most of coffee breaks and after parties — put the phone down
- Take thorough notes, especially unique perspectives and data points
- Raise your hand during Q&A — it increases your visibility
- Side events often deliver more value than the main stage — budget your time wisely

### Post-Event Follow-Up
- Organize notes and TG contacts the same day
- Send follow-up TG messages within 24 hours
- Share your key takeaways on Twitter / social media, tagging relevant people and projects
- Schedule follow-up deep-dive conversations
- Keep tracking the projects and trends that came up at the summit

## Notes

### Key Principles for General Web3 Business Conferences
1. **The speaker lineup is the core selling point:** Be clear about who's coming, their background, and what projects they represent
2. **The agenda needs standout moments:** Curated highlights, not a flat schedule
3. **Emphasize networking value:** The Web3 space is small — relationships are everything
4. **Side events are a bonus:** After parties / closed-door sessions / smaller meetups are equally important
5. **Lower the decision barrier:** Be clear on who it's for, what they'll get, ticket types and pricing
6. **Authenticity matters:** Include testimonials from previous attendees and real on-site photos
7. **Think globally:** Web3 is a global industry — ensure international accessibility in language and framing
