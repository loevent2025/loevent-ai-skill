# Cocktail Party × Technical

## Applicable Scenarios
AI engineer social mixers, developer Chill Nights, tech people evening socials, hackathon after parties, open-source contributor gatherings, Tech People Social Mixers

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Relaxed socializing, no agenda, natural conversation, slightly buzzing atmosphere
- **Core Value:** Tech people socializing, meeting interesting people, unwinding

## Audience Profiles
- **Primary Audience:** Technical practitioners — engineers and developers looking to meet peers
- **Secondary Audience:** Tech enthusiasts — people with genuine interest in technical topics
- **Tertiary Audience:** Technical founders — people building interesting things
- **Audience Behavior:** Socializing, chatting, meeting people, decompressing

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, conversational, and youthful
- ✅ Direct, magnetic, rhythmically written
- ✅ Short sentences and fragmented, scannable information
- ✅ Purely positive — showcase the appeal, don't explain it
- ✅ Scene-first — make people want to be there

### Strictly Prohibited
- ❌ Contrast phrasing ("tired of X? Come here instead," "not X but Y")
- ❌ Never negate or criticize any other type of event or occasion
- ❌ Transactional terms: "takeaways," "learning," "self-improvement," "networking," "resources"
- ❌ Do not write it like a tech talk or knowledge-sharing session

---

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Scene-First Opening (Required)
**Formula:** [What makes this venue special] + [Sensory experience] + [Vibe words]

**Examples:**
- "Rooftop. Craft beer. The kind of easy you almost forget exists when you're writing code."
- "Industrial loft, warm light, engineers holding drinks and actually talking."
- "Terrace, a slight buzz, a night where tech people aren't talking about bugs for once."

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (At Least 3 Required)
- **Visual:** Warm ambient light, city lights at night, industrial space, floor-to-ceiling windows
- **Taste:** Craft beer, whisky, handcrafted cocktails, small bites
- **Sound:** Chill beats, low conversation, the occasional burst of laughter
- **Touch:** Sea breeze, wood bar surface, the weight of a glass
- **Atmosphere:** Relaxed, slightly buzzing, chill, the vibe is just right

### 3. Time Flow Narrative (Recommended)
Use a fragmented timeline to build anticipation:

**Example:**
```
7:00 PM — First arrivals at the bar, drinks in hand, easy chat
8:00 PM — Room's starting to fill. Vibe is just right.
9:00 PM — Deep into several different conversations now
10:00 PM — The ones still here are the ones who clicked
```

### 4. Social Snapshot (Required)
Describe what's happening — not what people "can" do:

- ❌ "Participants can freely exchange on technical topics"
- ✅ "Someone's talking about a side project they've been tinkering with. Someone else is venting about being on-call. Someone's just zoning out, not thinking about anything at all."

### 5. Venue-Specific Experience (Required)
What can only be experienced here:

- Rooftop bar: "Drinking on the rooftop with a group of tech people — turns out there's a lot to talk about beyond code"
- Industrial loft: "Concrete walls, warm light — engineers looking unusually chill"
- Craft beer bar: "Craft beer + tech talk — the vibe is exactly right"

### 6. Describing the Crowd (Recommended)
By vibe, not by title:

- ❌ "AI engineers, algorithm specialists, technical directors"
- ✅ "A group of people doing interesting things in AI"
- ✅ "Engineers, infra people, open-source contributors, and the purely curious"

### 7. Low-Pressure Invitation (Required)
- ✅ "Come if you can — no pressure if you can't"
- ✅ "No agenda, no talks, just social"
- ✅ "Stay as long as you like"

🍺 [Venue + sensory lead]

[Location — something cool and specific about it]
[Crowd size — casual]

### Scene & Atmosphere (80–120 words)
[Five-sense description]
[Time flow]
[Social snapshot]

### Who's Coming (40–60 words)
[Vibe description — no titles]

### What Will Happen (40–60 words)
[Pure experience description]

### Practical Info + Registration (40–50 words)
[Casual, low-pressure CTA]

### Closing (20–30 words)
[Simple, inviting, no FOMO]

## Notes

### Technical Cocktail Party Principles
1. **Scene-first:** Lead with the venue and vibe — not the purpose of the event
2. **De-transactionalize it:** Not here to "learn" or "network" — here to chill
3. **Tech talk happens naturally:** It'll come up, but it's not the point
4. **Low pressure:** Come or don't — no anxiety created
5. **Describe people by vibe, not credential:** Say "interesting" — not what they do
6. **Short sentence rhythm:** Fragmented, scannable, with pace

### Prohibited
- ❌ Do not write it like a tech talk or knowledge-sharing session
- ❌ Do not emphasize "technical exchange" or "knowledge sharing"
- ❌ Do not use "high-quality technical networking"
- ❌ Do not create FOMO ("limited spots," "don't miss out")
