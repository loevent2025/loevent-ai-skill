# PIXEL ROCK / Pixel Rock Style

---

## Use Cases

Tech company recruitment events, developer parties, hackathons, programmer celebrations, gaming company promotions, tech community meetups, code-themed music festivals, geek culture events, product launch parties, creative recruitment posters, tech brand youth marketing, Developer Day activations

---

## Color Palette

**Primary Colors:** Pure white, orange, black

**Background:** Deep black, dark gray

**Accent Colors:** Orange-red, cyan, rainbow gradient halos

**Highlight Accents:** Orange cross symbols, cyan code text

**Saturation:** High saturation for accent colors; low saturation for backgrounds to create contrast

**Color Behavior:** Solid fills for primary text elements; photographic image overlays and halo effects permitted for backgrounds

---

## Recommended Color Combinations

- **Classic Rock:** Pure black (#000000) + pure white (#FFFFFF) + orange-red (#FF6B35) + cyan (#00FFFF)

- **Neon Party:** Deep black (#0A0A0A) + white (#FFFFFF) + hot orange (#FF4500) + rainbow gradient halo

- **Geek Rave:** Charcoal black (#1A1A1A) + pixel white (#FFFFFF) + fluorescent orange (#FF5722) + electric blue (#00D4FF)

- **Code Night:** Pure black (#000000) + bright white (#FFFFFF) + amber orange (#FFBF00) + mint green (#00FFA3)

## Visual Element Library (Select 2–3 per composition)

### Pixel Typography
- **Pixel headline** — 8-bit style pixel lettering as the primary visual focal point
- **Pixel Latin text** — Retro game-style English typeface
- **Pixel CJK text** — Dot-matrix style Chinese/Japanese characters
- **Code decorative text** — Programmatic snippets such as `while{true}`, `Rock();`

### Photographic Backgrounds
- **Live concert scene** — Silhouettes or halftone imagery of band performances or instrument play
- **Music equipment** — Rock elements including drum kits, guitars, and amplifiers
- **Human silhouettes** — Blurred outlines of performers or audience members
- **Stage lighting** — Abstract representation of live performance lighting effects

### Light & Glow Effects
- **Rainbow halo** — Rainbow-colored light arcs or orbs positioned in corners
- **Lens flare** — Multi-color light spots simulating camera lens effects
- **Noise texture** — Grainy film noise overlaid across the composition
- **Halftone dots** — Retro print-style dot screen effect

### Geometric Accents
- **Cross symbols** — "+" symbols in orange or other accent colors scattered across the layout
- **Pixel blocks** — Small solid-color square decorations
- **Arrow symbols** — Pixel-style directional arrows: `<` and `>`
- **Information panels** — Dark rectangular blocks framing dates or event details

---

## Element Selection Guidelines

- **Restrained pixel type:** Pixel-style headlines occupy 25–35% of the central composition — legible but not overpowering

- **Photography as atmosphere:** Background photographic imagery carries greater visual weight, serving as the primary mood-setting element

- **Light effects as accents:** Rainbow halos placed in corners; supportive rather than dominant, adding energy without competing with key content

- **Scattered symbols:** Geometric accents such as cross symbols distributed across the layout for compositional balance

- **Clear layering:** Foreground text → midground decorations → background imagery — distinct depth hierarchy maintained throughout

---

## Visual Treatment

### Pixel Typography Treatment
- **Solid white fill:** Primary headlines use pure white (#FFFFFF) with no added effects
- **Pixel aliasing:** Preserve the jagged edges of pixel fonts to maintain authentic 8-bit texture
- **Moderate weight:** Letterforms remain legible and clear without being excessively heavy
- **No shadows:** No drop shadows or 3D effects applied to text
- **Restrained sizing:** Text sized proportionally, leaving generous breathing room around the background

### Background Image Treatment
- **Unified duotone:** Photographic images converted to a single color tone (e.g., orange-red tone, cyan tone)
- **Halftone overlay:** Optional retro print-style halftone dot screen for vintage texture
- **Noise grain:** Light noise overlay added for tactile, raw quality
- **Moderate opacity:** Background image opacity set to 50–65% for controlled visibility
- **Edge fade:** Image edges transition softly into the black background with no hard borders

### Halo Effect Treatment
- **Corner placement:** Rainbow halos positioned in corners (e.g., upper left)
- **Soft diffusion:** Halo edges feathered naturally into the black background
- **Full spectrum:** Rainbow halos include multi-color gradients (red, orange, yellow, green, cyan, blue, violet)
- **Non-obstructive:** Halo placement avoids overlapping primary text zones

### Decorative Symbol Treatment
- **Solid fills:** Cross symbols and similar accents use flat solid color fills
- **Organic scatter:** Symbols distributed across the composition — seemingly random in placement yet visually balanced overall
- **Size variation:** Symbols may vary in scale to add depth and layering
- **Effect-free:** No glow or shadow applied to symbols

---

## Composition Strategy

- **Centered headline, moderate scale:** Pixel headline centered and visually prominent, but proportionally restrained

- **Top-to-bottom structure:** Brand logo at the top; primary headline in the upper-center; event details in the lower-center

- **Expanded photographic presence:** Background photography occupies greater visual area to strengthen atmospheric impact

- **Corner light effects:** Rainbow halos and similar glow effects confined to corner regions

- **Distributed balance:** Accent elements such as cross symbols scattered to balance the overall visual weight

- **Information panels:** Key details such as dates enclosed in dark rectangular blocks for emphasis

- **Generous white space:** Sufficient breathing room maintained around all text elements

- **Flat frontal perspective:** Straight-on, first-person viewpoint with no perspective distortion

---

## Typography Settings

**Fill Style:** Solid fill, single flat color, no gradients

**Prohibited Effects:**
- No outer glow or inner glow
- No halo effects
- No outlines or strokes
- No drop shadows or cast shadows
- No gradient fills
- No rim lighting
- No text effects of any kind

**Primary Headline:** Medium-to-large pixel typeface, pure white, 8-bit retro style — proportionally sized, not excessively dominant

**Subheadline / Tagline:** Small-to-medium pixel typeface or standard sans-serif, white or accent color

**Code Decorations:** Small monospace typeface, cyan or white, mimicking code snippet visuals

**Event Information:** Small typeface, dates and venue details enclosed within dark color panels

**Overall Principle:** All text remains clean, sharp, and highly legible. Sizing is restrained to avoid overwhelming the composition. Pixel typefaces convey retro gaming aesthetics; typographic hierarchy is established through font selection and size contrast, communicating a youthful, energetic, and geek-culture brand tone

---

## Mood & Atmosphere

High-energy rock, geek celebration, retro gaming, youthful vitality, code culture. The overall atmosphere is passionate, unconventional, and directed at the next generation of developers — blending pixel art with rock culture to communicate that a tech brand is young, fun, and creative. The result is a cultural identity built around the idea that code can be cool.
