## 📝 Required Content Elements

### 1. Contrast & Tension Building (required)

**Formula**:
```
"What happens when [an identity representing World X] meets [a skill/domain representing World Y]?"
```

**Key points**:
- Clearly state the identity contrast between both sides
- Use parallel structures: "X represents..., Y represents..."
- Emphasize the unexpected and novel nature of this combination

---

### 2. Chemical Reaction Description (required)

**Dynamic verbs**:
- ✅ Use: "collide," "fuse," "catalyze," "spark," "reshape"
- ❌ Avoid: "cooperate," "combine," "partner"

**Fusion formula**:
```
[Traditional strength] × [Web3 capability] = [Unique output]
```

---

### 3. Participant Collision Description (required)

**Describe who meets whom**:
```
"Here, [Background A] wants to explore [World B], and [Background B] wants to gain [Capability A]"
```

**Key points**:
- Emphasize the complementary nature of learners from different backgrounds
- Describe the two-way value exchange
- Convey the energy of mixed-background team formation

---

### 4. Scene & Atmosphere Building (select at least 2)

**a) Concrete scenes**
- Don't write: "The event will be held at XX"
- Do write: Specific descriptions of cross-disciplinary exchanges happening in the space
- Apply five senses: visual, auditory, tactile, atmospheric

**b) Passage of time**
- Describe what happens at specific moments throughout the learning journey
- Show the timeline from confusion to breakthrough

**c) Dynamic interactions**
- Don't write: "Participants can network freely"
- Do write: Specific scenes of learners from different backgrounds interacting

**d) Emotional energy**
- Bootcamp: transformation, breakthrough, rebirth, crossing over
- Hackathon: sprint, all-nighters, Eureka moments, pushing limits
- Demo Day: in full bloom, witnessing, recognition, launching off

---

### 5. Unique Outcome Imagination (required)

**Describe outcomes that only cross-disciplinary work can produce**:
- List deliverables that only this specific combination of backgrounds can achieve
- Avoid generic statements — provide clear, tangible application scenarios
- Emphasize the irreplaceability of cross-disciplinary fusion

---

### 6. Transformation Arc (recommended)

**Learner journey description**:
- **Beginning**: Arrive with [specific questions / curiosity]
- **Process**: Meet [certain people], collide with [certain ideas], break through [certain assumptions]
- **End**: Leave with [answers / skills / friends / a project]

## ✍️ Hook Writing Templates

### Version A – Collision & Tension
- Open with "What happens when X meets Y?"
- Emphasize identity contrast and mental model collision
- Best for: Web2 → Web3 transition bootcamps

### Version B – Formal & Professional
- Open with joint institutional endorsement
- Emphasize program objectives and curriculum framework
- Best for: University joint programs, industry-academia projects

### Version C – High-Energy Sprint
- Open with time constraints and extreme challenge
- Emphasize cross-background competition and collaboration
- Best for: Cross-disciplinary Hackathons

### Version D – Value-Oriented
- Open with two-way value for learners and enterprises
- Emphasize the market scarcity of cross-disciplinary talent
- Best for: Enterprise training, employment-oriented programs

## ⚠️ Prohibited Actions

- ❌ Disparaging either background ("Web2 is outdated" / "Web3 is unreliable")
- ❌ Over-promising transition outcomes ("Guaranteed salary doubling")
- ❌ Ignoring cross-disciplinary fusion value and writing generic training copy
- ❌ Replacing collision energy with passive verbs like "cooperate" or "combine"
- ❌ Treating cross-disciplinary learners as beginners while ignoring their existing strengths
- ❌ Over-hyping Web3 opportunities without acknowledging risks
