# Web3 Hackathon Knowledge Base – Investment Track

## Overview
Investment-focused Web3 hackathons center on startup project incubation and investor matchmaking. Participants are expected not only to deliver a working technical build, but to demonstrate clear commercial value and market potential. These events serve as a key channel for Web3 founders to secure funding, validate business models, and connect with investors and ecosystem funds — while also offering investment institutions a highly efficient way to identify promising early-stage on-chain projects. The unique dynamics of token economics and community-driven models in Web3 open up additional fundraising pathways that traditional hackathons cannot offer.

---

## Event Profile
- **Scale**: Tens to hundreds of participants; boutique format preferred
- **Duration**: Typically 48–72 hours of development + pitch day + post-event incubation support
- **Format**: Team building + business coaching + investor pitch + incubation matching
- **Participants**: Web3 founders, technical developers, product managers, investors (crypto VCs / ecosystem funds), incubators
- **Defining characteristics**: Strong commercial orientation, rich resource connections, significant post-event opportunities

## Participation Tips

### Business Preparation
- Define clearly: what on-chain or off-chain problem are you solving, and for whom?
- Research and understand the size of your target market and relevant on-chain data
- Study competing protocols and articulate your differentiation
- Design your tokenomics and token allocation structure before arriving
- Think through your customer acquisition, TVL growth, and community cold-start strategy

### Pitch Preparation
- Prepare both a 3-minute and 5-minute version of your pitch
- Structure: Pain point → Solution → Tokenomics → Market → Team → Funding ask
- Use on-chain data to make your case — avoid vague, unsubstantiated claims
- Prepare answers to common questions (e.g., how to prevent token sell pressure)
- Practice until your delivery feels natural and confident

### Investor Conversations
- Research the investment preferences and focus verticals of attending VCs and ecosystem funds
- Bring pitch decks and whitepapers at different levels of detail
- Know your baseline valuation and token warrant thinking
- Answer questions honestly — don't exaggerate TVL or user metrics
- Follow up proactively and agree on clear next steps

### Team Composition
- Smart contract development + frontend + business/operations is a strong combination
- Domain expertise in the target vertical is a significant differentiator
- Strong pitch communication skills matter
- Execution ability is valued more than ideas alone
- Community management experience is a meaningful plus
