# Café × General (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Themed café gatherings, interest group coffee hours, weekend cowork cafés, remote workers café, freelancer café days, themed pop-up cafés

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Café setting, casual gathering, cowork or chat, drop-in friendly
- **Core Value**: A change of scenery for work or socializing, meeting like-minded people, the comfort of a café

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: People who want a change of scenery—remote workers tired of working from home
- **Secondary Audience**: People looking for like-minded company—interested in a topic and wanting someone to chat with
- **Tertiary Audience**: Café lovers—people who enjoy the café atmosphere
- **Audience Behavior**: Coworking, drinking coffee, chatting, meeting people

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, chill, comfortable
- ✅ The warmth of a café
- ✅ Low barrier, low pressure
- ✅ Scene first

### Strictly Prohibited
- ❌ Don't be overly formal
- ❌ Don't emphasize "value" or "takeaways"
- ❌ Don't include an agenda or structured segments
- ❌ Avoid utilitarian descriptions

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Café Scene Opening (Required)
**Formula**: [Café atmosphere] + [What you'll be doing] + [Relaxed feel]

**Examples**:
- "Find a café. Work alongside a group of people"
- "Saturday afternoon. A café. Talk about whatever comes up"
- "Working from home is getting stale—time for a change of scenery"

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (Include at Least 3)
- **Sight**: Warm lighting, wooden tables, green plants, laptops open
- **Taste**: Pour-over, latte, pastries, brunch
- **Sound**: Background music, the hum of the espresso machine, soft conversation
- **Touch**: A comfortable chair, the warmth of a coffee cup
- **Atmosphere**: Comfortable, chill, focused yet relaxed

### 3. Flow-of-Time Description (Recommended)
**Example**:
```
2:00 PM — Arrive and grab a coffee
3:00 PM — Some people are working, some are chatting
4:00 PM — You've probably joined a conversation by now
5:00 PM — Some head out, some stay on
```

### 4. Flexible Participation (Required)
- "Stay the whole day, or just pop in for a quick coffee"
- "Feel like working? Work. Feel like chatting? Chat"
- "No agenda. Just show up whenever"

### 5. Venue Description (Recommended)
- What makes the café special (great pour-over, good pastries, plenty of seating)
- Environment details (quiet, good for working, has a terrace)
- Practical info (power outlets available, solid wifi)

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Remote Workers Café
- **Use For**: Coworking sessions for remote workers
- **Opening Format**: ☕💻 Remote Café | A change of scenery for work
- **Tone**: Companionable, focused, chill

### Variant B: Themed Chat Café
- **Use For**: Casual gatherings around a specific topic
- **Opening Format**: ☕💬 [Topic] Café | Coffee and conversation
- **Tone**: Relaxed, sharing, like-minded

### Variant C: Weekend Café Hangout
- **Use For**: Casual weekend gatherings
- **Opening Format**: ☕✨ Weekend Coffee Hour | No agenda, just hanging out
- **Tone**: Leisurely, zero pressure, weekend vibes

---

## Key Considerations

### Café Best Practices
1. **Café atmosphere is everything**: Scene first
2. **Low barrier, low pressure**: No sign-up needed, come and go anytime
3. **Flexible participation**: Cowork, chat, or just zone out
4. **Practical info matters**: Café highlights, power outlets, wifi situation
5. **No agenda**: This is not a meetup

### Prohibited
- ❌ Don't write it like an event or meetup
- ❌ Don't include structured segments
- ❌ Don't be overly formal
- ❌ Don't emphasize "takeaways"
