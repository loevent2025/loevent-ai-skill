# WEB3 BUSINESS CONFERENCES × EDUCATION

## Use Cases
Web3 introductory conferences, blockchain application training, enterprise Web3 training summits, Web3 career development forums, on-chain operations workshops, executive Web3 literacy programs

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Knowledge sharing + case study walkthroughs + on-chain hands-on practice + experience exchange
- **Core Value:** Knowledge building, on-chain skills, career development, Web3 onboarding

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Web3 Newcomers / Web2 Career Switchers — getting started, building foundational knowledge, finally understanding what Web3 actually is
- **Secondary Audience:** Traditional Industry Professionals — understanding what Web3 can do and how to integrate it
- **Tertiary Audience:** Enterprise Managers — understanding Web3's strategic value, making informed decisions
- **Extended Audience:** University and Graduate Students — career planning, getting ahead on Web3
- **Audience Behavior:** Learning new knowledge, mastering on-chain operations, career planning, building awareness

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Beginner-oriented:** Newcomers + traditional professionals — best for foundational and introductory events
- **Executive-oriented:** Managers + decision-makers — best for strategic Web3 literacy programs

## Content Elements
- Learning theme and positioning (Web3 landscape / DeFi / NFT / L1s, etc.)
- Speaker / instructor lineup (OGs + hands-on practitioners)
- Curriculum and agenda
- On-chain hands-on segment (wallet creation / Swap / Mint, etc.)
- Tool and resource recommendations
- Post-event learning pathways + community entry points

## Language Tone
- Encouraging and guiding
- Lower the perceived barrier to learning
- Emphasize "beginner-friendly," "no prerequisites," "hands-on on-chain," "immediately applicable"
- Case-study driven — no jargon stacking
- Genuine, accessible tone

## Content Structure
- Opening hook block: 15% — what problem does this solve?
- Learning content block: 35% — what you'll learn
- Instructor lineup block: 20% — why learn from them?
- Participation value block: 20% — who it's for + what you'll gain
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 12–16 total, warm and encouraging

## Emotional Tone
Learning, growth, career progress. The feeling of "not falling behind in the Web3 era."

## Attendance Tips

### Before the Event
- Be clear about the one problem you most want to solve
- Write down questions you want to ask
- If there's an on-chain hands-on segment, download a wallet in advance (MetaMask / OKX Wallet, etc.)
- Prepare a small amount of testnet gas or a small amount of mainnet funds
- Bring your laptop and charger

### On-Site Tips
- Take careful notes — especially step-by-step instructions and contract addresses
- Follow along with the hands-on portions; don't just watch — do it yourself
- Ask questions freely — the Web3 community is welcoming; no question is too basic
- Connect with instructors and fellow participants on TG / WeChat
- Stay vigilant about wallet security — never connect to unfamiliar links

### Post-Event Follow-Up
- Organize your notes and write a learning summary (post on Twitter / social media)
- Keep practicing on-chain
- Join the recommended learning communities and keep growing
- Share what you learned — teaching is the best way to learn

## Notes

### Key Principles for Education Web3 Business Conferences
1. **Lower the perceived barrier:** Make newcomers feel "I can actually get into Web3"
2. **Prioritize on-chain hands-on practice:** Doing beats listening every time — especially for on-chain operations
3. **Provide tools and resources:** Give participants something they can take away and use (wallet, dApp list, learning materials)
4. **Speak plainly:** Don't intimidate people with terms like hash, nonce, or Merkle tree
5. **Wallet security is non-negotiable:** Always cover wallet safety, phishing prevention, and private key management
6. **Provide a clear next step:** Show participants how to keep going after the event — recommend communities and learning resources
