# Web3 · Large-Scale Exhibition / Expo Knowledge Base

## Section 1: Scenario Definition and Characteristics

### Scenario Overview
Large-scale exhibitions and expos are the most impactful event format in the Web3 sector, typically bringing together global project teams, investment institutions, media, and community participants. They serve as the core arena for ecosystem showcasing, brand visibility, and business matchmaking.

### Core Characteristics
| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| Scale | 800–5,000+ attendees; multiple exhibition zones and parallel programming tracks |
| Duration | 2–5 days; includes a main forum, breakout tracks, exhibition floor, and social events |
| Format | Booth showcases + keynote presentations + panel discussions + business matchmaking + networking receptions |
| Positioning | Industry bellwether, ecosystem showcase platform, annual flagship event |

### Landmark Events
- Token2049 (Singapore / Dubai)
- Consensus (USA / Hong Kong)
- ETHDenver (Denver)
- Korea Blockchain Week (Seoul)
- Hong Kong Web3 Festival (Hong Kong)

## Section 5: Key Success Factors

### Planning Phase
- **Speaker lineup**: Founders of leading projects, prominent VC partners, industry thought leaders
- **Exhibitor recruitment**: Combination of established projects and high-potential rising stars; coverage across mainstream tracks
- **Media partnerships**: Global mainstream media network, regional KOL network
- **Venue selection**: Convenient transport links, comprehensive facilities, international conference-grade capacity

### Execution Phase
- **Agenda design**: Main forum + vertical breakout tracks + workshops + social events
- **Traffic flow planning**: Logical distribution across exhibition zones, conference areas, and networking areas
- **Interactive mechanisms**: On-chain check-in, POAP collection, social matching tools
- **Content production**: Live streaming, highlight reels, real-time text and image updates

### Distribution Strategy
- **Pre-event**: Speaker announcements, agenda releases, early-bird perks
- **During the event**: Real-time updates, KOL coordination, trending topic management
- **Long-tail**: In-depth retrospectives, highlight videos, case study development

## Section 6: Key Considerations

### Compliance
- Understand the regulatory environment of the host location (differences across Hong Kong, Singapore, Dubai, etc.)
- Avoid sensitive language around token sales, investment advice, and similar topics
- Ensure speaker background screening and content compliance controls are in place

### Operational Risks
- Over-commercialization leading to community reputation damage
- Contingency plans for last-minute speaker cancellations and agenda changes
- Crowd management and security arrangements

### Cultural Alignment
- Respect the decentralization ethos; avoid overly centralized operational approaches
- Balance commercial objectives with community values
- Prioritize the Builder community experience; do not skew purely toward business development
