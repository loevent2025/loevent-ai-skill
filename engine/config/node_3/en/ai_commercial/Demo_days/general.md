## Selection Criteria

---

### Criterion 1: Stage Presentation & Visual Quality

**Definition:** Whether the venue can support professional-grade product demonstrations with strong visual delivery. Demo Day is fundamentally a "credibility showcase" — stage quality directly influences how professional the event is perceived.

**Assessment Areas:**

* Standard stage area available (≥6m × 4m)
* LED screen resolution (P2.5 or higher recommended, 16:9 aspect ratio, 4K preferred)
* Screen visibility (clear sightlines from the back rows)
* Dual-screen / confidence monitor support (speaker teleprompter, timer)
* Lighting designed for "clear visibility" rather than entertainment effects
* Even audio coverage with no echo
* Pre-assigned camera positions (for media or livestream)

> The visual priority for Demo Day is not "flashy" — it's "professional, reliable, and clear."

---

### Criterion 2: Network & Technical Infrastructure

**Definition:** Whether the venue can provide the stable network and power conditions required for live AI demonstrations. Network reliability is the lifeline of any AI event.

**Assessment Areas:**

* Dedicated wired network connection at the stage (100 Mbps+)
* Manageable API call latency
* Isolated demo network (separate from attendee Wi-Fi)
* Sufficient upload bandwidth for livestreaming (50 Mbps+)
* Permission to bring external network equipment
* 4G/5G backup connectivity available
* Adequate power supply (for high-wattage equipment)
* Permission to conduct network stress testing in advance

> Unstable network = unreliable product.
> This is a hard requirement — failure to meet it is an automatic disqualification.

---

### Criterion 3: Demo Experience Zone Support

**Definition:** Whether the venue supports setting up a hands-on demo area where attendees can try products on-site. Demo Day is not just about watching — it's about experiencing.

**Assessment Areas:**

* Dedicated space available for a demo area
* Independent wired network per booth
* Sufficient power outlets (4+ per booth)
* Support for standard booth setups (2m × 2m or 3m × 3m)
* Relatively quiet zones for voice-based products
* Queue flow management capability
* Support for installing interactive large-screen displays

---

### Criterion 4: Spatial Layout & Traffic Flow Design

**Definition:** Whether the spatial layout supports a three-phase attendee journey: "focused viewing → distributed exploration → in-depth networking."

**Assessment Areas:**

* Balanced ratio between main stage and audience area (approx. 35% stage, 40% audience)
* Unobstructed sightlines for all attendees
* Ability to open up the space for networking after presentations
* Demo area does not interfere with the main stage
* Natural traffic flow throughout the venue
* Dedicated private meeting spaces available
* Registration area does not block the entrance

---

### Criterion 5: Business Conversion Support

**Definition:** Whether the venue supports the core objective of "deal-making and business development."

**Assessment Areas:**

* Private meeting rooms available
* Designated 1-on-1 meeting area
* Easy to arrange post-event follow-up meetings
* VIP reception area available
* Quiet negotiation spaces available
* Support for follow-up executive sessions

> The success metric for Demo Day is not applause — it's conversion rate.

---

### Criterion 6: Brand Presentation & Media Reach

**Definition:** Whether the venue supports brand visual identity and media distribution.

**Assessment Areas:**

* Permission for branded backdrop installation
* Ability to project brand logos on-screen
* Natural camera-friendly angles available
* Lighting conditions suitable for photography and video
* Permission for on-site livestreaming
* Permission for social media coverage
* Venue's own aesthetic does not clash with brand identity

---

### Criterion 7: Capacity & Comfort

**Definition:** Whether the venue can maintain a comfortable viewing and networking experience at the target headcount.

**Assessment Areas:**

* Adequate per-person floor space in theater-style seating
* Demo area does not become overcrowded
* Sufficient HVAC and ventilation
* Acoustic environment avoids echo
* Adequate restroom count (approx. 1 per 50 attendees)
* Buffer capacity for unexpected attendance fluctuations

---

### Criterion 8: Scheduling Flexibility & Setup Support

**Definition:** Whether the venue accommodates advance setup and flexible overtime.

**Assessment Areas:**

* Permission for setup 3–5 hours in advance
* Permission for a rehearsal the day before
* Overtime extensions allowed
* Reasonable teardown window
* Support for loading large equipment on-site
* Freight elevator or accessible loading access available

---

### Criterion 9: Safety & Compliance

**Definition:** Whether the venue meets safety requirements for a public roadshow event.

**Assessment Areas:**

* Valid business and operating licenses
* Fire safety compliance
* Security support available
* Livestreaming and recording compliance
* Authorization required from presenting teams for video distribution
* Additional review requirements for sensitive industry demos

---

## Default Priority Ranking

> The following is the standard priority ranking for AI commercialization Demo Days. The model should dynamically adjust based on specific event objectives.

### Hard Requirements (Failure to meet = automatic disqualification)

1. Network & Technical Infrastructure
2. Stage Presentation & Visual Quality
3. Capacity & Comfort
4. Demo Experience Zone Support

### Semi-Hard Requirements (Upgraded to hard requirements for fundraising / B2B scenarios)

5. Business Conversion Support
6. Spatial Layout & Traffic Flow Design

### Nice-to-Haves

7. Brand Presentation & Media Reach
8. Scheduling Flexibility
9. Transportation Accessibility

## Hybrid Scenario Guidelines

When Demo Day is combined with other event formats:

### 1️⃣ Demo Day + Reception

* Assess whether the space supports a "theater-style → free-flowing social" transition
* Evaluate whether lighting can switch from focused mode to ambient mode
* Confirm furniture can be cleared quickly
* Check whether the demo area can be converted into a networking zone

### 2️⃣ Demo Day + Summit

* Prioritize the same venue or an adjacent venue
* Evaluate turnaround time between sessions
* Assess whether parallel session spaces are sufficient
* Verify whether the media area is independent
