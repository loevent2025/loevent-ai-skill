# Web3 Cafe Cursor – Education Event Knowledge Base

## 🎯 Scenario Overview

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Format | Cafe setting + Self-study / Cowork + Ask anytime + Peer learning |
| Duration | 3–5 hours, flexible time slots, come and go freely |
| Participants | Learners, mentors / teaching assistants, tech enthusiasts |
| Core Objective | Learning atmosphere, peer companionship, on-demand Q&A, easy onboarding |

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style Override

When a Cafe Cursor education event is detected:

✅ Relaxed, inclusive, zero-pressure learning atmosphere
✅ "Sip coffee while you learn" vibe
✅ Ask questions anytime — no question is too basic
✅ Peer learning, helping each other out
✅ Flexible timing, go at your own pace

❌ Do NOT write it as a formal training course or lecture
❌ Do NOT use pressure language like "must master" or "you'll know it all by the end"
❌ Do NOT create learning anxiety
❌ Do NOT set skill-level entry barriers

**Core Principle**:
🎯 This is a "bring your laptop, come learn at a cafe" kind of space — mentors are on standby for questions, peers are here to grow together, coffee is the companion, and learning happens naturally

---

## 📝 Required Content Elements

### 1. Learning Atmosphere Opening (required)

Open with the "coffee + learn together" vibe:

**Formula**:
```
[Cafe setting] + [Learning together] + [Someone's here to help you]
```

**Key points**:
- ❌ Don't write: "Web3 development training"
- ✅ Do write: "Bring your laptop and come learn Web3 at a cafe"
- Make it feel like a relaxed learning space

---

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (include at least 3)

**Visual**:
- Space: "Sunlight pouring into the cafe," "long tables covered with laptops and notebooks," "someone reading docs"
- Learning elements: "Tutorials on screens," "someone coding along," "a flowchart drawn on the whiteboard"

**Taste**:
- Be specific: "pour-over coffee," "latte," "hot cocoa"
- ❌ Avoid: "beverages provided"
- ✅ Write: "Coffee's on tap — grab a cup when you need a break"

**Sound**:
- Music: "lo-fi beats," "soft background music"
- Ambient: "keyboard clicks," "someone occasionally asking 'how does this part work?'"

**Touch**:
- Temperature: "The cafe AC is set just right"
- Connection: "Getting stuck on the same thing as the person next to you — then figuring it out together"

**Atmosphere**:
- Energy level: "Focused but not intense," "the comfort of learning alongside others," "some people deep in concentration, others in discussion"
- Social dynamics: "Someone in headphones self-studying, someone else asking the person beside them a question"

---

### 3. Flexible Time Framework (required)

**Format**:
```
⏰ Pick a time slot that works for you:
- [Slot A] Morning session
- [Slot B] Afternoon session
- Stay as long as you like
```

**Key points**:
- Emphasize "come and go freely"
- Learn at your own pace
- No mandatory time commitment

---

### 4. Mentor / Q&A Support (recommended)

Having someone to answer your questions is the core value of Cafe learning:

**Key points**:
- ✅ "Mentors are on-site — ask anytime"
- ✅ "Don't worry about asking basic questions — everyone started as a beginner"
- ✅ "Stuck on something? Just ask the person next to you — everyone's happy to help"

---

### 5. Peer Learning Atmosphere (recommended)

Learning together beats learning alone:

**Key points**:
- ✅ "Learning with a group is way more productive than studying at home alone"
- ✅ "Someone's learning Solidity, someone's reading docs, someone's writing their first contract"
- ✅ "Everyone's at a different stage — that's fine. Learn your own thing, help each other when questions come up"

---

### 6. Bring Your Laptop (required)

Learning requires hands-on practice:

**Key points**:
- ✅ "Bring your laptop"
- ✅ "Pick up where you left off, or start from scratch"
- ✅ "No laptop? Come listen in on what others are working on — that's cool too"

---

### 7. Zero-Pressure Learning Framework (required)

Make learners feel at ease:

**Key points**:
- ✅ "Go at your own pace"
- ✅ "No tasks to complete"
- ✅ "Just coming to soak in the learning atmosphere is totally fine"

- ❌ "Must master"
- ❌ "You'll be able to... after this"
- ❌ "Fall behind if you don't learn"

---

### 8. Beginner-Friendly (required)

Make even zero-experience people feel welcome:

**Key points**:
- ✅ "Absolute beginners are welcome"
- ✅ "No matter where you are in your learning journey, you're welcome here"
- ✅ "Ask anything, anytime — there are no stupid questions"

## ❓ FAQ Templates

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Can I come with zero experience? | Of course! Just come soak in the learning atmosphere |
| Do I have to bring a laptop? | Recommended, but you're welcome to come and listen in without one |

## ✅ Cafe Cursor Education Event Quality Checklist

- [ ] **Learning atmosphere**: Opening conveys the "learn together" vibe
- [ ] **Bring your laptop**: Prompt attendees to bring their laptops
- [ ] **Flexible timing**: Emphasize come-and-go freedom, learn at your own pace
- [ ] **Mentor support**: Someone is available for questions
- [ ] **Peer learning**: Sense of companionship in learning together
- [ ] **Beginner-friendly**: Absolute beginners are welcome
- [ ] **Zero-pressure**: No learning tasks or goals imposed
- [ ] **Five-sense description**: At least 3 sensory details included

---

## ⚠️ Prohibited Actions

- ❌ Writing Cafe Cursor as a formal training course
- ❌ Assigning learning tasks or assessments
- ❌ Using pressure language like "must master" or "fall behind if you don't learn"
- ❌ Creating learning anxiety
- ❌ Setting technical skill barriers for entry
- ❌ Enforcing mandatory attendance duration
