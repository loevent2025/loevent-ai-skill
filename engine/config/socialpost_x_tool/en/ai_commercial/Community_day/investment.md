# Café × Investment (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Investor Cowork Café, Founder Coffee Hours, VC Office Hours @ Café, Portfolio Café Day, Early-Stage Project Café Meetups

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Relaxed café setting, informal conversation, chat over coffee, drop-in style
- **Core Value**: Low-key exchanges in a casual atmosphere, informal introductions, reducing the pressure of networking

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: Early-stage founders — Founders looking to casually meet investors
- **Secondary Audience**: Investors — VCs/Angels who want to review projects in a relaxed setting
- **Tertiary Audience**: Startup ecosystem adjacent — Financial advisors, incubators, anyone curious about early-stage projects
- **Audience Behavior**: Chatting over coffee, informal conversations, learning about projects/funds

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, informal, low-pressure
- ✅ Casual café atmosphere
- ✅ "Let's chat" — not "pitch me"
- ✅ Remove the tension of a demo day

### Strictly Prohibited
- ❌ Do not write it like a formal demo day or pitch event
- ❌ Do not use "fundraising opportunity" or "investment matching"
- ❌ Do not make it feel formal or high-pressure
- ❌ Avoid terms like "high-net-worth" or "top-tier"

---

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Café Scene Opening (Required)
**Formula**: [Café atmosphere] + [Casual conversation] + [Informality]

**Examples**:
- "Talking projects in a café beats a conference room any day"
- "Not a pitch session — just a coffee and a chat"
- "Investors are people too — they also drink coffee"

### 2. De-Formalized Descriptions (Required)
- "No slides, no timer"
- "Not a pitch — it's a conversation"
- "Talk about what you're working on, like you would with a friend"

### 3. Flow of the Afternoon (Recommended)
**Example**:
```
14:00 Investors arrive, grab a coffee first
14:30 Founders start dropping in, find someone interesting to chat with
16:00 Some are three conversations deep, others are working quietly in a corner
17:00 Stay if you want, leave when you're ready
```

### 4. Conversation Snapshot (Required)
- ❌ "Founders can introduce their projects to investors"
- ✅ "Someone's chatting about product direction, someone's discussing market trends, someone's just enjoying their coffee"

### 5. Office Hours Format (If Applicable)
- Investors are at the café during a set time window
- Founders can drop in for a chat
- No formal booking needed — just show up and talk

### 6. Low-Pressure Invitation (Required)
- ✅ "No deck required — just come ready to chat"
- ✅ "If there's a connection, great — if not, no worries"
- ✅ "Think of it as a casual intro"

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Investor Office Hours @ Café
- **Use For**: Recurring investor office hours at a café
- **Opening Format**: ☕ [Investor/Firm] Office Hours | Projects Over Coffee
- **Tone**: Relaxed, open, informal

### Variant B: Founder × Investor Café Mixer
- **Use For**: Casual gathering between founders and investors
- **Opening Format**: ☕ Founders × Investors Café | Grab a Coffee, Make an Intro
- **Tone**: Mutual, equal footing, relaxed

### Variant C: Portfolio Café Day
- **Use For**: Casual gathering between an investment firm and its portfolio companies
- **Opening Format**: ☕ [Fund Name] Portfolio Café | Family Coffee Time
- **Tone**: Family feel, warm, relaxed

## Key Considerations

### Investment Café Essentials
1. **De-Formalization Is the Core**: Emphasize "not a demo day," "no slides needed"
2. **Café Atmosphere**: Relaxed, casual, like chatting with friends
3. **Reduce Pressure on Both Sides**: Founders aren't nervous, investors aren't drained
4. **NFA Disclaimer Is Required**: Even in a casual setting
5. **Don't Flaunt Investor Titles**: No listing track records, no saying "top-tier"

### Prohibited Items
- ❌ Do not write it like a fundraising pitch event
- ❌ Do not emphasize "fundraising opportunities"
- ❌ Do not make it overly formal
- ❌ Do not create a sense of pressure
