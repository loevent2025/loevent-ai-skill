# AI Hackathon Knowledge Base – Investment Track

## Overview
Investment-focused AI hackathons center on startup project incubation and investor matchmaking. Participants are expected not only to deliver a working technical build, but to demonstrate clear commercial value and market potential. These events serve as a key channel for founders to secure funding, validate business models, and connect with investors — while also offering investment firms a highly efficient way to identify promising early-stage AI projects.

---

## Event Profile
- **Scale**: Tens to hundreds of participants; boutique format preferred
- **Duration**: Typically 48–72 hours of development + pitch day + post-event incubation support
- **Format**: Team building + business coaching + investor pitch + incubation matching
- **Participants**: AI founders, technical developers, product managers, investors, incubators
- **Defining characteristics**: Strong commercial orientation, rich resource connections, significant post-event opportunities

## Participation Tips

### Business Preparation
- Define clearly: what problem are you solving, and for whom?
- Research and understand the size of your target market
- Study your competitive landscape and articulate your differentiation
- Develop a basic business model framework before arriving
- Think through your customer acquisition and growth strategy

### Pitch Preparation
- Prepare both a 3-minute and 5-minute version of your pitch
- Structure: Pain point → Solution → Market → Team → Funding ask
- Use data to make your case — avoid vague, unsubstantiated claims
- Prepare answers to the most likely investor questions
- Practice until your delivery feels natural and confident

### Investor Conversations
- Research the investment preferences of investors attending
- Bring pitch decks at different levels of detail
- Know your baseline valuation thinking
- Answer questions honestly — don't oversell
- Follow up proactively and agree on clear next steps

### Team Composition
- Business + technical co-founder pairing is essential
- Domain expertise is a significant differentiator
- Strong pitch communication skills matter
- Execution ability is valued more than ideas alone
