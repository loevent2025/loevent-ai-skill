# Cocktail Party × Investment

## Applicable Scenarios
Investor social mixers, founder × investor mixers, VC Chill Nights, post-investment portfolio gatherings, angel investor evenings, venture community socials

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Relaxed socializing, no pitching, natural conversation, easy-going atmosphere
- **Core Value:** Meeting interesting people, unwinding, letting connections form on their own

## Audience Profiles
- **Primary Audience:** Investors — VCs and angels looking to meet peers or founders in a relaxed setting
- **Secondary Audience:** Founders — building genuine relationships with investors without the pressure of a pitch
- **Tertiary Audience:** Venture ecosystem adjacent — FAs, accelerators, startup service providers
- **Audience Behavior:** Socializing, talking, meeting people, decompressing

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, conversational, with a sense of taste and quality
- ✅ Direct, magnetic, rhythmically written
- ✅ Short sentences and fragmented, scannable information
- ✅ Purely positive — showcase the appeal, don't explain it
- ✅ Scene-first — make people want to be there before they know why

### Strictly Prohibited
- ❌ Contrast phrasing ("tired of X? Come here instead," "not X but Y")
- ❌ Transactional terms: "fundraising opportunities," "deal matching," "resource connections"
- ❌ Do not write it like a pitch event or demo day
- ❌ Do not emphasize "high-net-worth," "top-tier investors," or similar markers

---

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Scene-First Opening (Required)
**Formula:** [What makes this venue special] + [Sensory experience] + [Vibe words]

**Examples:**
- "A private club. Good whisky. The rare kind of relaxed evening in the venture world."
- "Rooftop bar. The Hong Kong harbour at night. Conversation that goes beyond deals."
- "A speakeasy-style bar — through the hidden door, everyone inside is interesting."

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (At Least 3 Required)
- **Visual:** City skyline, intimate setting, warm light, quality finishes
- **Taste:** Whisky, champagne, signature cocktails, refined bites
- **Sound:** Jazz, low conversation, the clink of glasses
- **Touch:** Leather seating, wood bar top, the warmth of a good drink
- **Atmosphere:** Exclusive feel, intimate, relaxed, natural

### 3. Time Flow Narrative (Recommended)
**Example:**
```
7:00 PM — Early arrivals at the bar, drinks in hand, easy chat
8:00 PM — The room fills up, people start to cluster
9:00 PM — Someone's talking about deal flow. Someone else is talking about their vacation.
10:00 PM — Conversations have found their own current. Nobody's rushing out.
```

### 4. Social Snapshot (Required)
- ❌ "Investors and founders can engage in in-depth exchange"
- ✅ "Someone at the bar is talking about deals they've been looking at. Someone on the couch is talking about where they're going for the holidays. Someone's out on the balcony just taking in the air."

### 5. Venue-Specific Experience (Required)
- Private club: "An exclusive kind of ease — everyone here is either an old friend or an interesting stranger"
- Whisky bar: "Good whisky, good conversation — the venture talk will come up naturally, but it won't be all there is"
- Rooftop: "City lights, a slight buzz, the kind of connection that exists outside of work"

### 6. Describing the Crowd (Recommended)
- ❌ "Top-tier VC partners, well-known angels, unicorn founders"
- ✅ "A group of people doing interesting things in the venture world"
- ✅ "Investors, founders, and people who are simply interesting"

### 7. Low-Pressure Invitation (Required)
- ✅ "No pitch session. No business card exchange time."
- ✅ "Come to social — not to do deals"
- ✅ "Connections happen on their own. No need to force it."

[Vibe description — no titles]

[Pure experience description — explicitly not a pitch]

[A touch of exclusivity, but not aggressive]

[Simple, tasteful, no FOMO]

## Notes

### Investment Cocktail Party Principles
1. **This is absolutely not a pitch event:** Explicitly emphasize no pitching, no deal talk
2. **Scene-first:** Lead with the venue and atmosphere before anything else
3. **De-transactionalize it:** Not here to "find resources" — here to social
4. **Describe people by vibe, not credential:** Don't parade "top investors"
5. **Exclusive feel without arrogance:** Intimate, not elitist
6. **Let connections form naturally:** Don't force the networking

### Prohibited
- ❌ Do not write it like a fundraising roadshow
- ❌ Do not emphasize "fundraising opportunities" or "deal flow matching"
- ❌ Do not list investors' titles or track records
- ❌ Do not create FOMO ("top-tier investors," "rare opportunity")
- ❌ Do not use "high-net-worth," "premium," or similar markers
