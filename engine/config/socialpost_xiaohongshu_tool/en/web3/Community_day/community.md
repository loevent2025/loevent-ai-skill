# Café × Community (AI / Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Product community Café pop-ups (e.g., Café Cursor), open-source project coffee gatherings, user community Café Days, Builder coffee hours, in-person meetups for tool users

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Café buyout or pop-up, user cowork, product team on-site, merch giveaways
- **Core Value**: Community belonging, meeting fellow users in person, talking directly with the team, picking up merch, building together

## Audience Segmentation
- **Primary Audience:** Product / tool users — people who use a specific product day-to-day
- **Secondary Audience:** Community members — active participants in the community
- **Tertiary Audience:** Prospective users — people curious about the product
- **Audience Behavior:** Cowork, talk about what they're building, connect with the team, grab stickers

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style (Reference: Café Cursor)

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, chill, builder culture
- ✅ "Come build with us" energy
- ✅ Strong product community belonging
- ✅ Short sentences, scannable, easy to skim
- ✅ Scene-first — the café is the main character

### Absolute Prohibitions
- ❌ Don't write it like a meetup or a sharing session
- ❌ No agenda, no speaking segments
- ❌ Don't emphasize "learning" or "takeaways"
- ❌ Avoid transactional language like "networking" or "connections"

---

## Content Enhancement Elements (Reference: Café Cursor Model)

### 1. Community Identity + Café Opening Hook (Required)
**Formula**: [Product / community name] + [café] + [build together]

**Examples**:
- "We bought out a café and invited Cursor users to come build"
- "Café [Product Name] | Bring your laptop, grab a coffee, let's code together"
- "[Product Name] users — let's meet in person"

### 2. Cowork as the Core (Required)
- "Bring your laptop and come build"
- "Not here to listen to talks — here to code"
- "Everyone around you is using the same tool"

### 3. Time-Slot Structure (Recommended — Reference: Cursor)
**Example**:
```
9:00–12:00   Morning session — grab a table and cowork
12:00–15:00  Afternoon session — grab a table and cowork
15:00–18:00  Evening session — grab a table and cowork
Or: drop in anytime for a coffee and say hi
```

### 4. Team On-Site (If Applicable)
- "The product team will be dropping in — ask them anything directly"
- "Engineers will be around — feel free to bring up issues you've run into"
- "Welcome to share what you're currently building"

### 5. Merch Culture (Café Cursor Signature Element)
- Free coffee (espresso drinks on the house)
- Stickers and pins (free for the taking)
- Custom merch (branded coffee beans, tab keys, etc.)
- Credits / trial access

### 6. Live Scene Snapshot (Required)
Describe what's actually happening — make it vivid:
- ❌ "Participants can interact with each other"
- ✅ "Someone's tuning a prompt, someone's debugging, someone's peering at their neighbor's screen, someone's swapping GitHub handles"

### 7. Drop-In Friendly (Required)
- "Stay all day or drop in for a quick coffee"
- "Popping by just to say hi is completely fine"
- "Stay as long as you want"

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Official Café Pop-Up (Café Cursor Style)
- **Best for:** Official product-hosted café events
- **Opening format:** ☕ Café [Product Name] [City] | Come build with us
- **Tone:** Official but chill, strong community feel, build together

### Variant B: Community-Organized Café Meetup
- **Best for:** Grassroots gatherings organized by community members
- **Opening format:** ☕ [Product Name] User Coffee Hangout | Fellow users, let's meet
- **Tone:** Grassroots, fellow-user energy, relaxed

### Variant C: Campus Café
- **Best for:** Product user gatherings on university campuses
- **Opening format:** ☕ [Product Name] on Campus | Campus builders, let's link up
- **Tone:** Student, youthful, exploratory

## Notes & Best Practices

### Community Café Checklist (Reference: Café Cursor)
1. **Cowork is the core**: Not here to listen — here to build
2. **Community belonging**: Emphasize "people who all use X"
3. **Team presence is a selling point**: Talking directly with the team is a highlight
4. **Merch culture**: Stickers, coffee, swag are standard for community events
5. **Flexible time slots**: Slot-based sign-up + drop-in option
6. **Low pressure**: Code if you want, chat if you want, drop in if you want

### What Made Café Cursor Work
- Bought out the café — drinks on the house
- Users brought their own laptops to cowork
- Team members on-site and available to talk
- Stickers, pins, custom coffee beans and other merch
- Slot-based sign-up that also accepted drop-ins
- No agenda, no talks — just build together

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Don't write it like a tech meetup
- ❌ No sharing or speaking segments
- ❌ Don't emphasize "learning" or "takeaways"
- ❌ Don't make it feel formal
