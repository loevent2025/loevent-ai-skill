# GENERATIVE — Generative Art Style Guide

## Applicable Use Cases

On-chain generative art, algorithmic NFT collections, art exhibition visuals, AI collaboration projects, creative coding showcases, procedural design systems, long-form generative collections, computational art displays

---

## Color Palette

- **Primary tones:** Algorithmic palettes, procedurally generated hues, systematic color gradients
- **Backgrounds:** White, black, or gradient fields that allow patterns to stand out
- **Saturation:** Variable — from monochromatic to vibrant multi-hue systems
- **Color behavior:** Mathematically determined distribution, noise-based variation, parametric color shifting

---

## Standard Color Combinations

| Theme | Colors |
|---|---|
| **Algorithmic Monochrome** | Black `#000000` + White `#FFFFFF` + Gray range `#333333` to `#CCCCCC` |
| **Generative Rainbow** | Hue rotated systematically across the full spectrum via noise modulation |
| **Organic Code** | Earth tones — Ochre `#CC8800` + Moss Green `#6B8E23` + Clay Red `#B7410E` + Cream `#FFF8DC` |
| **Digital Nature** | Cyan `#00CED1` + Coral `#FF7F50` + Sage `#9DC183` + Lavender `#E6E6FA` |

## Visual Elements

- Recursive fractal patterns (Mandelbrot set, Julia set, L-systems)
- Perlin / Simplex noise fields generating organic textures
- Flow field particle systems exhibiting emergent behavior
- Voronoi diagrams and Delaunay triangulations
- Circle packing with collision detection
- Algorithmic line work (turtle graphics, recursive branching)
- Grid distortion via noise functions
- Particle swarms with flocking behavior
- Cellular automata patterns (Conway's Game of Life variants)
- Bézier curve networks with parametric variation

---

## Treatment & Effects

- Layered transparency to increase visual complexity
- Gaussian noise overlay for added texture
- Gradient mesh interpolation
- Random distribution of elements
- Iterative accumulation of marks
- Mathematical blend modes
- Parametric opacity variation
- Dynamic noise modulation
- Procedural texture generation
- Alpha channel variation for depth

## Composition Strategies

- Emergent compositions arising from algorithmic rules
- Symmetry broken through controlled randomness
- Density gradients from sparse to dense
- Radial or grid-based organizational systems
- Edge-aware distribution
- Attractor-based element placement
- Recursive spatial subdivision
- Organic growth patterns originating from seed points
- Balance achieved through mathematical constraints
- Controlled chaos via bounded randomness

---

## Typography Guidelines

- **Color selection:** Choose solid colors with high contrast against the background
  - White background: Pure Black `#000000`
  - Black background: Pure White `#FFFFFF`
  - Cream / light background: Pure Black `#000000` or Dark Gray `#333333`
  - Alternative solid colors: Cyan `#00CED1`, Coral `#FF7F50` (ensure sufficient contrast against background)
- **Fill method:** Solid color fill only — single color, no gradients
- **Prohibited effects:**
  - No algorithmic gradients
  - No noise modulation
  - No opacity variation
  - No glow effects
  - No strokes
  - No shadows
  - No text effects of any kind
- **Overall principle:** Typography must remain clean, clear, and highly legible — serving as a stable anchor within the generative art, creating a deliberate "human vs. algorithmic" contrast with the computationally generated patterns

Computational beauty, organic emergence from code, mathematical poetry, controlled randomness. The overall atmosphere is intellectual yet visually captivating — celebrating the intersection of art and algorithm, where complexity emerges from simple rules.
