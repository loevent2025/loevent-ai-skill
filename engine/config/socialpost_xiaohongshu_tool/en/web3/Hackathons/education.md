# Web3 Hackathon Knowledge Base – Education Track

## Overview
Education-focused Web3 hackathons are designed for Web3 learners and early-stage developers. They emphasize the learning journey and skill development while lowering the barrier to entry. These events are one of the most effective pathways into Web3 development — using a "learn by building" approach to help participants rapidly acquire core skills like smart contract development and DApp construction, backed by comprehensive guidance and mentor support. They are especially well-suited for developers making the transition from Web2 to Web3.

---

## Event Profile
- **Scale**: Tens to hundreds of participants; mid-sized for optimal mentorship
- **Duration**: Typically 24–48 hours, with pre-event training sessions
- **Format**: Pre-event curriculum + team building + mentor-led development + showcase and judging
- **Participants**: Students, Web2 career changers, Web3 newcomers, beginner developers
- **Defining characteristics**: Low barrier to entry, high mentor density, strong learning culture, inclusive environment

## Participation Tips

### Mindset
- Don't worry about being "not good enough" — everyone here is a beginner
- Ask questions boldly; there are no dumb questions
- Enjoy the learning process; focus less on the outcome
- Support your teammates and grow together
- Shipped and imperfect beats unfinished and perfect

### Learning Strategies
- Make full use of the pre-event training and learning materials
- Set up your wallet and familiarize yourself with the testnet before arrival
- Explore the project templates and boilerplate code ahead of time
- Ask mentors for help the moment you get stuck
- Take notes you can refer back to later

### Project Approach
- Choose a simple, completable project scope
- Lean on the provided templates and example contracts
- Get it working on testnet first, then optimize
- Aim for a complete deployment, not complexity
- Prepare to clearly demo your project's core feature

### After the Event
- Save your learning materials and code
- Stay in touch with your learning partners
- Continue deepening your Solidity or Rust knowledge along the suggested path
- Try deploying a simple DApp independently
- Join the learning community and keep the conversation going
- Contribute to open-source projects to build real experience
