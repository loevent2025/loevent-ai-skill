# Cross-Sector Collaboration × General / Event Playbook
*(AI / Model Commercialization Edition)*

## Applicable Scenarios
Cross-industry exchange summits, traditional industry × tech sector dialogues, diverse-background builder gatherings, cross-domain innovation salons, industry convergence networking events, cross-sector thematic sharing sessions

## Scenario Profile
- **Format:** Cross-domain sharing, multi-background exchange, cross-sector dialogue, open networking
- **Core Value:** Expanding cross-sector perspectives, building diverse networks, sparking innovation, breaking cognitive boundaries

## Audience Definition
- **Primary Audience:** Cross-sector explorers — professionals from one field curious about others
- **Secondary Audience:** Multi-background professionals — specialists from different industries
- **Tertiary Audience:** Innovation seekers — entrepreneurs and professionals looking for cross-sector opportunities
- **Extended Audience:** Cross-sector observers — people interested in the convergence of different domains
- **Audience Behavior:** Expanding perspective, building connections, discovering opportunities, gaining new insights

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Open exchange:** Practitioners from various fields — suited for open-format networking
- **Theme-focused:** Professionals gathered around a specific cross-sector topic — suited for thematic sharing
- **Innovation-oriented:** Entrepreneurs + domain experts — suited for innovation exploration

## Cross-Sector Content Enhancement Elements (Required)

### 1. Contrast and Tension
**Narrative formula:**
"When [representatives of World X] meet [representatives of World Y] — what happens?"

**Required elements:**
- Clearly articulate the diversity of participant backgrounds
- Use contrast framing: "Some people bring experience from X; others bring a perspective shaped entirely by Y"
- Emphasize the novelty and possibility that this diverse mix creates

### 2. Chemistry Description
**Use dynamic verbs:**
- ❌ "exchange," "share," "partner"
- ✅ "collide," "encounter," "clash," "catalyze," "spark"

**Core question:** "Why does cross-sector dialogue produce sparks that single-domain conversations simply cannot?"

### 3. Participant Collision Description
- [Industry A] practitioners encounter [Industry B] experts
- [Traditional domain] veterans meet [emerging domain] pioneers
- [Technical thinking] meets [business thinking] head-on

### 4. Scene and Atmosphere Building (Select at least 2)
- **Concrete scene:** The collision of industry jargon from different worlds; unexpected moments of resonance
- **Sense of time:** The journey from strangers to familiar faces to discovering shared ground
- **Dynamic interaction:** Lively debate, unexpected connections, new ideas taking shape in real time
- **Emotional energy:** The surprise of "you do that too?"; the inspiration of "I never thought about it that way"

### 5. Unique Outcomes to Imagine
List the connections and ideas that can only emerge from cross-sector conversation

### 6. Transformation Arc (Recommended)
Describe the cognitive shift from "I only understand my own field" to "the world is so much bigger than I realized"

## Notes & Best Practices

### Key Principles for General Cross-Sector Events
1. **Diverse backgrounds are the foundation:** Participants must come from genuinely varied domains
2. **Design collision mechanisms intentionally:** Free networking alone isn't enough — build in structured moments for cross-sector sparks
3. **Foster an open, inclusive atmosphere:** Every background should feel equally welcome
4. **Build in post-event connection:** Give participants a mechanism to stay in touch afterward
5. **Theme can be focused or open:** Choose based on the event's purpose
6. **Prevent single-industry dominance:** Monitor and balance the ratio of participants by background
