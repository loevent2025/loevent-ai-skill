# Cross-Sector Collaboration × Community / Event Playbook
*(AI / Model Commercialization Edition)*

## Applicable Scenarios
Cross-community joint events, multi-community activations, cross-domain open-source collaboration, community alliance building, cross-circle builder gatherings, ecosystem co-creation initiatives

## Scenario Profile
- **Format:** Multi-community collaboration, cross-circle exchange, cross-domain co-building, community alliance events
- **Core Value:** Community boundary-breaking, cross-circle connections, ecosystem convergence, co-creation culture

## Audience Definition
- **Primary Audience:** Community members — active participants from different communities and circles
- **Secondary Audience:** Community organizers — leaders looking to expand their community's reach
- **Tertiary Audience:** Cross-circle explorers — people who want to engage meaningfully across multiple communities
- **Extended Audience:** Ecosystem partners — organizations interested in cross-community collaboration
- **Audience Behavior:** Cross-circle exchange, building connections, participating in co-creation, expanding networks

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Community activation:** Members from Community A + members from Community B
- **Cross-circle co-building:** Core contributors from multiple communities
- **Ecosystem convergence:** Developers and users from different ecosystems

## Cross-Sector Content Enhancement Elements (Required)

### 1. Contrast and Tension
**Narrative formula:**
"When [Community A] meets [Community B] — what sparks will fly?"

**Required elements:**
- Clearly articulate the distinctive traits and differences between the two communities
- Use contrast framing: "Community A is known for…; Community B brings…"
- Emphasize the novelty and possibility of this particular community pairing

### 2. Chemistry Description
**Use dynamic verbs:**
- ❌ "collaborate," "partner," "connect"
- ✅ "collide," "converge," "meet," "resonate," "co-create"

**Core question:** "Why does bringing these two communities together create value that neither could generate alone?"

### 3. Participant Collision Description
- [Community A] builders meet [Community B] builders
- [Technical community] members go head-to-head with [product community] members
- Cultural collision between [domestic community] and [global community]

### 4. Scene and Atmosphere Building (Select at least 2)
- **Concrete scene:** The clash of community-specific terminology, moments of unexpected cultural fusion
- **Sense of time:** The journey from strangers to icebreakers to deep conversation
- **Dynamic interaction:** Cross-community team formation, joint co-creation, new communities emerging
- **Emotional energy:** The novelty of breaking out of your circle, the surprise of finding your people across the divide

### 5. Unique Outcomes to Imagine
List the projects, content, and connections that can only emerge from cross-community collaboration

### 6. Transformation Arc (Recommended)
Describe the shift from "we come from different worlds" to "we can actually build something together"

## Notes & Best Practices

### Key Principles for Cross-Sector Community Events
1. **Preserve community identity:** The goal isn't to erase differences — it's to unite while respecting them
2. **Icebreaking mechanisms matter:** Design intentional moments for members of different communities to connect quickly
3. **Co-creation must produce something:** Exchange alone isn't enough — there should be a tangible joint output
4. **Ongoing collaboration mechanism:** One event isn't sufficient; build a structure for continued engagement
5. **Community organizers must be involved:** Cross-community collaboration needs buy-in at the community leadership level
6. **Prevent single-community dominance:** Monitor and balance the ratio of participants from each community
