# AI BUSINESS CONFERENCES × TECHNICAL

## Use Cases
AI technical summits, large model technology conferences, MLOps forums, AI architect summits, AI security conferences, ML engineer conferences

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Technical talks + architecture sharing + hands-on workshops + technical panels
- **Core Value:** Technical insights, architecture learning, hands-on experience, peer exchange

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** AI Engineers / ML Engineers — learning technical approaches, discovering best practices
- **Secondary Audience:** AI Technical Leads / Architects — evaluating technical solutions, hiring, strategic decisions
- **Tertiary Audience:** Technical Product Managers — understanding technical boundaries, building better products
- **Extended Audience:** Technical Investors — assessing technical feasibility, identifying technical trends
- **Audience Behavior:** Learning techniques, evaluating solutions, exchanging experience, finding collaborators

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Engineer-oriented:** AI engineers + ML engineers — best for technical learning events
- **Architect-oriented:** Technical leads + architects — best for technical decision-making events

## Content Elements
- Technical summit theme and positioning
- Technical speaker lineup
- Technical agenda
- Workshop and hands-on segments
- Technical community entry points
- Open-source project and tool recommendations

## Language Tone
- Technically oriented, but approachable and direct
- Use plain language to explain technical concepts
- Emphasize "what you'll learn" and "lessons learned the hard way"
- Let code and practice do the talking
- Practical over impressive — no jargon for jargon's sake

## Content Structure
- Opening hook block: 15%
- Technical content block: 35% — technical themes + highlights
- Speaker lineup block: 20% — technical backgrounds
- Learning value block: 20% — what you'll walk away with
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 8–12 total, technical feel without being dry

## Emotional Tone
Technically rigorous, hands-on oriented, learning and growth. The energy of "exchanging ideas with the best AI engineers in the field."

## Attendance Tips

### Before the Event
- Review the agenda and mark the sessions you most want to attend
- Prepare technical questions to ask during sessions
- If there's a workshop, check what you'll need to prepare in advance
- Bring your laptop and charger

### On-Site Tips
- Sit up front — easier to follow code and architecture diagrams
- Take careful notes, especially technical details
- Ask when you're unsure — technical audiences are generally welcoming
- Connect with speakers for follow-up questions later

### Post-Event Follow-Up
- Organize your notes and try to reproduce what you learned
- Share your takeaways in technical communities
- Follow speakers' technical blogs or accounts
- Keep tracking the relevant technical developments

## Notes

### Key Principles for Technical AI Business Conferences
1. **Technical substance is the core:** Not concepts — details
2. **Speakers must have real hands-on backgrounds:** People who've actually built it
3. **Hands-on workshops add significant value:** Participants learn more by doing than by listening
4. **Code and architecture diagrams belong on screen:** Technical events should feel technical
5. **Communicate difficulty level upfront:** Let participants set appropriate expectations
