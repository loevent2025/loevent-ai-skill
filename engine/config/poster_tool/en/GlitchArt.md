# GLITCH ART — Visual Style Guide

## Use Cases
Conference key visuals, hackathons, NFT launches, crypto art festivals, tech disruption events, digital art exhibitions, experimental music festivals

## Color Palette
- **Primary tones:** High-saturation blue, vivid red, electric cyan, intense magenta
- **Background:** Pure black or deep dark base with high-contrast color overlays
- **Accent colors:** Neon green, pure white, deep purple, toxic yellow
- **Saturation:** Extreme saturation with oversaturated RGB channels
- **Color expression:** RGB channel separation, chromatic aberration, color overflow

### Common Color Combinations
- Classic Glitch: Electric blue + vivid red on black, with cyan-magenta RGB separation
- Digital Error: Neon green + intense magenta + pure white with color channel shift
- System Crash: Deep purple + toxic yellow + electric cyan at maximum saturation

## Styling Treatments
- RGB channel displacement and separation
- Horizontal and vertical pixel sorting
- Screen tears with offset layers
- Compression artifacts and JPEG glitch
- Interlacing and scan line effects
- Digital noise overlay at varying intensities
- Motion blur with directional streaks
- Chromatic fringing and aberration
- Data corruption with temporal artifacts
- Bit-depth compression and quantization errors

## Composition Strategy
- Intentionally broken symmetry with displaced elements
- Layered fragments creating depth through disruption
- Concentrated distortion zones contrasted against cleaner areas
- Horizontal stripe and vertical tear patterns
- Asymmetric compositions with chaotic energy
- Strategic placement of intact elements within the chaos
- Rhythmic repetition of glitch patterns
- Dynamic diagonal displacement
- Controlled chaos — focal points emerge from disorder

---

## Text Settings
- **Color selection:** Choose solid colors with high contrast against the black background
  - Recommended primaries: Pure white (#FFFFFF), electric blue (#00BFFF), vivid red (#FF0000)
  - Alternative solids: Neon green (#00FF00), intense magenta (#FF00FF), electric cyan (#00FFFF)
- **Fill method:** Solid color fill only — single color, no gradients
- **Prohibited effects:**
  - No RGB channel separation
  - No chromatic aberration or color shift
  - No pixelated distortion
  - No scan line overlay
  - No glitch tear effects
  - No data stream corruption
  - No noise overlay
  - No text effects of any kind
- **Overall principle:** Keep text clean, sharp, and highly legible — serving as a clear anchor within the glitched background. Use font selection and high-contrast solid colors to create visual impact, establishing an "order vs. chaos" contrast against the disrupted surroundings.

Digital rebellion, system failure aesthetics, technical collapse as art. ## Example Prompt
Render as a flat image, surface graphic design, pattern design. Fill frame edge-to-edge — no external borders, no canvas/paper texture, no mockup. Content fills the entire canvas to the edges with absolutely no margin or white space around the perimeter. No visible text, letters, numbers, or color codes anywhere in the image.

Apply a glitch art aesthetic suffused with digital corruption. The background is filled entirely with pure black for maximum contrast. The primary compositional feature is a large geometric shape fragmented into displaced rectangular blocks, with electric blue and vivid red color channels violently separated to produce ghost effects extending to all edges. Horizontal scan lines of varying thickness sweep the full width, creating the illusion of screen tearing. RGB channel displacement causes cyan and magenta shadows to offset in opposing directions from the primary elements. Pixelated distortion zones are scattered throughout the composition — some areas show severe pixel sorting forming horizontal color bands, while others remain sharp for rhythmic contrast. Digital noise patterns cover the entire canvas at varying intensities, denser in certain regions. Neon green and toxic yellow vertical glitch streaks tear through the composition from top to bottom, evoking system error aesthetics. Data stream trails suggest motion artifacts with temporal distortion. Pure white fragments punctuate key areas as high-contrast accents against the dark base. Deep purple compression artifacts cluster in the corners and extend toward the edges. All elements bleed to the canvas boundary — the intentionally displaced and fragmented grid creates controlled digital chaos that fills the entire frame.

The overall energy is aggressive and disruptive — pure glitch aesthetics celebrating technical collapse and digital rebellion, ideal for event campaigns and NFT launches that embrace raw digital corruption as artistic expression.

**Text areas use pure white (#FFFFFF) or electric blue (#00BFFF) as a solid fill — single color only, with no RGB separation, no chromatic aberration, no pixelation, no scan lines, no glitch effects, and no text effects of any kind. Text must remain clean and sharp with high legibility, serving as a clear anchor within the chaotic background to create an "order vs. chaos" visual contrast.**
