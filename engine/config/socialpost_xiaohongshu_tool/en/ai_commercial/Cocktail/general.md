# Cocktail Party × General

## Applicable Scenarios
Social mixers, industry cocktail parties, themed parties, Networking Nights, interest-based gatherings, Friday Drinks, Weekend Gatherings

## Scene Characteristics
- **Format:** Relaxed socializing, no agenda, natural conversation, easy-going atmosphere
- **Core Value:** Meeting interesting people, having a good time, enjoying the vibe

## Audience Profiles
- **Primary Audience:** People who want to social — folks from all backgrounds looking to meet someone new
- **Secondary Audience:** Industry practitioners — socializing around a shared theme or field
- **Tertiary Audience:** Curious people — drawn to the theme, crowd, or just the atmosphere
- **Audience Behavior:** Socializing, drinking, chatting, meeting people, enjoying the moment

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, conversational, and youthful
- ✅ Direct, magnetic, rhythmically written
- ✅ Short sentences and fragmented, scannable information
- ✅ Purely positive — showcase the appeal, don't explain it
- ✅ The scene itself is the star

### Strictly Prohibited
- ❌ Contrast phrasing ("tired of X? Come here instead," "not X but Y")
- ❌ Negating or criticizing any other type of event or occasion
- ❌ Transactional terms: "takeaways," "value," "gains," "networking," "resources"
- ❌ Creating anxiety or FOMO

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Scene-First Opening (Required)
**Formula:** [What makes this venue special] + [Sensory experience] + [Vibe words]

**Examples:**
- "Rooftop. Sunset. That warm, hazy hour."
- "Terrace bar. Ocean breeze. Chill beats."
- "A hidden bar tucked inside an old villa — step through the door and you're in another world."

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (At Least 3 Required)
- **Visual:** Sunset, city lights, warm ambient light, aesthetic space
- **Taste:** Champagne, cocktails, craft beer, small bites
- **Sound:** Chill beats, live DJ, low murmur of good conversation
- **Touch:** Sea breeze, gentle warmth of the buzz, the weight of a glass
- **Atmosphere:** Relaxed, chill, slightly buzzing, the vibe is just right

### 3. Time Flow Narrative (Recommended)
**Example:**
```
6:00 PM — Still light out. Early arrivals at the bar, already chatting.
Sunset — Everyone drifts toward the windows.
Nightfall — The music gets a little louder.
Nobody's in a hurry to leave.
```

### 4. Social Snapshot (Required)
Describe what's happening — not what people "can" do:

- ❌ "Participants can network freely"
- ✅ "Someone at the bar just met and they're already deep in it. Someone on the couch is exchanging Instagram handles. Someone's out on the terrace just catching the breeze."

### 5. Venue-Specific Experience (Required)
- Rooftop: "Catching the sunset from the rooftop, slightly buzzing as the city lights all turn on at once"
- Yacht: "Watching the skyline from the water, becoming friends with a stranger on the deck"
- Heritage villa: "Socializing surrounded by history — the conversation just feels different"
- Beach: "Barefoot in the sand, sea breeze, cold beer, summer in its purest form"

### 6. Describing the Crowd (Recommended)
- ❌ "Entrepreneurs, investors, designers, and product managers"
- ✅ "A group of interesting people"
- ✅ "You'll meet interesting people. What exactly they do? You'll find out when you talk to them."

### 7. Low-Pressure Invitation (Required)
- ✅ "Come if you can — no pressure if you can't"
- ✅ "Nothing to prepare. Just bring yourself."
- ✅ "Stay as long as you like. When the vibe's right, you won't want to leave."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Venue-Driven
- **Best for:** When the venue itself is the biggest draw
- **Opening format:** 🍸 [Venue description] + [Sensory experience]
- **Tone:** Aesthetic, chill, immersive

### Variant B: Interest-Group Social
- **Best for:** Socializing around a specific theme or community
- **Opening format:** ✨ [Community / Theme] + Social Night
- **Tone:** Relaxed, like-minded, vibes-aligned

### Variant C: Seasonal / Holiday Party
- **Best for:** Seasonal or holiday gatherings
- **Opening format:** 🎉 [Season / Holiday] + Party
- **Tone:** Celebratory, easy, happy

## Notes

### General Cocktail Party Principles
1. **The scene is the main character:** Venue and atmosphere are the primary draw
2. **Purely positive framing:** No contrast phrasing; don't diminish anything else
3. **Five-sense immersion:** Help readers "see it," "hear it," "feel it"
4. **Low-pressure invitation:** Come or don't — no anxiety created
5. **Describe people by vibe, not title:** Say "interesting" — not what they do
6. **Short sentence rhythm:** Fragmented, scannable, with pace

### Prohibited
- ❌ "Tired of...? Come here instead..."
- ❌ "Not X, but Y"
- ❌ "Don't miss out"
- ❌ "In-depth exchange" / "high-quality networking"
- ❌ Any form of negative comparison or implicit criticism
