# Café × Education (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Study Group Cafés, Study Cafés, Mentor Office Hours @ Café, Student Coffee Meetups, Campus Café Meetups, Bootcamp Coffee Hangouts

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Café-based studying/discussion, casual mentor Q&A, peer-to-peer support, campus hangouts
- **Core Value**: Relaxed learning atmosphere, lowering the barrier to asking questions, peer support, making mentors more approachable

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: Learners — course participants, self-taught learners, students
- **Secondary Audience**: Mentors — those who want to connect with students in a relaxed setting
- **Tertiary Audience**: Study buddies — people looking for others to learn alongside
- **Audience Behavior**: Studying, asking questions, discussing, helping each other

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Warm, encouraging, relaxed
- ✅ Ease learning anxiety
- ✅ Equal, conversational feel
- ✅ Café-level comfort

### Strictly Avoid
- ❌ Don't be too serious or formal
- ❌ Don't create exam or assessment pressure
- ❌ Don't make it feel like a classroom
- ❌ Avoid "must" or "have to" language

---

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Relaxed Learning Scene Opening (Required)
**Formula**: [Café atmosphere] + [Learning/discussion] + [Relaxed feel]

**Examples**:
- "Studying in a café just hits different"
- "Asking questions over coffee — way less intimidating"
- "Let's find a café and get through this assignment together"

### 2. Pressure-Reducing Language (Required)
- "There are no stupid questions"
- "Your mentor is right there — just ask"
- "Everyone's been confused before — that's how learning works"

### 3. Timeline Flow (Recommended)
**Example**:
```
2:00 PM  Arrive, grab a coffee, open your laptop
3:00 PM  Some people working on assignments, others watching tutorials
4:00 PM  Stuck on something? Ask the person next to you or a mentor
5:00 PM  Wrapping up — chat about what to tackle next week
```

### 4. Peer Support Atmosphere (Recommended)
- "Those who get it first help those still working through it"
- "The thing you're stuck on — the person next to you might have just figured it out"
- "Learning together beats learning alone"

### 5. Mentor Presence (When Applicable)
- "Mentors will be there, but it's not a class"
- "Got a question? Just ask — no need to raise your hand"
- "Think of it like asking a friend"

## Key Considerations

### Education Café Best Practices
1. **Reduce learning pressure**: A café is more relaxed than a classroom
2. **Encourage questions**: There are no stupid questions
3. **Foster peer support**: Learning together beats learning alone
4. **Mentor as equal**: It's not a lecture — it's a conversation
5. **Warm tone**: Encouraging, supportive, companionable

### Strictly Avoid
- ❌ Don't make it feel like a classroom
- ❌ Don't create assessment pressure
- ❌ Don't be overly formal
- ❌ Don't emphasize "must master" or "need to know"
