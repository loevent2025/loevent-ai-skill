# HACKATHONS × COMMUNITY

## Use Cases
Community hackathons, DAO hackathons, ecosystem builder events, NFT project hackathons, fan-driven build events, social hackathons, community contribution challenges

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale:** 30–200 people
- **Duration:** 24–72 hours (or longer async formats)
- **Format:** Community co-building, collaborative development, social integration, relaxed atmosphere
- **Core Value:** Community belonging, collaborative experience, contribution recognition, social connection

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Community Members — active members within the project community
- **Secondary Audience:** Community Developers — developers who want to contribute code to the community
- **Potential Audience:** Potential Contributors — people who want to get involved in community building
- **Extended Audience:** Community Fans — supporters who participate in community activities
- **Audience Behavior:** Contributing to the community, earning recognition, building social connections, deepening a sense of belonging

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Builder Focus:** Primarily community developers — best for tool and feature development
- **Open Community:** Full community participation — best for creative and content-based challenges

## Content Elements
- Community identity callout
- Co-building theme overview
- Contribution directions (not just code)
- Community recognition mechanisms
- Social activity schedule
- Contribution recognition formats (POAP, roles, badges)

## Language Tone
- Community-native, casual register
- Emphasize "building together"
- Contributions aren't limited to code
- Light and fun atmosphere
- Recognize and appreciate contributors

## Content Structure
- Opening callout block: 15%
- Co-building overview block: 35% — theme + contribution directions
- Social activities block: 20%
- Participation details block: 20%
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 14–18 total, energetic and warm

## Emotional Tone
Warm, collaborative, celebratory. The energy of "building together, growing together." Supporting the community, recognizing contributors.

## Style Variants

## Notes

### Key Principles for Community Hackathons
1. **Contributions aren't limited to code:** Design, content, and operations all count
2. **Recognition matters:** POAP, roles, featured showcases, and appreciation go a long way
3. **Keep the atmosphere light:** Weave in social activities throughout
4. **Community identity is central:** Emphasize "our community" and "together"
