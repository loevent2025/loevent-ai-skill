# Café × Technical (AI / Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
AI tool user Cafés, developer cowork days, coding cafés, tech product user gatherings, open-source project café meetups, builder cafés

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Café buyout or pop-up, cowork + casual socializing, bring your laptop and build
- **Core Value**: Code while you caffeinate, meet like-minded people, talk with the product team, pick up merch

## Audience Segmentation
- **Primary Audience:** Tool / product users — developers who use a specific tool day-to-day
- **Secondary Audience:** Tech enthusiasts — people with strong interest in a particular technical direction
- **Tertiary Audience:** Prospective users — people curious about the tool
- **Audience Behavior:** Cowork, code, talk about what they're building, grab stickers

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, chill, builder culture
- ✅ "Coffee and code" energy
- ✅ Product community belonging
- ✅ Short sentences, scannable, easy to skim
- ✅ Scene-first — the café atmosphere is the main character

### Absolute Prohibitions
- ❌ Don't write it like a tech talk or meetup
- ❌ Don't emphasize "learning" or "takeaways"
- ❌ Don't make it feel formal
- ❌ Avoid transactional language like "networking" or "connections"

---

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Café Scene Opening Hook (Required)
**Formula**: [Café atmosphere] + [tool / product name] + [build together]

**Examples**:
- "Coffee, code, a room full of people using Cursor"
- "We bought out a café and invited [Product Name] users to come build"
- "Bring your laptop, grab a coffee, ship something while you're at it"

### 2. Sensory Immersion (Include at Least 3)
- **Visual**: Warm café lighting, laptops open everywhere, sticker-covered surfaces
- **Taste**: Espresso, pour-over, lattes, light bites
- **Sound**: Keys clacking, low murmur of conversation, the hiss of the espresso machine
- **Touch**: Wooden tables, the warmth of a coffee cup, the feel of a good keyboard
- **Atmosphere**: Focused but loose, chill, build vibe

### 3. Time Flow Description (Recommended)
**Example**:
```
9:00   Walk in, grab a coffee, find a seat
10:00  Start coding — the person next to you is coding too
12:00  Lunch break — everyone shares what they're working on
15:00  Someone just shipped a small feature
17:00  Wrap up, swap GitHub handles on the way out
```

### 4. Live Cowork Snapshot (Required)
Describe what's actually happening:
- ❌ "Participants are free to code"
- ✅ "Someone's tuning a prompt, someone's debugging, someone's peering at their neighbor's screen picking up tricks"

### 5. Product Team On-Site (If Applicable)
- "The product team will be there — ask anything directly"
- "Engineers will be dropping in — bring your questions"
- "Not customer support — this is builder-to-builder"

### 6. Merch / Giveaways (Recommended)
- Stickers, pins, custom merch
- Free coffee
- Credits / trial access
- Custom coffee beans (if available)

### 7. Low-Pressure Participation (Required)
- ✅ "Stay all day or drop in for a quick coffee"
- ✅ "No sharing required, no demos required — just come build"
- ✅ "Chat if you want, focus if you want"

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Product User Café (Cursor Style)
- **Best for:** Gatherings for users of a specific tool or product
- **Opening format:** ☕ Café [Product Name] | Bring your laptop and come build
- **Tone:** Product community feel, chill, build together

### Variant B: Developer Cowork Day
- **Best for:** General developer cowork sessions
- **Opening format:** ☕💻 Coding Café | An afternoon of building together
- **Tone:** Focused, companionable, everyone doing their own thing but together

### Variant C: Open-Source Project Café Meetup
- **Best for:** Open-source community in-person gatherings
- **Opening format:** ☕💙 [Project Name] Café | Contributors meet IRL
- **Tone:** Community spirit, open-source culture, relaxed

## Notes & Best Practices

### Technical Café Checklist
1. **The café atmosphere is the core**: Not a conference room — a café
2. **Cowork is the primary format**: Bring your laptop and build — not here to listen
3. **Product / community belonging**: Emphasize "people who all use X"
4. **Low pressure**: Focus and code, or chat, or drop in — all valid
5. **Merch culture**: Stickers, coffee, small giveaways are a bonus
6. **Flexible slots**: Choose a time slot or just show up

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Don't write it like a tech meetup or talk
- ❌ No agenda, no speaking, no demo segments
- ❌ Don't emphasize "learning" or "takeaways"
- ❌ Don't make it feel formal
