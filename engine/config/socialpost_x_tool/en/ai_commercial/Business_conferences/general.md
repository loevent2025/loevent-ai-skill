# B2B Conference × General (AI/Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
AI industry forums, large model commercialization summits, AI product leadership conferences, enterprise AI application forums, AI founder conferences, AI growth summits, vertical industry AI forums

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Keynote addresses, panel discussions, breakout sessions, executive sessions, networking
- **Core Value**: Industry insights, business experience, in-depth exchange, high-quality connections

## Audience Positioning
- **Primary Audience**: AI product and business leaders—decision-makers responsible for AI product direction and commercialization
- **Secondary Audience**: AI technology leaders—technical executives who need to understand business requirements
- **Tertiary Audience**: AI founders—entrepreneurs building AI products
- **Extended Audience**: Enterprise AI decision-makers—executives evaluating AI applications
- **Audience Behavior**: Acquiring industry insights, learning business experience, building partnerships, identifying growth opportunities

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Product + Business**: Product leaders + Founders, suitable for product and growth topics
- **Technical + Business**: Technology leaders + Business leaders, suitable for implementation topics
- **Executive Level**: Enterprise executives + Founders, suitable for strategy and trends topics

## Content Elements
- Conference theme and positioning
- Core topic design
- Speaker lineup
- Panel / dialogue sessions
- Breakout session design (if applicable)
- Networking sessions
- Post-event community

## Language Guidelines
- Business-first perspective
- Data and case study driven
- Problem-oriented to spark resonance
- Avoid vague trend descriptions
- Keep technical terms in English (Agent, RAG, LLM)
- Emphasize "actionable" over "conceptual"

## Structure Strategy
- Opening information block: 15%
- Background and problem block: 20%
- Topic content block: 35%
- Participation value block: 20%
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density control: 8-12 total

## Emotional Tone
Pragmatic and business-focused, in-depth exchange. A professional feel of "discussing what actually works in practice." Anti-hype, anti-buzzword, execution-oriented.

## Style Variants

## Background Pain Point Templates

### AI Product Direction
**Trend Overview:**
AI product form factors are flourishing, but the number of products that have achieved true PMF remains small. Copilot, Agent, AI-native... the debate over product form factors is far from settled, and the gap between user expectations and product capabilities persists.

**Core Questions:**
❓ Copilot vs Agent vs AI-native—which form factor best fits your use case?
❓ What do users expect from AI products? How much of that are we delivering?
❓ How does the growth and retention logic for AI products differ from traditional products?
❓ How do you price a product so users feel they're getting real value?

### AI Commercialization Direction
**Trend Overview:**
Building an AI product is easy; making money from it is hard. Lots of free users but low paid conversion, high costs but pricing hard to raise, fast growth but poor retention... the commercialization challenge is greater than expected.

**Core Questions:**
❓ What are the business models available for AI products? What are the pros and cons of each?
❓ How do you calculate the unit economics for an AI product?
❓ How do you manage token costs? How do you pass them on to users?
❓ How do strategies differ between going from 0 to 1 versus 1 to 10?

### Enterprise AI Application Direction
**Trend Overview:**
Enterprise AI procurement has entered a rationalization phase. Many POCs have been conducted, but few have made it to production with measurable value. Enterprises are starting to ask tougher questions: What's the actual ROI? Can it be quantified?

**Core Questions:**
❓ What kind of AI capabilities are enterprises most willing to pay for?
❓ How do you prove the value of AI to an enterprise?
❓ What does the enterprise AI procurement decision process look like?
❓ How do you balance customization demands with a standardized product?

## Key Considerations

### B2B Conference Best Practices
1. **Topics must be specific**: No vague trend discussions—frame concrete questions
2. **Speakers must have hands-on experience**: Emphasize frontline experience over titles
3. **In-depth exchange**: Keep conference size moderate to ensure quality interaction
4. **Business perspective**: This is not a technical conference—it's about business and product strategy
5. **Actionable content**: Everything shared should be repeatable and applicable
