# Web3 Large-scale Exhibitions/Expos Knowledge Base

## Scenario Overview

Large-scale exhibitions/expos are the most impactful and largest-scale event formats in the Web3 industry, bringing together global blockchain projects, investment institutions, developers, and industry media. Such events typically include diverse segments like booth displays, keynote speeches, panel discussions, and product launches.

## Time Compression Strategies

### Loose Mode (30+ days)
- Exhibitor recruitment can be extended to 8-10 days
- Innovative elements like metaverse sub-venues can be added
- Ample time for multiple rounds of rehearsals

### Compact Mode (15-29 days)
- Exhibitor recruitment compressed to 5-6 days
- Parallel processing of booth construction and equipment procurement
- Simplification of on-chain experience segments

### Urgent Mode (8-14 days)
- Limitation on the number of exhibitors
- Use of standardized booth solutions
- Retention of only core on-chain check-in functionality

### Extreme Mode (1-7 days)
- Adoption of rapid construction solutions
- Cancellation of customized booths
- Retention of only basic offline functions

## Risk Warnings

- ⚠️ Large-scale exhibitions involve fire safety and security approvals; venue compliance must be confirmed in advance.
- ⚠️ On-chain systems require stress testing in advance.
- ⚠️ High demand for multilingual support; translation teams need to be prepared in advance.
