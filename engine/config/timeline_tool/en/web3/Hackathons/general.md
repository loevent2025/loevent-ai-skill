# Web3 Hackathon Knowledge Base

## Scenario Overview

Hackathons are a core event format in the Web3 industry for fostering innovative projects and discovering developer talent. Participants form teams to complete project development within a limited time, covering various tracks such as smart contracts, DApps, and infrastructure tools. The activities emphasize technical practice, teamwork, and rapid iteration.

## Time Compression Strategies

### Loose Mode (30+ days)
- Multiple rounds of recruitment and screening
- Complete pre-event training cycle
- Ample technical workshop arrangements
- Detailed judging process

### Compact Mode (15-29 days)
- Streamlined recruitment process
- Pre-event training compressed to online format
- Reduced number of workshops
- Parallel handling of judge invitation and track design

### Urgent Mode (8-14 days)
- Targeted invitation of known developers
- Use of existing development templates
- Simplified tracks focusing on core directions
- Judges primarily participate online

### Extreme Mode (1-7 days)
- Mini-hackathon format
- Single track
- Small-scale targeted invitations
- Rapid judging mode

## Risk Warnings

- ⚠️ Development environment stability is crucial; stress testing should be conducted in advance
- ⚠️ Participating projects involve smart contract security risks; audit guidance should be provided
- ⚠️ Emergency plans for network failures need to be prepared
- ⚠️ Advance agreement on intellectual property ownership is necessary
