# Web3 Hackathon Knowledge Base – General Track

## Overview
General Web3 hackathons are open, all-inclusive development competitions welcoming developers and Web3 enthusiasts of all backgrounds. With no restrictions on tech stack or application domain, participants are free to build whatever they envision. These events offer broad reach, an accessible entry bar, and serve as an ideal platform for Web3 development skill-building, technical exploration, and early-stage project incubation. They are also an important channel for ecosystem partners to discover and attract talented Builders.

---

## Event Profile
- **Scale**: Tens to hundreds of participants; large-scale hackathons can reach thousands
- **Duration**: Typically 24–72 hours of intensive development + showcase and judging
- **Format**: Team building + mentor guidance + product demo + judging and awards
- **Participants**: Developers, product managers, designers, founders, students
- **Defining characteristics**: Highly open format, diverse tracks, wide creative space

## Participation Tips

### Pre-Event Preparation
- Study the technical documentation and SDK of your target blockchain
- Research past winning projects to understand judging criteria
- Set up your development environment and wallets
- Brainstorm a few backup project directions and stay flexible
- Review sponsor track requirements and bounty specifications

### Project Selection
- Solve a real on-chain problem rather than show off technical complexity
- Leverage the unique advantages of blockchain: decentralization, transparency, composability
- Make sure the demo clearly communicates the on-chain value delivered
- Think about the project's scalability potential
- Differentiation is critical — what makes your approach unique?

### Team Formation
- Ideal mix: smart contract developer + frontend developer + product/design
- Smart contract security awareness is critical
- Teammates with domain expertise are a significant advantage
- Complementary skills beat headcount every time

### Demo Tips
- Hook the audience in the first 30 seconds
- Clearly articulate what problem you're solving
- Showcase a working, on-chain product
- Highlight the unique value that blockchain enables
- Acknowledge limitations honestly — judges respect that
