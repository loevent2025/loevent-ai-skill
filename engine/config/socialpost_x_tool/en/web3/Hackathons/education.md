# HACKATHONS × EDUCATION

## Use Cases
Beginner hackathons, learning-focused hackathons, bootcamp hackathons, university hackathons, guided onboarding hackathons, skill-building competitions, mentor-intensive events

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale:** 30–200 people
- **Duration:** 24–48 hours
- **Format:** Learning + hands-on practice, heavy mentor involvement, beginner-friendly judging
- **Core Value:** Skill development, practical experience, mentor guidance, onboarding pathway

## Audience Targeting
- **Primary Audience:** Beginner Developers — people who want to learn Web3 through hands-on building
- **Secondary Audience:** Students — CS and related-major students looking to build portfolio projects
- **Tertiary Audience:** Switching Developers — developers transitioning from Web2
- **Extended Audience:** Non-tech Learners — product managers and operators who want to understand development
- **Audience Behavior:** Learning new skills, gaining practical project experience, receiving mentor guidance, building developer networks

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Pure Beginner:** Primarily newcomers — best for entry-level hackathons
- **Mixed Level:** Beginners paired with experienced developers — best for mentorship-focused formats

## Content Elements
- Learning objectives
- Mentor setup (ratio and backgrounds)
- Pre-event learning resources
- Judging criteria (beginner-friendly)
- Workshop and teaching segments
- Post-event learning pathways

## Language Tone
- Lower the psychological barrier to entry
- Emphasize "learning" over "competing"
- Mentor support is the core selling point
- Judging criteria should feel approachable (prioritize participation and growth)
- Encourage experimentation and learning from mistakes

## Content Structure
- Opening info block: 15%
- Learning value block: 35% — what you'll learn + mentor setup
- Event schedule block: 25% — Workshop + Hacking + Judging
- Participation details block: 15% — who it's for + requirements
- Call-to-action block: 10%
- Emoji density: 12–14 total, friendly and encouraging

## Emotional Tone
Enthusiastic, supportive, learning-centered. "Your first hackathon — we've got your back the whole way."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Beginner Friendly
- **Best for:** Newcomer-only events, entry-level hackathons

## Notes

### Key Principles for Education Hackathons
1. **Lower the psychological barrier:** Repeatedly reinforce "beginner-friendly" and "mentor support"
2. **Be specific about learning outcomes:** Tell people exactly what they'll walk away with
3. **Make judging criteria approachable:** Prioritize learning, participation, and growth
4. **Provide enough mentors:** This is the core selling point — don't understaff it
