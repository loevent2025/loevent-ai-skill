## Evaluation Criteria

### Criterion 1: Business Image & Positioning

**Definition:** Whether the venue's external image (brand, interior design, location) matches the event's business caliber and audience expectations. The AI Agent industry blends technology innovation with business professionalism—venue image should avoid the extremes of "overly traditional and outdated" and "low-cost and casual."

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Venue brand recognition (whether the brand is known and respected by the target audience)
- Business relevance and tech industry association of the location (tech park/CBD > traditional CBD only > remote locations)
- Lobby and common area design quality—should convey a sense of technology and modernity, rather than traditional hotel-style luxury
- Types of events the venue has previously hosted (whether it has hosted AI/tech B2B events, not just traditional industry conferences or academic forums)
- For high-end events targeting enterprise CXOs and investors, lean toward premium business positioning; for events targeting developers and product managers, prioritize a sense of innovation over traditional prestige

---

### Criterion 2: AV & Presentation Infrastructure

**Definition:** Whether the venue's hardware can support core event segments such as live AI Agent product demos, keynote addresses, and customer case study presentations. AI Agent product demos typically involve real-time API calls, multi-turn dialogue demonstrations, and workflow showcases, requiring higher projection fluency and network performance than standard business presentations.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Projector/LED screen resolution and size (AI product demos often display code interfaces, dialogue UIs, and data dashboards—clarity requirements are higher than for slide presentations; screen size must ensure visibility from the back row)
- Audio system type (built-in venue system vs. external professional system required)
- Microphone setup (handheld, lapel, and tabletop mic count)
- Video conferencing equipment (cameras, codecs, support for mainstream platforms—remote customers or international teams joining is common)
- Multi-camera recording capabilities (AI B2B events frequently require recordings for subsequent content marketing)
- Simultaneous interpretation equipment and interpreter booths (for events with international customers or investors)
- Dedicated demo conditions: independent large screen for live product demos, external device connectivity (laptops/tablets), support for rapid screen switching (between multiple products or customer case studies)
- Backup contingency conditions (ability to quickly switch to pre-recorded demo videos in case of network interruption)

---

### Criterion 3: Network Infrastructure

**Definition:** Whether the network environment can support real-time AI Agent product demos, API calls, large-scale video conferencing, and similar scenarios. AI Agent product demos are extremely network-dependent—real-time LLM API calls, multi-step workflow demonstrations, and streaming dialogue output all require stable, low-latency connectivity.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Total bandwidth and upload bandwidth (must support real-time AI product API calls, LLM inference responses, and streaming output demonstrations)
- Whether the stage/demo area has dedicated wired network access (wireless networks pose excessive risk during demo scenarios)
- Network latency stability (during live AI Agent dialogue demos, API response latency directly impacts audience experience—delays exceeding 2 seconds are unacceptable in a business demo context)
- Concurrent device capacity (all attendees plus operational devices connected simultaneously)
- Support for dedicated SSID/VLAN setup (isolating demo network from public Wi-Fi to ensure demo bandwidth is not throttled)
- Network access restrictions (some enterprise or campus networks block specific API ports or overseas access—confirm that the AI product's required API services can be accessed normally)
- 4G/5G signal coverage (as an emergency backup option)
- Whether early venue access is allowed for network stress testing (the cost of a failed demo at a B2B event is extremely high—advance verification is essential)

---

### Criterion 4: Spatial Layout Flexibility

**Definition:** Whether the venue space can be flexibly configured for multi-functional zones including the main hall, breakout sessions, product demo areas, and business meeting areas. AI Agent B2B conferences often include multiple segments—keynotes, demos, networking, and business discussions—requiring natural transitions between different activities.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Whether the main hall supports multiple layout configurations (theater, classroom, roundtable, U-shape, boardroom)
- Number of independent breakout session spaces (whether 2-4 separable meeting rooms are available)
- Soundproofing between spaces (concurrent breakout sessions must not interfere with each other)
- Product demo/showcase area location and size (whether independent booths can be set up, each with dedicated power and network access—AI Agent product showcases typically require a laptop, large screen, and stable network)
- Whether the networking/refreshment area is a dedicated open space (not a hallway or corridor; the networking segment is a core opportunity for building business relationships at AI B2B events)
- Privacy of business meeting corners (1-on-1 business discussions or investor conversations should remain undisturbed)
- Whether the registration area is positioned at the entrance with clear wayfinding
- Furniture mobility (whether tables and chairs are fixed or can be rearranged)

**Layout Type Quick Reference:**

| Layout Type | Best For | Headcount | Characteristics |
|-------------|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Roundtable | Executive sessions, Investor networking | 10-25 | Facilitates in-depth discussion |
| Boardroom | Formal business meetings, Strategic discussions | 15-40 | Professional and authoritative |
| Theater | Product launches, Keynote addresses | 50-500 | High capacity, speaker-focused |
| Classroom | Training-style customer sessions, Note-taking required | 30-150 | Supports note-taking and hands-on practice |
| U-Shape | Interactive workshops, Small customer meetings | 20-50 | Facilitates interaction |

---

### Criterion 5: Space Capacity

**Definition:** Whether the area and occupancy of each functional zone meets the event's scale requirements.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Actual capacity of the main hall under the target layout (not maximum capacity—calculate based on the intended layout)
- Whether individual breakout room capacity matches breakout session attendance
- Product showcase area size (estimate based on number of booths × per-booth area; AI Agent product booths typically require a desk, screen, and operating space—minimum 4 m² per booth)
- Whether the networking/refreshment area can accommodate all attendees simultaneously (avoid overcrowding that impairs business networking)
- Whether ceiling height meets stage construction and lighting/audio rigging requirements
- Fire safety capacity (venue's officially certified maximum occupancy)

---

### Criterion 6: Transportation & Accessibility

**Definition:** How conveniently attendees can reach the venue—this directly impacts attendance rate and participant experience.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Walking distance to the nearest metro/bus station
- Number of parking spaces and parking convenience for those driving
- Travel time from major transportation hubs such as airports and high-speed rail stations (especially critical when a high proportion of attendees are out-of-town customers or investors)
- Nearby hotel availability (essential for large-scale summits requiring accommodation)
- Ride-hailing pickup and drop-off convenience (whether the area is congested during peak hours)
- Venue entrance visibility (easy to locate, with clear signage)
- Nearby dining options (availability of business lunch venues and post-event dinner options)

---

### Criterion 7: Service Quality & Support

**Definition:** Whether the venue's services can support the operational needs and attendee experience of an AI Agent B2B event.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- On-site technical support responsiveness (whether a dedicated AV technician is available on-site; technical issues during AI product demo segments require rapid response)
- Catering and refreshment service (in-house catering vs. external catering allowed vs. designated vendor—assess flexibility)
- Venue cooperation with event setup (whether early access for setup is permitted, whether external production teams are allowed on-site)
- Independent HVAC control (zone-based temperature control, self-adjustable during sessions)
- Adjustable lighting (zone control, brightness switching—product demo segments require dimmed ambient lighting to highlight screen displays, while keynote segments require stage lighting coordination)
- Venue emergency response capabilities (response protocols for power outages, network failures, and equipment malfunctions)
- Cleaning efficiency and refreshment area turnaround between sessions

---

### Criterion 8: Brand Identity Alignment

**Definition:** How well the venue's spatial character aligns with the host organization's brand image. AI Agent companies typically project a brand identity of "cutting-edge technology + product-driven + innovation," rather than academic rigor or traditional corporate formality.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Whether the overall space design conveys a "tech innovation" signal (modern design/tech park/innovation space > traditional hotel ballroom/academic lecture hall/government conference center)
- Whether customized branding is permitted (backdrops, directional signage, brand color application, product visual displays)
- Whether the venue's own brand identity conflicts with the host (e.g., a space named after a competitor, or a venue with a completely mismatched identity)
- Visual quality of photography/livestream backgrounds (AI B2B event content is frequently used for subsequent social media distribution and content marketing)
- Whether the space can convey the host's intended brand message (cutting-edge, professional, product excellence, open ecosystem, etc.)
- For events targeting enterprise customers and investors, the space must combine professional business credibility with tech innovation appeal—both are indispensable

---

### Criterion 9: Privacy & Information Security

**Definition:** Whether the venue can meet the privacy and information security requirements of business negotiations, product roadmap discussions, and investor communications.

**Evaluation Areas:**
- Whether the meeting space is independently enclosed (not an open co-working space)
- Soundproofing quality (discussions involving unreleased product details, commercial terms, and funding progress must not leak)
- Whether a dedicated VIP corridor or executive entrance is available (for a premium experience when key customers or investors arrive)
- Venue policies on recording and livestreaming
- Network security (whether encrypted networks are supported, whether public network isolation is available—AI product demos often involve internal APIs and unreleased features)
- Whether executive session segments can be independently isolated with controlled access

## Default Priority Ranking

> **Note:** The following is the standard priority ranking for AI Agent B2B conference scenarios. The model should dynamically adjust based on the specific event's audience profile, brand positioning, and event format.

**Hard Requirements (must be met—otherwise disqualify immediately):**
1. Space Capacity — If it cannot accommodate the attendees, nothing else matters
2. AV & Presentation Infrastructure — Product demos and keynote addresses are the core event segments
3. Network Infrastructure — AI Agent product demos are extremely network-dependent; a failed demo carries business consequences far beyond technical embarrassment
4. Transportation & Accessibility — Directly impacts customer and investor attendance rate

**Nice-to-Have (beneficial if met, can be weighed against trade-offs):**
5. Business Image & Positioning — Varies with audience sensitivity; elevate to hard requirement for CXO/investor-facing events, may be deprioritized for developer-focused events
6. Spatial Layout Flexibility — Lower requirement for single-keynote events; higher requirement for multi-segment events (keynotes + demos + networking)
7. Service Quality & Support — Impacts experience but can be supplemented with an in-house operations team
8. Brand Identity Alignment — A nice-to-have, but elevate to hard requirement for brand launch events
9. Privacy & Information Security — Lower requirement for open summits; elevate to hard requirement for events involving unreleased products or funding discussions

## Hybrid Scenario Guidelines

When an AI Agent B2B conference is combined with other formats (e.g., "morning summit + afternoon workshop" or "summit with an embedded Demo Day showcase"), the model should:

1. Take the **union** of evaluation criteria from each scenario's knowledge base
2. For overlapping criteria, apply the **higher standard**
3. For conflicting criteria, flag the **priority and trade-off recommendations** (e.g., a summit requires a polished business atmosphere vs. a workshop requires a hands-on operational setup—resolve this through spatial zoning rather than choosing a single compromise venue)
