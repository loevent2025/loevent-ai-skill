# Café × General (AI / Model Commercialization Edition)

## Applicable Scenarios
Themed café gatherings, interest group coffee hours, weekend cowork cafés, remote workers cafés, freelancer café days, themed pop-up cafés

## Scenario Characteristics
- **Format**: Café setting, casual gathering, cowork or chat as you please, drop-in friendly
- **Core Value**: A change of scenery for work or socializing, meeting like-minded people, the comfort of a good café

## Audience Segmentation
- **Primary Audience:** People who want a change of scene — remote workers tired of working from home
- **Secondary Audience:** People looking to connect around a shared interest — want someone to talk to about a topic they care about
- **Tertiary Audience:** Café lovers — people who just enjoy a good café atmosphere
- **Audience Behavior:** Cowork, drink coffee, chat, meet people

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style

### Must Follow
- ✅ Relaxed, chill, comfortable
- ✅ The warmth of a café setting
- ✅ Low barrier, low pressure
- ✅ Scene-first

### Absolute Prohibitions
- ❌ Don't make it feel formal
- ❌ Don't emphasize "value" or "takeaways"
- ❌ No agenda or structured segments
- ❌ Avoid transactional framing

---

## Content Enhancement Elements

### 1. Café Scene Opening Hook (Required)
**Formula**: [Café atmosphere] + [what you'll do] + [relaxed feel]

**Examples**:
- "Find a café and work alongside a group of people"
- "Saturday afternoon, a café, some good conversation"
- "Working from home is getting old — come find a change of scene"

### 2. Sensory Immersion (Include at Least 3)
- **Visual**: Warm lighting, wooden tables, plants, open laptops
- **Taste**: Pour-over, latte, pastries, brunch
- **Sound**: Background music, the espresso machine, quiet conversation
- **Touch**: A comfortable chair, the warmth of a coffee cup
- **Atmosphere**: Comfortable, chill, focused but loose

### 3. Time Flow Description (Recommended)
**Example**:
```
14:00  Walk in, order a coffee
15:00  Some people working, some people chatting
16:00  Maybe drift into a conversation
17:00  Those who need to go, go — those who want to stay, stay
```

### 4. Flexible Participation (Required)
- "Stay all day or pop in for a coffee and leave"
- "Work if you want, chat if you want"
- "No agenda — come whenever"

### 5. Venue Description (Recommended)
- What makes the café special (great pour-over, excellent pastries, lots of seating)
- Ambiance notes (quiet, good for work, has an outdoor terrace)
- Practical info (power outlets available, solid WiFi)

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Remote Workers Café
- **Best for:** Cowork sessions for remote workers
- **Opening format:** ☕💻 Remote Café | Work somewhere different today
- **Tone:** Companionable, focused, chill

### Variant B: Themed Chat Café
- **Best for:** Casual gatherings centered around a shared topic
- **Opening format:** ☕💬 [Topic] Café | Coffee and conversation
- **Tone:** Relaxed, sharing, like-minded people

### Variant C: Weekend Café Hangout
- **Best for:** Low-key weekend social gatherings
- **Opening format:** ☕✨ Weekend Coffee Hours | No agenda, just hanging out
- **Tone:** Leisurely, no pressure, weekend energy

## Notes & Best Practices

### General Café Checklist
1. **The café atmosphere is the core**: Scene-first in all copy
2. **Low barrier, low pressure**: No sign-up required, come and go as you please
3. **Flexible participation**: Cowork, chat, or just sit — all valid
4. **Practical venue info**: What makes this café worth coming to, power outlets, WiFi
5. **No agenda**: This is the key distinction from a meetup

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Don't write it like an event or a meetup
- ❌ No structured segments
- ❌ Don't make it feel formal
- ❌ Don't emphasize what attendees will "gain" or "take away"
