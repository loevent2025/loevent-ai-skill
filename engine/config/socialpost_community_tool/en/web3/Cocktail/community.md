# Web3 Cocktail Party — Community Event Knowledge Base

## 📌 Module Activation Criteria

Apply the rules in this module when the event theme involves the following keywords:
- Community Builder Night, ecosystem social mixer, DAO member party
- Hackathon After Party, community celebration mixer, Builder Mixer
- Ecosystem anniversary party, community milestone celebration, contributor appreciation night
- OG meetup, community reunion, Frens Night

---

## 🎯 Scenario Overview

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Format | Free-form socializing + lightweight rituals + party atmosphere + community bonding |
| Duration | 2–4 hours, relaxed and free-flowing vibe |
| Participants | Community Builders, ecosystem developers, DAO members, community newcomers, OGs |
| Core Objective | Community bonding, Builder downtime, fostering belonging, culture building |

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style Override

When a cocktail-party-style community event is detected:

✅ Relaxed, party energy, community warmth
✅ Conversational, rhythmic, Builder culture
✅ Scene first, atmosphere leads
✅ Community connections happen naturally — don't force the word "networking"
✅ WAGMI spirit throughout, but don't overdo the slogans

❌ Do NOT over-emphasize "resource matchmaking," "partnership opportunities," or "expanding your network"
❌ Do NOT write it like a formal community meeting or work report
❌ Avoid transactional language — keep the community vibe authentic
❌ Do NOT create a sense of distance between OGs and newcomers

**Core Principle**:
🎯 This is a community party. Frens are the main characters. Connections happen naturally. A sense of belonging is the best takeaway.

## 📝 Required Content Elements

### 1. Scene-First Opening (required)

Lead with the venue's "party feel" elements:

**Formula**:
```
[Venue atmosphere] + [Social / party elements] + [Community feel]
```

**Key Points**:
- ❌ Don't write: "We will be hosting a community gathering"
- ✅ Do write: the venue, the drinks, that feeling of "frens coming together"
- Make it feel like a community party, not a community meeting

---

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (include at least 3)

**Sight**:
- Lighting: "warm glow," "neon lights," "candlelight," "city lights through the windows"
- Space: "rooftop terrace," "industrial loft," "open bar area"
- Community elements: "community memes scrolling on the projector," "walls covered in Builder artwork"

**Taste**:
- Be specific: "ice-cold craft beers," "signature cocktails," "pizza and finger food"
- ❌ Avoid: "refreshments will be provided"
- ✅ Do write: "beers are already on ice, and the pizza is on its way"

**Sound**:
- Music: "chill beats," "lo-fi hip hop," "DJ spinning the community-voted playlist"
- Ambient sounds: "clinking glasses," "laughter," "someone in the corner chatting about their latest project"

**Touch**:
- Temperature: "the warm buzz of being slightly tipsy," "evening breeze on the terrace"
- Connection: "finally shaking hands with someone you've been chatting with online for a year," "hugging the person who helped you debug that contract"

**Atmosphere**:
- Energy level: "chill," "relaxed," "slightly buzzed," "vibes are just right," "energy keeps building"
- Social dynamics: "small clusters forming," "someone just met and is already deep in conversation," "OGs and newcomers mingling together"

---

### 3. Time-Lapse Narrative (recommended)

Use fragmented timeline moments to create party rhythm:

**Format**:
```
[Time] [What's happening right now] — short, community-flavored
```

**Key Points**:
- Show the natural flow from "arriving" to "not wanting to leave"
- Weave in community highlight moments (raffle, group photo, impromptu sharing)
- Make it feel like a party with rhythm, not a scheduled agenda

---

### 4. Social Snapshot (required)

Describe "what IS happening," not "what COULD happen":

**Key Points**:
- ❌ "Attendees will have the opportunity to freely mingle"
- ✅ "Someone at the bar just met and they're already deep in conversation; someone on the couch finally met the fren they've been chatting with online all year"

- ❌ "Networking opportunities will be available"
- ✅ "Conversations happen naturally — you might meet your next collaborator while grabbing a refill"

**Use dynamic verbs**:
"Clinking glasses," "hugging," "finally meeting IRL," "swapping Twitter handles," "chatting until nobody wants to leave," "heading out together for round two"

---

### 5. Organic Community Connections (recommended)

Don't force it — let the connections be part of the backdrop:

**Key Points**:
- ❌ "Expand your professional network"
- ✅ "That person you've been chatting with online for a year? Tonight you finally clink glasses face to face"

- ❌ "Discover collaboration opportunities"
- ✅ "One conversation leads to another, and suddenly you're cooking up something together"

- Connections happen organically — no need for transactional framing
- Use details and scenes to convey community warmth

---

### 6. Community Rituals (recommended)

Lightweight but warm community moments:

**Key Points**:
- Raffle moment: "Not a formal raffle — just everyone raising their drinks and shouting a number"
- Group photo moment: "Everyone squeezing in, yelling 'WAGMI,' then one more shot"
- Gratitude moment: "No speeches — just raising a glass and saying thanks to everyone"
- Impromptu sharing: "Anyone who wants to say a few words can hop up — no pressure"

---

### 7. Newcomer-Friendly Atmosphere (required)

Make newcomers feel welcomed:

**Key Points**:
- ✅ "OGs and newcomers hanging out together — nobody cares when you joined"
- ✅ "Don't worry if you're shy — everyone here is a fren"
- ✅ "Don't know anyone? No worries — by the end of your first drink, you will"
- ❌ Do NOT create a sense of distance between OGs and newcomers

---

### 8. No-Pressure Participation Framework (required)

Make attendees feel relaxed and low-commitment:

**Key Points**:
- ✅ "No prep needed — just bring yourself"
- ✅ "Want to talk projects? Cool. Just want to chill and have a drink? Also cool"
- ✅ "Come or don't — but if you happen to be free"
- ✅ "Stay as long as you like — when the vibe's right, you won't want to leave"

- ❌ "This is a rare community event"
- ❌ "Don't miss out"

---

### 9. Community Spirit Expression (recommended)

Integrate naturally — don't over-slogan:

**Key Points**:
- ✅ "GM frens," "WAGMI," "LFG" — use naturally
- ✅ "A bunch of people doing interesting things in Web3"
- ✅ "The community exists because of you"
- ❌ Don't slogan every sentence — keep it natural

---

### 10. Crowd Description — Focus on "Frens" (recommended)

Don't describe by job title — describe by community identity:

**Key Points**:
- ❌ "Founders, investors, developers"
- ✅ "A bunch of frens building in this ecosystem"
- ✅ "Coders, designers, community folks, and some who are purely vibing"
- ✅ "You'll meet some interesting people — what exactly they do? You'll find out when you chat"

## ✅ Cocktail Party Community Event Copy Quality Checklist

- [ ] **Scene first**: Venue / party atmosphere is the opening hook — not "We will be hosting..."
- [ ] **Five-sense details**: At least 3 sensory details included
- [ ] **Party feel**: Overall atmosphere reads as community party, not community meeting
- [ ] **Dynamic portrayal**: Social scenes are happening now, not "could potentially happen"
- [ ] **Organic connections**: Don't force it — let the details speak
- [ ] **Newcomer-friendly**: Newcomers feel welcomed — no OG gatekeeping vibes
- [ ] **Relaxed tone**: Casual, community-driven, conversational throughout
- [ ] **Non-transactional**: No over-emphasis on "connections," "resources," or "opportunities"
- [ ] **Low pressure**: Participation is framed as easy and optional
- [ ] **Community spirit**: WAGMI / GM woven in naturally — not over-sloganed
- [ ] **Belonging**: Implies this is a recurring community gathering with continuity

---

## ⚠️ Prohibited Actions

- ❌ Writing a community party as if it were a formal meeting or work report
- ❌ Over-emphasizing "professional connections," "partnership opportunities," or other transactional language
- ❌ Creating a sense of distance between OGs and newcomers
- ❌ Using pressure tactics like "Don't miss out" or "This is a rare opportunity"
- ❌ Writing it as a structured agenda instead of a party with rhythm
- ❌ Pushing project or product promotion that disrupts the social atmosphere
- ❌ Overdoing the slogans and losing the natural feel
- ❌ Implying excessive drinking or inappropriate behavior
- ❌ Over-commercializing a community event

---

## 🚫 Banned Vocabulary

**Transactional language**:
❌ "Expand your network," "resource matchmaking," "partnership opportunities"
❌ "Deep exchange," "high-quality networking," "make connections"
❌ "Actionable insights," "value," "takeaways"

**Pressure tactics**:
❌ "Don't miss out," "rare opportunity," "limited spots"
❌ "Community extravaganza," "event of the year" (unless it actually is)

**Distance-creating language**:
❌ "Industry heavyweights," "all-star lineup," "VIP guests"
