# Web3 Cafe Cursor — Technical Events Knowledge Base

## 🎯 Scenario Overview

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Format | Café setting + BYOL (Bring Your Own Laptop) coworking + Tech chitchat + Lightweight demos |
| Duration | 3–6 hours, flexible time slots, come and go as you please |
| Participants | Protocol developers, smart contract engineers, tech enthusiasts, project technical teams |
| Core Objective | Technical co-creation, developer connections, project exchange, tool exploration |

---

## 🎨 Tone & Style Override

When a Cafe Cursor technical event is detected:

✅ Relaxed, geeky, café atmosphere
✅ "Sipping coffee while writing code" vibe
✅ Tech exchange is natural conversation, not a formal session
✅ Demos are "check out what I built," not a pitch
✅ Flexible timing — come and go freely

❌ Do not write it like a formal tech conference or workshop
❌ No rigid agenda
❌ Avoid transactional language like "technical insights" or "intensive learning"
❌ Do not create a technical barrier — newcomers should feel welcome

**Core Principle**:
🎯 This is a "bring your laptop to the café and code together" tech social space. Coffee is the medium, code is the conversation, and connections happen naturally.

## 📝 Required Content Elements

### 1. Café Atmosphere Opening (mandatory)

Lead with the "coffee + code" vibe:

**Formula**:
```
[Café setting] + [Bring your laptop] + [Coding together feel]
```

**Guidelines**:
- ❌ Don't write: "We are hosting a technical exchange event"
- ✅ Do write: "Bring your laptop — let's code at the café together"
- Make people feel this is a relaxed coworking space

---

### 2. Five-Sense Immersion (include at least 3)

**Visual**:
- Space: "sunlight streaming into the café," "a long table lined with MacBooks," "someone sketching architecture on the whiteboard"
- Tech elements: "code running on screens," "someone debugging a contract," "terminal windows flickering"

**Taste**:
- Be specific: "pour-over coffee," "lattes," "free refills on Americanos"
- ❌ Avoid: "Beverages provided"
- ✅ Do write: "Coffee's on us — fuel for your code, unlimited refills"

**Sound**:
- Music: "lo-fi beats," "soft background music"
- Ambient sound: "a symphony of keyboard clatter," "someone mutters 'finally squashed that bug'"

**Touch**:
- Temperature: "the café AC set just right"
- Connection: "you start chatting with the person next to you and realize you're both using the same framework"

**Atmosphere**:
- Energy level: "focused but not intense," "chill coding vibe," "some people grinding code, others chatting"
- Social dynamics: "someone with headphones deep in code, others huddled over a technical discussion"

---

### 3. Flexible Time Framework (mandatory)

Flexible timing is core to Cafe Cursor:

**Format**:
```
⏰ Pick a time slot that works for you:
- [Slot A] Morning session — for early-bird coders
- [Slot B] Afternoon session — for post-lunch focus
- [Slot C] Full day — stay as long as you like
- Or just drop by for a coffee and a quick hello
```

**Guidelines**:
- Emphasize "come and go freely"
- No mandatory time commitment
- Dropping in for a coffee and a chat is perfectly fine

---

### 4. Tech Exchange Happens Naturally (recommended)

Tech discussions are conversations, not presentations:

**Guidelines**:
- ❌ "Technical sharing session"
- ✅ "Before you know it, you're comparing frameworks with the person next to you"

- ❌ "In-depth technical exchange"
- ✅ "Someone's stuck on a bug, the person next to them leans over and says 'I've hit that one before'"

---

### 5. Lightweight Demo Atmosphere (recommended)

Demos are "let me show you something," not a pitch:

**Guidelines**:
- ✅ "Want to show off something you've been working on? Just pull it up — people will gather"
- ✅ "Not a formal demo — just 'check out what I built, pretty cool right?'"
- ✅ "Someone's showing a contract they just wrote while others chime in with feedback"
- ❌ "Scheduled demo session" or "project pitch"

---

### 6. Bring Your Laptop (mandatory)

This is the core element of Cafe Cursor:

**Guidelines**:
- ✅ "Bring your laptop"
- ✅ "Got something to code? Great. Don't? That's fine too — come for the conversation"
- ✅ "Pick up where you left off on a project, or start something new"

---

### 7. Newcomer-Friendly Atmosphere (mandatory)

Make tech newcomers feel welcome:

**Guidelines**:
- ✅ "Whether you've been coding for ten years or just getting started — you're welcome"
- ✅ "Got a question? Ask the person next to you — everyone's friendly"
- ✅ "Just soaking in the coding atmosphere is reason enough to come"
- ❌ No technical prerequisites

---

### 8. Compliance Disclaimer (when project demos are involved)

```
"⚠️ Technical exchange event — project demos do not constitute investment advice (NFA)"
```
