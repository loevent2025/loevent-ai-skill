# Web3 Large-Scale Conference Knowledge Base — Community Scene

## Scene Overview
Community-focused Web3 large-scale conferences center on interaction and exchange among builders and enthusiasts, emphasizing the decentralized ethos, peer-to-peer dialogue, and a strong sense of belonging. These events are flexible in scale and diverse in format, serving as key venues for Web3 community building, on-chain experience sharing, and global builder connections. They are also a living expression of Web3 culture, meme spirit, and community self-governance.

---

## Scene Characteristics
- **Scale**: Tens to hundreds of participants
- **Duration**: Typically 2–4 hours; full-day for large Community Day events
- **Format**: Meetup presentations + panel discussions + open networking + themed salons + IRL social gatherings
- **Participants**: Builders, developers, DAO members, entrepreneurs, enthusiasts, and students
- **Defining Traits**: Relaxed atmosphere, high interactivity, decentralized organization, Web3-native social dynamics

## Community Operations Recommendations

### Event Preparation
- Begin promotion 2 weeks in advance (Twitter/X + Telegram + Farcaster)
- Choose a venue that's easy to access and has a relaxed atmosphere
- Prepare check-in and POAP claim workflows
- Plan icebreaker and facilitation activities
- Provide refreshments and light snacks (coffee is a staple)

### Setting the Atmosphere
- Organizers should warmly welcome new builders with a GM
- Design simple icebreakers (e.g., "What are you building?")
- Prepare topic starter cards to spark conversation
- Encourage attendees to share their projects and directions
- Keep the atmosphere relaxed, friendly, and Web3-native

### Community Maintenance
- Post a recap thread and photos after each event
- Build and maintain an active Telegram / Discord community group
- Organize regular online and offline IRL events
- Encourage members to self-organize talks and meetups
- Build and preserve the community's content, stories, and on-chain memories
