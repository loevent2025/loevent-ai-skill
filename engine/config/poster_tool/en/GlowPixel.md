# PIXEL RETROWAVE AESTHETICS

## Applicable Scenarios
Retro-themed parties, synthwave/vaporwave music events, indie game promotions, nostalgia-themed exhibitions, 80s retro nights, tropical resort campaigns, aviation/travel brands, retro electronic music album covers, arcade event activations, vintage tech product launches, pixel art exhibitions, vaporwave culture events

## Color Palette
- **Primary Colors:** Retro neon spectrum — Electric Purple (#7B68EE), Neon Pink (#FF6B9D), Sunset Orange (#FF9F43), Tropical Cyan (#00CED1)
- **Background:** Purple-blue gradient sky, transitioning from Cobalt Blue (#4169E1) at the top to Violet (#8A2BE2) in the mid-range, then to Neon Pink-Orange (#FF7F9F) at the horizon
- **Silhouette Colors:** Deep Purple-Blue (#2E1A47) and Midnight Blue (#191970) for palm tree and building outlines
- **Accent Colors:** Golden Sunlight (#FFD700), Coral Pink (#FF6F61), Mint Cyan (#98FF98) for floral details
- **Saturation:** High saturation (70–100%), targeting a vivid, punchy "neon retro" visual impact
- **Color Behavior:** Sky displays a dramatic purple-pink-orange gradient; ocean reflects sky tones; overall palette is warm and dreamlike

### Recommended Color Combinations
- Tropical Dusk: Cobalt Blue (#4169E1) → Violet (#8A2BE2) → Neon Pink (#FF6B9D) → Sunset Orange (#FF9F43)
- Miami Nights: Deep Purple (#4B0082) → Electric Purple (#9370DB) → Hot Pink (#FF69B4) → Coral (#FF7F50)
- Cyber Coast: Midnight Blue (#191970) → Blue-Violet (#6A5ACD) → Pink Lavender (#DDA0DD) → Peach (#FFDAB9)

## Visual Elements

### Core: Pixelated Rendering
- **Full-frame pixel rendering** — all elements presented on a visible pixel grid at a consistent pixel unit size (recommended: 2–4px units)
- **Limited color palette** — simulates the color constraints of 8-bit/16-bit game consoles, using 3–5 brightness levels per hue
- **Dithering effects** — checkerboard or diagonal pixel dithering at color transitions, emulating gradient rendering on retro displays

### Scene Elements
- **Palm tree silhouettes** — iconic tropical elements in dark outlines, positioned at the foreground edges to frame the composition
- **Sunset / dual suns** — glowing sun(s) at the horizon (dual suns or sci-fi style accepted), with horizontal scanlines or halo effects
- **Ocean / water surface** — purple-blue gradient with pixelated wave texture and light reflections
- **Distant city skyline** — silhouetted futuristic urban outline positioned at the horizon line
- **Tropical flora / flowers** — decorative vegetation at the bottom foreground with rich, vibrant colors
- **Halftone dot pattern** — regular dot arrays in the sky or transition zones for a retro print aesthetic

### Decorative Details
- Pixelated star bursts / cross-shaped sparkles dotting the night sky
- Horizontal scanline overlay simulating a CRT monitor effect
- Simple geometric clouds with sharp, pixelated outlines

## Effect Processing
- Maintain sharp pixel edges throughout — anti-aliasing is strictly prohibited
- Sky gradients rendered via pixel dithering, not smooth blending
- Pixelated halo around the sun, suggested by horizontal line patterns
- Ocean reflections rendered as horizontal pixel strips mirroring sky colors
- Silhouette elements filled with flat solid colors and sharp edges
- Optional subtle CRT scanline overlay (horizontal lines at 5–15% opacity)
- Overall tone skews warm (sunset scenes) or cool (night scenes) — maintain consistency throughout
- Dark border frame along edges optional, simulating a vintage monitor or poster mount effect

## Composition Strategy
- **Classic three-band composition** — sky (top), ocean/horizon (middle), foreground vegetation (bottom)
- Palm trees extending from the bottom or sides to create a natural framing device
- Visual focal point centered on the horizon sun/sunset
- Distant city silhouette adding spatial depth
- Foreground flora adding layering and chromatic richness
- Text areas placed in the sky zone (top) or a bottom information bar
- Dark footer bar can serve as a dedicated text/information area
- Symmetrical or near-symmetrical layout to convey a balanced, classic retro poster feel

## Typography Settings
- **Color Selection:**
  - Sky areas: use Neon Pink (#FF6B9D) or Pale Pink (#FFB6C1) for contrast
  - Dark footer areas: use light-colored text — White (#FFFFFF), Light Cyan (#E0FFFF), or Pale Pink (#FFC0CB)
  - Emphasis text may use a pink-to-cyan gradient, but must be pixelated
- **Fill Method:** Solid color fill — single color, no gradients
- **Prohibited Effects:**
  - No gradient fills (unless pixelated headline treatment)
  - No shadows / drop shadows
  - No outer glow / inner glow
  - No outlines (unless pixel-style stroke)
  - No blur effects
  - No smooth text effects of any kind
- **Typeface Style:**
  - Main headline: Pixelated script / cursive with a neon tube aesthetic; horizontal scanlines may pass through
  - Subheadlines / info text: Pixel-style bold sans-serif, angular, 8-bit game font aesthetic
- **General Principle:** Typography must maintain a sharp pixel aesthetic consistent with the overall pixel art style — avoid any smooth anti-aliasing effects
