# Cross-Sector Collaboration × Investment / Event Playbook
*(AI / Model Commercialization Edition)*

## Applicable Scenarios
Cross-industry investor exchange forums, traditional corporate capital × tech investment dialogues, Web2 × Web3 investment summits, corporate capital × VC joint salons, cross-sector investment thematic conferences, multi-capital cross-sector dialogues

## Scenario Profile
- **Format:** Cross-sector investment sharing, multi-capital dialogue, cross-domain deal introductions, industry resource collision
- **Core Value:** Cross-sector investment perspectives, multi-capital networking, industry resource matching, expanded investment frameworks

## Audience Definition
- **Primary Audience:** Cross-sector investors — investors looking to understand opportunity in an adjacent field
- **Secondary Audience:** Cross-sector founders — entrepreneurial teams that need capital from multiple domains
- **Tertiary Audience:** Corporate/strategic capital — enterprise investment arms seeking cross-sector opportunities
- **Extended Audience:** FAs and incubators — startup service organizations focused on cross-sector ventures
- **Audience Behavior:** Expanding investment horizons, discovering cross-sector opportunities, building diverse networks, learning cross-domain investment judgment

### Common Audience Configurations
- **Investor-core:** VC + corporate capital + angels — suited for investor exchange events
- **Capital matching:** Investors + cross-sector founders — suited for deal introduction events
- **Industry convergence:** Corporate capital + VC + industry partners — suited for industry investment events

## Cross-Sector Content Enhancement Elements (Required)

### 1. Contrast and Tension
**Narrative formula:**
"When [investors focused on Domain X] meet [investors focused on Domain Y] — what different opportunities do they each see?"

**Required elements:**
- Clearly articulate the different focus areas of the investor types present
- Use contrast framing: "VCs evaluate technical potential; corporate investors evaluate deployment capability"
- Emphasize the scarcity and value of this cross-sector perspective combination

### 2. Chemistry Description
**Use dynamic verbs:**
- ❌ "cooperate," "invest," "connect"
- ✅ "collide," "connect," "catalyze," "empower," "reshape"

**Core question:** "Why does a cross-sector perspective surface opportunities that investors in a single domain simply cannot see?"

### 3. Participant Collision Description
- [Tech sector] investors encounter [traditional industry] capital
- [Early-stage VCs] meet [growth-stage PE investors]
- [Pure financial investors] go head-to-head with [strategic corporate investors]

### 4. Scene and Atmosphere Building (Select at least 2)
- **Concrete scene:** Different investment theses clashing over the same deal; one project, two completely different readings
- **Sense of time:** From individual perspectives to mutual understanding to discovering shared opportunity
- **Dynamic interaction:** Debates over investment logic; discussions about divergent project assessments
- **Emotional energy:** The surprise of discovering a blind spot; the excitement of a cognitive framework being expanded

### 5. Unique Outcomes to Imagine
List the investment opportunities that can only be found through a cross-sector capital perspective

### 6. Transformation Arc (Recommended)
Describe the shift from "I don't understand that domain" to "I had no idea there were opportunities like this"

## Notes & Best Practices

### Key Principles for Cross-Sector Investment Events
1. **Diverse investor lineup is the foundation:** Multiple investor types from genuinely different backgrounds are required
2. **NFA disclaimer is non-negotiable:** Any investment-related event must include a compliance notice
3. **Perspective collisions must be specific:** Use real scenarios or case studies to illustrate the differences
4. **Go beyond simple deal introductions:** The focus should be the exchange and collision of investment thinking
5. **Surface the opportunity clearly:** Help participants see the tangible value of a cross-sector perspective
6. **Apply appropriate participation criteria:** Investor exchange events should screen for relevance and quality
