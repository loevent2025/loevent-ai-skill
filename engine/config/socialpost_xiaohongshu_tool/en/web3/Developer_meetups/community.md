# WEB3 DEVELOPER MEETUPS × COMMUNITY

## Use Cases
Web3 community reunions, DAO community anniversaries, core contributor gatherings, community organizer meetups, Builder guild events

## Event Characteristics
- **Format**: Community gathering + member networking + DAO governance discussions + social activities
- **Core Value**: Community belonging, reconnecting with old friends, DAO co-building, sustained connection

## Audience Profile
- **Primary Audience**: Core community contributors — maintain relationships, participate in governance
- **Secondary Audience**: Active builders — deep engagement, build a sense of belonging
- **Tertiary Audience**: New members — integrate into the community, meet OGs
- **Extended Audience**: Other community organizers — share experiences, cross-community collaboration
- **Audience Behavior**: Maintain relationships, participate in governance, contribute code / content, build social connections

### Common Audience Combinations
- **Core-focused**: Core + active members, ideal for in-depth exchange and governance discussions
- **Open-focused**: All members, ideal for community expansion and onboarding new members

## Content Elements
- Community identity activation (on-chain identity / NFT / SBT)
- Community retrospective / on-chain data and progress updates
- Contributor sharing sessions
- Community governance / DAO development discussions
- Social activities
- Future roadmap planning

## Tone & Language
- Conversational, community-oriented language with Web3 vernacular
- Emphasize "we," "OG," "fren," "community"
- Warmth, belonging, and WAGMI spirit
- Feeling of reconnecting with long-time friends
- Participatory, DAO co-building energy

## Content Structure
- Opening Hook: 15% — Community identity and belonging
- Community Content Block: 30% — Retrospectives, on-chain progress
- Member Exchange Block: 30% — Sharing, governance discussions
- Social Activity Block: 15%
- Call to Action Block: 10%
- Emoji Density: 14–18 total, warm and welcoming in tone

## Emotional Atmosphere
Warm, belonging, OG energy. The feeling that "community is home — WAGMI."

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Community Reunion
- **Best For**: Regular community member gatherings
- **Opening Format**: 💙 {Community Name} Reunion | GM frens — it's been too long
- **Tone**: Warm, familiar, old friends

### Variant B: DAO Building Session
- **Best For**: Meetups focused on community governance and development
- **Opening Format**: 🏠 {Community Name} Co-Build Session | Let's make the DAO even better, together
- **Tone**: Collaborative, participatory, governance-focused

### Variant C: Anniversary Celebration
- **Best For**: Community or protocol launch anniversary events
- **Opening Format**: 🎉 {Community Name} — One Year Strong! | Thank you to every Builder who showed up
- **Tone**: Celebratory, appreciative, reflective

## Attendee Tips

### For Core Contributors
- Come with ideas on community governance
- Share your contributor story and how you feel about the journey
- Help new members get settled and onboarded
- Take ownership of a community task or bounty

### For New Members
- Introduce yourself proactively — share your on-chain identity
- Engage actively with OGs
- Learn the community's culture and history
- Find where you can contribute (code, content, design)

## Key Reminders

### Community Meetup Essentials
1. **Belonging is the core**: Make members feel "this is our DAO"
2. **OG energy**: Nurture long-term relationships — through bear and bull alike
3. **Participation**: Involve members in governance and community building
4. **Recognize contributors**: Make sure contributions are visible (POAPs, shoutouts)
5. **Long-term mindset**: Community is built over time, not at a single event
